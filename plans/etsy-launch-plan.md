# Etsy Launch Plan — the shop that earns and feeds the platform

Written 20 July 2026 from the multi agent research sweep (school planners,
build at home kits, launch mechanics, funnel case studies). Companion skill:
`.claude/skills/printables-engine/SKILL.md` makes the products this plan sells.

The one line strategy: every product is profitable at the point of sale
(roughly 73 to 84 percent gross margin), and every buyer is a warm, already
paying parent who can be moved, compliantly, onto the Guided Childhood list
and toward the starter pack. Etsy is the compounding list builder and review
engine. The founder 50 fills faster through the newsletter, LinkedIn and
schools; do not expect Etsy to fill it alone (per 1,000 buyers expect 100 to
250 email opt ins and 10 to 30 members over a year).

The timing fact that orders everything else: US back to school demand peaks
July to mid August and it is late July now. School planner listings go first.
UK wave follows 15 August to 10 September.

## Phase 0 — Revive the existing shop (this week, one sitting)

We are reusing Justin's dormant Etsy shop, not opening fresh. That may keep
shop age and any old reviews, both mild positives. Checklist:

1. Audit what is there: old listings, old reviews, shop name history,
   whether the $10k Offsite Ads threshold was ever crossed (check Finances,
   this decides whether opting out is still possible).
2. **Opt out of Offsite Ads immediately** (Settings, Offsite Ads). It takes
   15 percent per attributed order, pushes total take to roughly 40 percent
   on a £5 item, and becomes mandatory for life at 12 percent once the shop
   crosses $10k in a 365 day window. Only optable while under the line.
3. Rename and rebrand: Guided Childhood (or GuidedChildhoodShop if taken),
   banner and icon from the design system, DiGi golden star present.
4. Deactivate every old listing that does not fit the brand. Do not delete
   anything carrying reviews; edit and repoint instead where possible.
5. About section and shop announcement: Justin as the face, the expert
   credibility line, and the one explicitly permitted funnel line: join
   parents getting a free guide every month at Guided Childhood.
6. Policies: digital download policy set, no returns on digital, fast reply
   expectation. Fill EVERY profile field (completeness feeds ranking).
7. If VAT registered, give Etsy the VAT number so the 20 percent VAT on fees
   stops. If not, budget fees at the VAT inclusive rates.
8. Payment and bank checks so the first sale does not hit a verification wall.
9. Before 11 August: read Etsy's new Prohibited Items Policy (effective that
   date) and the June 2025 Creativity Standards. Everything we sell is our
   own work; AI assisted imagery must be disclosed per policy.

## Phase 1 — The 30 listing launch grid (weeks 1 to 3)

Price floor £4. Nothing below it except deliberate entry offers. A £3 item
nets £2.18 after fees (27 percent take); £12 nets £10.01 (17 percent).

| Lane | Listings | Price | Job |
|---|---|---|---|
| Routine and screen time charts (Oliver) | 8 | £4 to £5 | Review velocity engine. Morning, after school, bedtime by age band (5 to 7, 8 to 11, 12 to 14), ADHD variant, screen agreement |
| School planners | 6 | £8 to £12 | FIRST TO SHIP. 2 to 3 colourways as separate listings, dated 2026 to 27 plus undated, UK Sept to Aug variant, hyperlinked, GoodNotes plus PDF plus A4 and Letter |
| Parent guides (existing, repackaged) | 6 | £4 to £6 | The credibility layer nobody else has. Claims rule: healthier habits yes, reduces anxiety never |
| Build at home kits | 6 | £4 to £12 | Boredom jar, Zara escape kit 9 to 13, science cards with household materials list, Sofia calm down kit |
| Bundles | 4 | £15 to £25 | The revenue core. Family Screen Reset Kit, End of Term Survival Kit, priced 20 to 30 percent under the sum |

Build order: planners (season), then charts (review engine), then bundles as
their components exist. Each listing built by the printables-engine skill so
nothing ships without its funnel page and listing package.

Listing execution standard (every listing, no exceptions):
- Title around 70 characters, front loaded, format and age band named
- All 13 tags, phrased differently from the title
- Every attribute filled (blanks silently exclude us from filtered search)
- All 10 photo slots as a mini sales page: lifestyle mockup first (chart on
  a real fridge), flat lay, instant download overlay, sizing, how it works
- 5 to 15 second video: real hands using the product on a table
- 2000px minimum, house palette and Nunito, warm not corporate
- Strike through pricing: list high, run a permanent 25 to 40 percent sale

## Phase 2 — Wire the funnel (week 1, parallel)

The rules line is transaction location, not links. Never move a purchase off
Etsy, never say cheaper on our site, never market through Etsy Messages,
never auto add buyers to the list (GDPR plus Etsy policy).

1. Build /etsy-bonus on the platform: free bonus per product family, email
   gated, tracked separately so Etsy sourced signups are measurable.
2. Last page of every PDF: the named bonus for that product. Chart buyers
   get a free DiGi mini lesson (the strongest bridge, the child meets the
   platform). Guide buyers get the family agreement template.
3. Footer URL on fridge dwelling pages: the months long billboard.
4. Auto message on every order: download help plus one soft line pointing at
   the bonus page inside the file. Nothing else, ever.
5. Welcome sequence, 3 emails over 7 days: deliver bonus and introduce the
   brand, one practical win, starter pack offer. Then the weekly freebie
   cadence (the Big Life Journal pattern: 200k parent list built this way).
   Every CTA routes to /starter-pack per house rule 9.
6. Once the newsletter and socials push traffic TO listings, enrol Share and
   Save: 4 percent rebate on orders we referred, and external converting
   traffic is itself a ranking signal.

## Phase 3 — Launch and cold start (weeks 2 to 6)

1. Push the newsletter and socials at 3 hero listings during their new
   listing honeymoon (roughly the first days to two weeks of boost).
2. Review flywheel: 7 to 10 percent of buyers review at the top of the
   niche. A £12 product needs 15 to 25 visible reviews to look safe, which
   means 150 to 300 sales through the £4 to £5 entry listings first. That is
   the charts' job. Allowed tactics only: one polite post purchase message,
   a genuinely free bonus for all buyers, a warm ask on the PDF last page,
   reply to every review. Nothing tied to sentiment, never ask twice.
3. Reels cadence in the Natalia format: hands on table demo of a real
   product, one free resource drop with urgency (free for a limited time)
   every couple of weeks as the follower pump.
4. Cadence: 5 to 10 new listings per week for 8 to 10 weeks (documented
   seller at this cadence reached about 5,000 sales in year one). Watch
   Search Analytics weekly; double down on converting keywords.
5. Price rises after traction: the documented case lifted $8 to $12 after 50
   sales for 35 percent more revenue at only 15 percent conversion cost.

## Phase 4 — Seasonal calendar (repeats yearly)

- Mid June: dated planner listings live (re date the SAME listings, never
  relist; reviews compound on one listing ID)
- 1 July to 15 August: US back to school push
- 15 August to 10 September: UK push, A4 first
- October: Christmas line (the La Casita seasonal pattern), teacher gifts
  peak in November
- December: End of Term Survival Kit, new year planner refresh
- January: habit and routine reset angle
- May: re date planners for the next academic year
- Every school holiday: boredom buster and build at home kits resurface

## Metrics that decide things

- Visits to buyer: plan on 2 to 4 percent for the first 90 days (digital
  average 6 to 7, top shops 12 to 20). Below 1 percent after 100 visits on a
  listing means photos or price, fix before adding traffic.
- Buyer to email: plan on 10 to 15 percent via the PDF bonus.
- Email to member: 2 to 5 percent over 6 to 12 months is the warm list goal.
- North star: Etsy sourced members per month, visible via /etsy-bonus
  tracking.

## What we never do

No Offsite Ads. No Etsy Ads on sub £8 listings. No listings under £4 except
deliberate entry offers. No affiliate links in PDFs. No medical claims. No
PLR or marketplace template content (Creativity Standards ban it). No review
incentives. No moving transactions off Etsy. No deleting reviewed listings.
