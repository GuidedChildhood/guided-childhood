# Market evidence: what parents pay for (verified research, 5 July 2026)

Deep research run, 103 agents, adversarial 3 vote verification per claim.
Sources include SEC filings, Companies House, Fortune, NatWest first party
data, Internet Matters UK tracker. Full trail in the session transcript.

## The six categories, verdicts

1. Screen time conflict tools, standalone: NO surviving evidence of
   willingness to pay. Weakest category.
2. Chores and rewards: PROVEN UK engagement. NatWest Rooster Money
   354,238 active UK users, kids average £474.76 a year through the
   platform, per chore payments rising year on year, 27.4% of families
   run a recurring allowance. (Caveat: proves engagement and money
   routing, base mixes free and paid tiers.)
3. School admin: big UK market (ParentPay £124M revenue, ~50% of UK
   schools, ESS bought at £400M EV) but monetises B2B via schools.
   No evidence parents pay for it standalone.
4. Safety monitoring, two flavours: family LOCATION is the largest paid
   market (Life360: 2.7M paying circles, $96.3M quarterly subscription
   revenue, ARPPC $137.63, SEC filed). Standalone parental CONTROL
   software is the weakest UK conversion measured: 93% awareness of
   controls overall, third party safety software 37% aware and only 15%
   using (59% drop off), cost versus free platform controls named as the
   reason. Churn drivers documented: price versus performance anger,
   renewal and cancellation friction, vendors stranding paid features
   (Circle). Efficacy problem: CHI 2018 UCF study, teens of app
   monitoring parents reported MORE exposure to harm; direct parental
   involvement predicted less. Monitoring apps did not help.
5. Parenting advice, scripts, coaching: PROVEN at the HIGHEST price.
   Good Inside 100,000+ subscribers at $23.25 to $28 a month, $34M
   revenue 2025, ~50% YoY growth, profitable. The ceiling price of the
   whole market.
6. Kids digital literacy education, standalone: NO surviving evidence of
   standalone willingness to pay.

Bundles: no verified case of a digital parenting OS bundle converting as
a bundle. Winning pattern (Life360): nail one job, get paid, then expand.
Price anchors: ~$11.50/month safety location blended, $23 to 28/month
coaching ceiling, ~$100/year controls (contested and churny), £0
expectation for controls among many UK parents.

## Decisions this drives for Guided Childhood

- LEAD the pitch with coaching and scripts (the proven premium
  category): know exactly what to say at the hard moment. £12.99 is
  roughly half the proven ceiling.
- SECOND punch: Quests (the UK proven earn mechanic pointed at screens
  and the family agreement instead of only pocket money).
- School catcher and literacy curriculum are MOAT and retention, not
  the conversion headline. They are the delighted discovery after
  signup and the reason to stay.
- The no surveillance philosophy is an evidence backed attack line:
  controls convert worst, churn hardest, and the research says
  involvement beats monitoring. "We teach the free settings and give
  you the words" cites Internet Matters and UCF directly.
- Honour the churn lessons in product from day one: one tap cancel,
  honest renewal emails, never strand a paid feature.
- Homepage simplification (Bark style route first): hero carries the
  coaching promise plus age buttons, quests card second, school catcher
  demo third. Everything else lives inside.
