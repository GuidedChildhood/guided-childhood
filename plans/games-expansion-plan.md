# Games: our educational games by age, printable and phone, outdoor and real communication

**Note from Justin, 10 Jul 2026. No dashes in any copy.**

## The principle

No phone before the age is right. So for the youngest, the games are printable, offline, outdoor, and about real communication with real people. On phone educational games appear only where the age genuinely fits, and always as a small part of a mostly offline childhood. Every game is ours, built in house, gated by stage. Never an external app suggestion.

## Live today (on main, in code)

The Games tab shows only our games via `gamesForStage(stage)`, no external picks.
- Foundation, 4 to 7, pre reader, no reading needed: Animal Match, Count the Dots.
- Builder, 8 to 10: Quick Maths.
- Explorer, 11 to 13 and up: Times Table Sprint, What Is an Advert, Real or Fake.

## The gap

- Foundation is thin and still screen based. For this age the brand wants printable, outdoor, and talking, not a screen.
- Nothing yet for outdoor play or real communication at any age.

## To build

### Batch 1, printable, outdoor and real communication (offline first, all ages)
- Outdoor scavenger and nature bingo cards, printable, scaled by age.
- Family talk cards: real conversation prompts for the dinner table, by age.
- Outdoor challenge sheets that earn stars in the quest economy: twenty minutes outside, build a den, learn to skim a stone.
- Communication and kindness missions: call a grandparent, say a proper thank you, teach someone something.

### Batch 2, age appropriate on phone educational games (older ages only)
- More maths: number bonds, fractions, mental maths ladders.
- Reading and literacy games for confident readers, Builder and up.
- More media literacy judge games for Explorer and up: spot the ad, spot the deepfake, safe or not.

### Where they live
- Printables through the existing printable pack, one tap to print.
- On phone games in the quest games registry plus QuestGamePlayer, gated by `gamesForStage`.
- Outdoor and communication missions and every game earn stars, the same economy as the chores, so the reward is real world play, never more screen time.
