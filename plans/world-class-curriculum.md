# The world class curriculum — every area, every tradition

**Written 9 Jul 2026 · The complete digital literacy architecture · Blends every framework, pedagogy and learning leader · Builds on curriculum-matrix.md, lessons-depth-plan.md, lesson-structure-plan.md and digital-literacy-landscape.md · No dashes**

Justin's ask: make sure the curriculum covers every area of digital literacy and blends the best of every learning tradition, from Montessori to the best private and state practice to every leader in digital learning, into the best curriculum in the world. Here is the architecture, from a deep research synthesis. Sources are in the research handoff.

## 1. The verdict

Our current 9 strands are a genuinely best in class **online safety and wellbeing** curriculum. They are not yet a full **digital literacy** curriculum. The missing half is the empowering, creating, computing, data, money and citizenship side. To be the best in the world we add five strands, deepen three, weave in four cross cutting threads, and run the whole thing on a spiral, mastery gated, universally designed engine.

## 2. The complete domain map (what a digitally literate 16 year old needs)

Twenty one domains, merged from every major framework (UKCIS Education for a Connected World, UNESCO AI, DigComp 2.2, ISTE, AASL, CSTA, the News Literacy Project, Ofcom, NCSC CyberFirst, and the data, finance, civic and sustainability literatures): functional fluency; self image and identity; relationships, communication and collaboration; digital footprint and reputation; bullying, hate and conflict; information, media and news literacy; health, wellbeing and balance; privacy and personal data; cyber security; copyright, ownership and creation rights; AI literacy and safety; computational thinking and coding; data literacy; digital creativity and making; digital citizenship, ethics and children's rights; online civic and democratic participation; financial and consumer literacy online; accessibility, inclusion and the digital divide; environmental cost of technology; law, regulation and reporting; and problem solving, resilience and help seeking.

## 3. The recommended strand list: 9 becomes 14

**Keep, revised:**
1. Self image and identity
2. Online relationships and collaboration (broaden to include co creation)
3. Online reputation and digital footprint
4. Online bullying, hate and conflict
5. Information, media and news literacy (rename and deepen from managing online information: add deepfakes, algorithmic feeds, the information ecosystem, lateral reading)
6. Health, wellbeing and digital balance (deepen persuasive design and mental health)
7. Privacy and personal data (split security out, see 10)
8. Copyright, ownership and creation rights (add AI generated content)
9. AI literacy and safety (broaden from AI safety: add how it works and creating with AI, mapped to UNESCO's understand, apply, create)

**Add five:**
10. **Cyber security** (split from privacy, mapped to NCSC CyberFirst): passphrases, two factor, phishing and social engineering, malware and safe devices, basic threat modelling.
11. **Computational thinking and coding** (CSTA and Barefoot spine, an embedded Scratch style creation tool): decomposition, algorithms, debugging, creating.
12. **Data literacy** (the eight components): understanding, collecting, questioning, visualising, and the ethics of data and algorithms.
13. **Money, scams and the online economy** (financial and consumer literacy): in game spend, loot boxes, dark patterns, fraud, influencer marketing, subscriptions.
14. **Digital citizenship, rights and civic voice**: children's digital rights, ethics, and using digital tools to participate and be heard.

**Four cross cutting threads**, woven into every strand rather than made separate, to keep the matrix teachable at 14 not 21: accessibility and inclusion (a UDL default), environmental cost of tech (one lesson per age tier), functional fluency (bootstrapped in the early years), and law and reporting (embedded wherever risk appears).

This takes the matrix from about 45 topics (9 by 5) to about 70 (14 by 5).

## 4. The spine per age tier

- **4 to 5, Reception.** Character and story first, concrete, unplugged, playful. Kindness, my body and my name are private, ask a trusted grown up, real versus pretend.
- **5 to 7, KS1.** Play into first structure. Unplugged algorithms, public versus private, feelings online, who made this.
- **7 to 11, KS2.** Inquiry and mastery. Source checking, first coding, first data handling, footprint, passwords, in game spend, first phenomenon projects.
- **11 to 14, KS3.** Concept based depth and projects. Recommendation algorithms, misinformation and deepfakes, cyber security, data ethics, dark patterns, AI literacy, civic voice.
- **14 to 16, KS4.** Discussion, ethics and real projects. Contested dilemmas, AI system design and ethics, financial resilience, the information ecosystem, a capstone project.

## 5. The pedagogy blend, by age (what we steal from each tradition)

- **Young, 4 to 7:** Montessori (self directed, concrete materials, choice), Reggio (a made artefact the child documents), Sesame (character bonds), play, and Universal Design for Learning. Explicit small steps for anything safety critical (Rosenshine).
- **Middle, 7 to 14:** IB inquiry and concept based units (organise strands around big transferable concepts, not topics), Bloom mastery gating, Rosenshine modelling and review, spaced retrieval and the Bruner spiral, and one Finnish phenomenon per term that pulls several strands together (for example the attention economy).
- **Oldest, 14 to 16:** Harkness discussion (the elite private school round table) for contested and ethical questions, which is the perfect home for our non negotiable that DiGi never allows or denies but returns a calibrated pathway; project and problem based learning for the applied; mastery for the technical.
- **Every age:** a knowledge rich base (name the core knowledge each strand guarantees, so critical thinking has something to stand on), Singapore style worked examples then faded practice, and an explicit spiral so privacy at 5 (my body, my name) becomes privacy at 15 (data brokers, consent, tracking).

## 6. What the learning leaders teach us (steal the mechanism)

- **Khan and Khanmigo:** a tutor that guides rather than tells, and mastery gated progression. Give DiGi a Socratic mode.
- **Duolingo:** the daily micro habit and a gentle streak, tuned to wellbeing not guilt, paired with real mastery checks.
- **Prodigy, DreamBox, IXL, Century:** adaptive difficulty and a fine grained skills map with a placement diagnostic, plus teacher and parent analytics that show what a child now understands.
- **Scratch, code.org, Minecraft Education, Harvard Creative Computing:** an embedded make it mode so coding and creativity are hands on, tightly age sequenced.
- **Sesame:** validates the DiGi Squad model, deepen the parasocial bond for the young.
- **Oak National, BBC Bitesize, Seneca, Twinkl:** the complete lesson package (video plus slides plus task plus quiz plus teacher notes), rigorous key stage mapping, automatic spaced retrieval, and the printable home link.

## 7. The delivery model, per lesson package

Character led **video** (DiGi and the Squad model the idea and the virtue) to **interactive** (adaptive practice, a making tool, or a Harkness dilemma) to **task** (create an artefact or solve a real problem) to **mastery check** (gates progression, feeds analytics) to **spaced retrieval** (resurfaces prior strands) to **home link** (a printable or conversation prompt). Every lesson offers a UDL choice of input (watch, read, listen) and output (make, write, speak, build).

## 8. Priorities, ranked

1. **Add a Cyber security strand** (split from privacy, map to NCSC CyberFirst). The biggest safety gap, and national scaffolding already exists.
2. **Add Computational thinking and coding** with an embedded creation tool. This is the line between a safety programme and a literacy.
3. **Deepen managing online information into full Information, media and news literacy** (deepfakes, algorithms, lateral reading, the Finnish civic framing). The defining literacy of the decade.
4. **Broaden the AI strand to full AI literacy** using UNESCO's understand, apply, create, keeping the calibrated pathway ethos in the ethics dimension. Being current in 2026 beats a decade of incumbency.
5. **Add Money, scams and the online economy.** The fastest rising real world harm, and England is making digital financial education compulsory.
6. **Add Data literacy** (the eight components), taught concretely with concreteness fading for the young.
7. **Build mastery gating plus spaced retrieval into the engine.** The proven mechanism (Bloom two sigma, Rosenshine) that turns content into learning, and the thing the pathway needs anyway.
8. **Make every strand explicitly spiral and concept based, 4 to 16.** Revisit big transferable concepts at rising depth.
9. **Adopt Universal Design for Learning as the build standard**, designing in SEND, EAL and disadvantaged learners from the start.
10. **Add a digital creativity and making thread with real artefacts** (a Scratch anti bullying game, a stop motion, an audit). Creation is where motivation and mastery meet.
11. **Introduce the oldest tier to Harkness dilemmas**, scored on reasoning not conclusion, the natural home of never allow or deny.
12. **Weave accessibility, environmental cost and children's rights as cross cutting threads**, one dedicated lesson per age tier where a full strand is not warranted.

## 9. First actions

2. Write the concept spine per strand (the big idea that spirals 4 to 16).
3. Build the mastery gate and spaced retrieval into the lesson engine, since the pathway needs it too.
4. Prioritise the five new strands' content in the order above, starting with cyber security and computational thinking.
