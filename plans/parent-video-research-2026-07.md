# Parent video research: the topic map and design, July 2026
**13 Jul 2026 · The deep research output the script sessions read. 104 agents extracted Internet Matters early years, Internet Matters Digital Matters and Common Sense Digital Citizenship, adversarially verified 25 claims (23 confirmed, 2 refuted), and synthesised the design for our parent first then child video and lesson system, ages 0 to 16. This file feeds plans/video-script-sessions.md and plans/prompt-script-session.md.**

## The one paragraph verdict

All three benchmarks converge on the model we already believe in: teach the parent first, keep every unit short and action shaped, and make the child's version choice driven. Internet Matters proves the parent only 0 to 5 band (a free 4 episode video series where the parent acts between episodes and the child never watches). Digital Matters proves the child interactivity (a branching story where every chapter forces a binary decision with instant feedback, paired with a quiz, free with no family sign up). Common Sense proves the age banded strand structure across the whole school run (six strands, games matched to age, roughly 20 minutes per game plus lesson unit). The peer reviewed evidence backs parent first directionally: a 2025 meta analysis found parents' knowledge gains led to measurable reductions in both parents' and children's screen time, and an RCT showed a self directed app raised parent knowledge in one week and converted to reported action. Nobody closes the parent to child handoff loop. We do, with Star Lessons and quests. That handoff is our moat.

## Part A: what each resource does that is worth adopting

### Internet Matters, Online Safety Starts Early (0 to 5)
- **Parent first, child never watches.** A free 4 episode video series (made with EE) for parents, carers and educators of under 5s. The four themes: device setup and parental controls; screen time and how devices make the child feel (mindfulness); device safety features; the conversations to have with a toddler. (Episode titles vary slightly across sources; treat the four themes as reliable, the exact titles as approximate.)
- **Watch one, then act.** Their stated cadence, verbatim from the page: watch a single video, follow the related resources to take one action, then move to the next. This is the load bearing format rule for Track P.
- **The 0 to 5 hub organises parent advice into five areas:** age guides, digital wellbeing habit building, sharing milestone moments safely, managing screen time, choosing apps.

### Internet Matters, Digital Matters (KS2, pitched at 9 to 11)
- **Once Upon Online:** a multi part interactive story, each chapter forces a binary decision, a reaction screen after every choice, multiple endings, playable together or alone. Example: the Managing Online Information module follows Adil judging a claim about his favourite streamer before deciding to share. This binary decision with instant feedback mechanic is the exact pattern for our choice and scenario slides and the branching story variant of the player.
- **Test Your Knowledge:** an age appropriate quiz paired with every story. Story then quiz, always.
- **Free with no family sign up, no child data collected.** Only teachers register, to unlock lesson packs (plans, slides, companion guides, offline handouts, curriculum links across all four UK nations). Every lesson names explicit opportunities to involve parents and ships a parent companion guide. Our kid link model (no account, no login) already matches this.
- **Topic spine:** cyberbullying, misinformation, passwords and data security, privacy, screen time, wellbeing, friendship, online games, social media, AI tools, digital citizenship. New modules added regularly, aligned to Education for a Connected World.

### Common Sense, Digital Citizenship (K to 12, ages 5 to 18)
- **Six core strands carried through every age band:** media balance and wellbeing; privacy and security; digital footprint and identity; relationships and communication; cyberbullying, digital drama and hate speech; news and media literacy.
- **Digital Passport (grades 3 to 5, roughly ages 8 to 11):** six interactive games, each a self guided game plus a teacher led wraparound lesson, each unit about 20 minutes. The 20 minute game plus lesson unit is the size to copy. (The specific mini game names did not survive verification; do not cite them.)
- **Digital Compass (grades 6 to 8, roughly 11 to 14):** a choose your own path game, eight characters each facing a different dilemma, eight modules mapped to the strands, branching that rewards replay, printable extensions. NOTE: Common Sense retired Digital Compass on 30 June 2026, so it is a design precedent, not a live competitor. Our 11 to 16 branching story design is effectively net new in the market.

### The evidence on parent learning (Track P format rules)
- A 2025 systematic review and meta analysis (JMIR Pediatrics, Zgambo et al., 11 studies) found parents' improved knowledge, skills and attitudes led to measurable reductions in both parents' and children's screen time.
- A 2018 RCT (n 5,032, Bright et al.) showed a self directed safety app significantly raised parent knowledge within one week and increased reported safety actions, while controls did not change.
- **Honest caveats (do not overclaim in copy):** the RCT is a home injury app, not digital literacy; outcomes are self reported over one week; the meta analysis link is narrative with high heterogeneity. The strongest causal framing was refuted in verification. So: parent first is directionally evidence backed, not proven for durable child behaviour change. In copy, cite the sources by name and say what they found, never more.

## Part C: the recommended design (what the script sessions write to)

1. **One short DiGi Squad parent video per topic.** Target 2 to 4 minutes, watch one then act cadence, exactly one calibrated thing to try (never allow or deny), produced through the existing Higgsfield pipeline. This is Track P in the template.
2. **The video gates the handoff.** The parent's try this tonight beat ends by sending the matching Star Lesson or Family Quest to the child through the existing private link, no child account flow.
3. **The child session is the full flowing deck** (Track C in the template): DiGi intro, animation beats, slides woven between every pair of beats, and the choice slides carry the Once Upon Online pattern: a real decision, instant reaction, never a mark out of ten mid lesson. The last two choice slides are the quiz, and the quiz pays stars.
4. **Age banding:** 0 to 5 parent only; 5 to 7 co watch; 7 to 11 Star Lesson; 11 to 14 child leads; 14 to 16 near adult register. The 21 modules are the spine; the 0 to 5 band is additive.
5. **Free to families, sign up free, no child data.** Already our model. Each lesson keeps its printable pack and gains a parent companion note (the parent_note column already exists).

## Part D: shortest build path to v1 (reuse, do not build)

1. Pick one flagship 7 to 11 topic that already exists as a module: **Misinformation, deepfakes and AI content** (it mirrors Internet Matters' proven Adil story) and one 0 to 5 parent only topic: **screen time and how devices make a small child feel**.
2. Write both session files first (the script session lane), then cut the two parent videos in the watch one then act format when Higgsfield credits land.
3. Wire each parent video to the existing Star Lessons handoff. Nothing new to build: the send, the kid link, the stars and the parent push all ship today.
4. The child deck reuses the interactive player as is; the branching story variant of the player (chapter choices change the path) is the one genuinely new platform feature, and it can wait for v2 because choice slides with reaction feedback already carry the pattern.
5. Scripts live in the database (non negotiable 6). The platform lane lands them after JP red pens the files.

## Refuted in verification (never cite)

- The named Digital Passport mini games (Password Protect, Twalkers, Share Jumper, Search Shark): refuted 0 to 3. Cite the six game suite and the 20 minute unit, never the game names.
- The strong causal stat (Hedges g minus 0.47 screen time reduction as moderate proof parent teaching changes the child): refuted. Use the meta analysis' own narrative finding, worded as it words it.

## Open questions (instrument, do not guess)

1. Optimal parent video length per band: the evidence says short and act between, not a tested minute count. A/B 2 minute vs 4 minute DiGi videos.
2. The watch to send conversion (parent watches, then sends the Star Lesson) is unmeasured anywhere. Instrument it from day one; it is our key funnel step.
3. Where the parent only to child controlled line sits per topic (Internet Matters hands control to the child around 9). Decide per topic in the session files.
4. With Digital Compass retired (June 2026), our 11 to 16 branching story has no live competitor reference. Treat it as net new.
