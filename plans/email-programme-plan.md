# The email programme: from lifecycle chain to the most useful email a parent gets

**Date:** 2026-07-04. Justin's brief: more personable, more information, friendlier like Dr Becky's, images, tasks to do, sign up teasers, comprehensive guides and news, a whole shareable library.

## The Dr Becky mechanics we adopt
1. **Story first, always.** Every email opens with a real moment ("A mum told me this week that the 6pm handover had turned into a nightly war...") before any teaching. One idea per email, never three.
2. **Written as Justin, to one parent.** First person, warm, specific. Sign off with name and a P.S. line (the P.S. is the highest read line in any email: use it for the tease or the small ask).
3. **One small task per email.** Read tonight's script. Do the two minute phone audit. Tick one device setting. Each task is a deep link into the app that marks itself done and feeds the streak. The email becomes part of the daily loop, not noise beside it.
4. **Warm visuals.** Header illustration (DiGi or squad, one consistent Higgsfield set stored in /public/email), one hero image per email, stage coloured accents, the child's pathway progress bar in the digest. Never stock photos of sad children.

## The content engine
5. **Weekly: one thing worth knowing.** Short news email: an OS change, a ban milestone, a new app pattern. The research watch pipeline (device module) FEEDS this: approved detections become newsletter items, so the news writes itself from data we already maintain.
6. **Monthly: the comprehensive guide.** One deep, evidence based guide per month (the first smartphone, the gaming handover, the group chat years), matching the monthly lesson drop.
7. **The library.** Every guide and newsletter publishes to /library as a permanent page: SEO surface, shareable link, and the email archive parents can browse. Every email carries "Forward this to a parent who needs it" and the library link. Sharing is the referral engine before a referral programme exists.
8. **The tease, honestly.** Free tier emails name what paid adds where it is natural ("Founder families also get the agreement builder, 31 of 50 places left"), never more than one tease per email, always true.

## Segmentation (all data already exists)
By stage (content matches the child's age), by tier (free gets teases, paid gets depth), by engagement (lapsed parents get the warm catch up chain, never guilt), by feature usage (never set up devices → the device email).

## Build order
1. Voice and layout pass on the existing five lifecycle emails (story openings, P.S. lines, task links, image slots).
2. Feature education chain (one feature per email, days 3 to 21, already planned) written in the same voice.
3. /library route + publish the first monthly guide.
4. Weekly news email wired to research watch approvals (after the device module ships).
5. Higgsfield email illustration set, one batch with the moments tiles.
