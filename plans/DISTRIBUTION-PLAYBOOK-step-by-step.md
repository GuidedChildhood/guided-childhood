# The distribution playbook — the easy step by step guide to getting parents

**For:** Justin. **Written:** 2026-07-11. **Voice:** plain, no dashes.

This is the do this, then this guide for **getting people to the app**. Not the tech setup, that is a different job. This is marketing and distribution only: how a parent who has never heard of you ends up doing the three question stage check and paying.

The strategy behind it is already written in two plans. This is the follow along version you actually work from day to day:
- `plans/week-of-2026-07-11-starter-story-growth-plan.md` (the paid creator engine)
- `plans/guided-digital-pathway-growth-plan.md` (the organic flywheel)

Every step says **what to do**, **how**, and **done when**. Work top to bottom. Tick the box.

---

## The one number and the one hook

**The number:** 2,500 free signups a month. That is what £10k MRR needs at a 4 percent conversion (from `plans/mrr-10k-review.md`). Every action below exists to feed that number. When you are unsure what to do, ask "does this get more parents to the stage check," and if it does not, drop it.

**The hook, used everywhere, the same every time:** *What stage is your child? Three questions, no sign up.* This is your version of Early's push ups to turn off your alarm. It is showable in fifteen seconds, it is personal, and it routes to the one page that already works with no login. Every post, every video, every link ends there: **guidedchildhood.co.uk/starter-pack**.

Do not vary the hook while you are still learning what works. One hook, everywhere, until the numbers tell you to change it.

## What this costs, and the free start (read this first)

You can run the whole first month or two for **£0**. Almost every channel here is free, and the one that costs money is optional and capped. Do not spend anything until the free channels prove the hook converts.

**Free, and where you start:**
- Your own posting on LinkedIn. Free.
- Parent groups on Facebook, Mumsnet, Reddit. Free.
- The share card and referral loop. Free (one Claude build).
- The email drip that converts them. Free on the Resend free tier.
- The content engine writing your posts. Free, you already have the skill.
- Search pages later. Free to build.

**The only thing that costs money is the paid creators, and it is a ceiling not a bill:**
- The test is capped at **£750 to £1,000 total** across two or three creators.
- You can start much smaller, one micro creator at £150 to £250 is a real test.
- Cheaper still, many small creators will do it for a **free lifetime membership or a small gift** instead of cash. That is a near free way to test the creator format.

**The money rule:** do not pay a penny until your free posts and group seeds are sending parents to the stage check and some are signing up. Once the free traffic converts, paying a creator just pours more people into a funnel you already know works. You pay to scale something proven, never to find out if it works. So run Parts 2 and 3 first, watch the sheet, and only reach Part 4 once you have seen signups from free traffic.

## The channels, in the order you switch them on

You do not do all of these at once. You turn them on in this order, because the early ones are free and fast and the later ones compound slowly.

1. **Your own posting** (LinkedIn first). Free, starts today, you control it.
2. **Parent groups** (Facebook groups, Mumsnet, Reddit). Free, seeds real conversations.
3. **The share loop** (the stage card with a referral link). Turns every user into a distributor.
4. **Paid parent creators** (the Starter Story engine). The scale lever, costs money, needs the above working first.
5. **Search** (device and script landing pages). Slow to start, compounds for months.
6. **Schools** (assembly pack, pilots). Institutional trust, a later door.

Email is not a distribution channel, it is what converts the traffic you get. Keep the drip on (`plans/email-sequence-plan.md`), but it is not on this list because it does not bring new people, it keeps the ones who arrived.

## PART 1 — Get ready to measure (do this once, before anything)

You cannot run distribution blind. Two tiny things first.

- [ ] **1.1 The one sheet.** Make a Google Sheet with a row per day: date, free signups, quiz starts, and a notes column for "what I posted or which video went live." This is your whole analytics system. Early tracked spikes on a sheet, not a dashboard. Copy that.
      *Done when:* the sheet exists with today's row.

- [ ] **1.2 The share card.** Confirm the stage result gives a shareable card with a link back (from the flywheel plan). If it is not live, that is the one build this playbook needs, so ask a Claude session: *"Build the shareable stage result card with a referral link, per plans/guided-digital-pathway-growth-plan.md."*
      *Done when:* finishing the quiz gives you an image or link you would actually send a friend.

## PART 2 — Turn on your own posting (Week 1, free, starts now)

Your LinkedIn already works, the literacy post format lands. This is the fastest traffic you have.

- [ ] **2.1** Use the content engine skill to produce the week's posts: run `/content-engine` (or ask a Claude session to run it) against the cliff versus ramp message and the ban. It writes LinkedIn posts and a Substack issue in your voice.
      *Done when:* you have five posts ready for the week.

- [ ] **2.2** Post one a day, five days. Each ends with the same hook and link: what stage is your child, three questions, guidedchildhood.co.uk/starter-pack.
      *Done when:* five posts are up and logged in the sheet.

- [ ] **2.3** Reply to every comment for the first hour after posting. LinkedIn pushes posts that get early replies. This is fifteen minutes that doubles reach.
      *Done when:* you have replied to the early comments on each post.

## PART 3 — Seed the parent groups (Week 1, free, alongside Part 2)

Where worried parents already argue about phones. You show up as a helpful parent, not an advert.

- [ ] **3.1** Join five Facebook parenting groups, plus Mumsnet, plus one or two parenting subreddits.
- [ ] **3.2** For a few days, just be useful. Answer a phone or screen question with a real, warm reply. No link yet. You are building the right to post.
- [ ] **3.3** Then, where it genuinely fits a thread, drop the stage check as a helpful tool, not a pitch: *"There is a free three question thing that tells you what stage your child is at, it helped me think about it."* Use the content engine's other channels variants so the wording fits each place.
      *Done when:* you have seeded at least five real threads and logged any signup spike in the sheet.

> **The rule for groups:** be a parent who helps, roughly one in ten posts mentions the tool. The mission filter and the one in ten rule in the content engine skill are exactly this. Break it and you get banned from the group, which is worse than silence.

## PART 4 — Launch the paid creator test (Week 2, this is the scale lever)

This is the Starter Story engine, the whole reason for the strategy plan. Follow it exactly, small first.

- [ ] **4.1 Cut three demo clips.** Fifteen to twenty seconds each, the stage reveal on a real phone, camera on you or a parent, never on a child's face. One clip per hook variant so creators can pick.
      *Done when:* three clips exist you would be happy to see on someone's feed.

- [ ] **4.2 DM twenty UK parenting creators.** Micro accounts, 10k to 250k, whose content already includes real family life and the phone question. Use the win win win pitch from the strategy plan: they keep making their content, they get paid, their audience gets a genuinely useful tool.
      *Done when:* twenty DMs sent, logged with who and when.

- [ ] **4.3 Book two or three.** One video each. Pay against a **guaranteed view count**, roughly £2 to £3 per thousand views, with a hard cap so your total test spend is £750 to £1,000. You cannot lose more than the cap.
      *Done when:* two or three creators are booked inside the cap.

- [ ] **4.4 Send each a brief.** Include the pinned first comment written for them, in your voice, no dashes: *"It's Guided Childhood. Three questions, no sign up, tells you your child's stage. Link in my bio."* And the one hard rule: the camera stays on the parent, never the child's face.
      *Done when:* every booked creator has the brief and the pinned comment.

- [ ] **4.5 Watch the spike.** When each video goes live, watch the next 48 hours in your sheet. A video that spikes both views and signups earned its money. Keep those creators, cut the ones that did not move the line.
      *Done when:* every test video has a 48 hour result logged and a keep or cut decision made.

## PART 5 — Scale what worked (Week 3 onward)

- [ ] **5.1** Move the creators who spiked to a small monthly retainer, two to four videos a month, still on guaranteed views.
- [ ] **5.2** Take the single best video and test one thing at a time: the first frame, the caption, the exact three questions shown, the posting time. Change one, compare, keep the winner. This is how a lucky video becomes a repeatable one.
- [ ] **5.3** Keep your own posting and group seeding running underneath. Paid and organic feed each other: a creator video sends viewers who share their own stage card into the same groups you are already in.
      *Done when:* you have at least one creator on retainer and one winning variant proven.

## PART 6 — The compounding layers (start now, pay off later)

These are slow. Start them early so they are working by month six.

- [ ] **6.1 Search pages.** The device hub content already exists in the database. Ask a Claude session to turn it into public pages, one per device and age band ("set up an iPhone for an 11 year old"), and one public script page per problem. Forty plus pages of high intent search traffic (see gap 5 in the MRR review). This compounds while you sleep.
- [ ] **6.2 Schools.** The free assembly pack is the top of the school funnel. Offer it for an email, no commitment. One pilot school is 200 to 800 households reached with institutional trust. This is a later door, do not let it slow the parent engine.
      *Done when:* the search pages are being built and the assembly pack captures emails.

## The weekly rhythm you repeat (pin this to the wall)

Once you are running, marketing is one loop you do every week. Sam Parr's rule: one or two time bound tasks a day, written down, or nothing happens.

- **Monday:** run the content engine, get the week's five posts. Check last week's sheet, decide which creators to keep.
- **Tuesday to Friday:** post one a day, reply in the first hour, move the creator engine forward by one step (a DM, a brief, a spike logged).
- **Friday:** look at the sheet. Signups up or down, which channel moved it, what to do more of next week.

One number on the wall: are we tracking toward 2,500 signups a month. Everything else is detail.
