# Road to 16: the parent and child curriculum plan

Written 18 July 2026. Research plus plan only, nothing built yet.
This is the source of truth the parent and child lessons get written against.
Parent and child facing. Reuses the school curriculum tables. Does not
restructure the school side.

## Framework grounding (what a hostile expert will check us against)

- UKCIS Education for a Connected World (EFCW): 8 strands, progression in
  four age bands (EYFS to 7, 7 to 11, 11 to 14, 14 to 18) written as "I can"
  statements. Our five stages map cleanly: Foundation and Builder sit in the
  first two bands, Explorer in 11 to 14, Shaper straddles 11 to 14 and
  14 to 18, Independent in 14 to 18. Our lessons DB already uses the EFCW
  strand names (migration 018). https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/education-for-a-connected-world
- Common Sense Media digital citizenship scope and sequence: six topics, one
  lesson per topic per grade from K to 12, built with Harvard Project Zero.
  Confirms our choice of small yearly lessons per strand rather than one big
  course. https://www.commonsense.org/education/digital-citizenship
- Rosenshine, Principles of Instruction (American Educator, 2012): daily
  review, small steps, model, guided practice, check understanding, high
  success rate, weekly and monthly review. Our lesson format below is a
  direct house adaptation.
- UNESCO AI competency framework for students (2024): four aspects
  (human centred mindset, ethics of AI, AI techniques and applications,
  AI system design) at three levels (Understand, Apply, Create).
  https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/ai-competency-framework-students
- Day of AI (MIT RAISE): free AI literacy modules by age from 5 to 7 up to
  grades 9 to 12, covering artificial vs natural, how AI learns, deepfakes
  and algorithmic bias. Validates starting AI seeds at age 5, not age 11.
  https://www.dayofai.net/curriculum

Defensibility rule: every stage row below names its framework anchor. If a
claim has no anchor it does not go in a lesson.

## 1. THE LADDER (4 strands x 5 stages)

One line per cell: what the child can do or understands by the END of that
stage. AI and social media strands begin meaningfully at Explorer; before
that they get seeds. Existing seeded titles from migration 018 slot under
these rows; nothing needs renaming.

### Strand: Safe online
- Foundation (4 to 7): tells a trusted grown up every time something on a
  screen feels wrong, and knows the never share list: name, school, address,
  passwords. [EFCW EYFS to 7, Privacy and security; Online relationships]
- Builder (8 to 10): makes and keeps strong passwords, can tell a real
  friend online from a stranger playing one, and has a rehearsed telling
  plan with no fear of losing the device. [EFCW 7 to 11, Online
  relationships; CSM Privacy and Security grades 3 to 5]
- Explorer (11 to 13): runs their own permissions (location, camera, mic),
  can mute, leave and report in a group chat, and knows block and tell on
  every app they actually use. [EFCW 11 to 14, Privacy and security]
- Shaper (13 to 15): recognises sextortion, scams and pressure tactics
  aimed at teens, knows the first call plan, and can walk the safety
  settings of any new platform unaided. [EFCW 14 to 18, Privacy and
  security; CSM Privacy and Security grades 9 to 12]
- Independent (16+): secures an adult digital life (two factor, money,
  data) and knows where help lives beyond a parent. [EFCW 14 to 18]

### Strand: Healthy balance
- Foundation (4 to 7): ends screen time without a meltdown because the
  timer is the rule, and screens sleep outside the bedroom. [CSM Media
  Balance and Well Being K to 2; EFCW Health, wellbeing and lifestyle]
- Builder (8 to 10): keeps the bedroom rule as a family norm and can name
  what screens push out: sleep, play, people. [EFCW 7 to 11, Health,
  wellbeing and lifestyle]
- Explorer (11 to 13): notices what the feed does to their mood and can
  name the tricks built to hold them: autoplay, streaks, endless scroll.
  [EFCW 11 to 14, Health, wellbeing and lifestyle]
- Shaper (13 to 15): protects nine hours of sleep against the phone and can
  plan their own week's balance without being policed. [EFCW 14 to 18]
- Independent (16+): designs their own digital habits on purpose and can
  say what technology gives them and what it costs them. [CSM Media
  Balance and Well Being grades 9 to 12]

### Strand: AI and chatbots (seeds before Explorer)
- Foundation seed (4 to 7): knows a computer can pretend: a voice or picture
  on a screen may not be a real person, and asking a grown up is clever.
  [Day of AI ages 5 to 7, artificial vs natural]
- Builder seed (8 to 10): knows AI learns from examples and gets things
  wrong, so answers get checked with a person. [Day of AI grades 3 to 5;
  UNESCO Understand level]
- Explorer (11 to 13): knows a chatbot is a tool, not a friend; can feel
  the pull of a voice that always agrees and keep it in its place. [UNESCO
  human centred mindset, Understand]
- Shaper (13 to 15): uses AI to learn rather than to skip learning, and
  verifies before believing or sharing because seeing is no longer proof.
  [UNESCO Ethics of AI, Apply; Day of AI deepfakes module]
- Independent (16+): builds with AI: prompting, judging output, knowing
  failure modes, and what it means for work. [UNESCO Create level]

### Strand: Social media ready (seeds before Explorer)
- Foundation seed (4 to 7): knows the difference between a picture of a
  thing and the thing itself, real and made up. [EFCW EYFS to 7, Managing
  online information]
- Builder seed (8 to 10): spots when a screen is selling something and does
  the five second check before anything is shared. [EFCW 7 to 11, Online
  reputation; CSM News and Media Literacy grades 3 to 5]
- Explorer (11 to 13): can explain how the algorithm builds a feed from
  watch time, and that filters are not faces, BEFORE any account exists.
  [EFCW 11 to 14, Self image and identity; CSM grades 6 to 8]
- Shaper (13 to 15): completes the platform readiness set below: for each
  named platform, its risk shape, its settings and its judgement call; and
  reads their own footprint the way an employer will. [EFCW 14 to 18,
  Online reputation]
- Independent (16+): owns their online record and their information diet as
  a deliberate adult choice. [CSM News and Media Literacy grades 9 to 12;
  EFCW 14 to 18, Managing online information]

## 2. LESSON FORMAT (Rosenshine, house adaptation)

Every parent and child co watch lesson runs the same seven beats, ten to
twelve minutes. Rosenshine principle in brackets.

1. Recap of last step: one question on the previous lesson's catchphrase
   [daily review]
2. One small new idea, and only one [small steps]
3. DiGi models it in a short video or worked example [models]
4. Try together: parent and child do it for real, the parent note gives the
   parent the exact words [guided practice]
5. Quick check: 2 or 3 choice questions, written so most children get most
   right [check understanding, high success rate]
6. Catchphrase recap: say it together, note what comes next [review]
7. DiGi handoff for anything the family wants to go deeper on

### Mapping onto the EXISTING lessons table (013 + 017 + 018), no schema change

## 3. PLATFORM LESSONS AT SHAPER (13 to 15)

Seven named platform readiness lessons, one per platform a UK teen will
meet. Each covers: what it is, its real risk shape, a safety settings
walkthrough sourced from the platform's own family pages (URLs below, the
defensible source for every settings claim), and the one judgement call it
trains. Settings change; each lesson's why_it_matters carries the source
URL and a checked date so refresh is a content edit, not a rebuild.

1. WhatsApp: risk shape is group chats: pile ons, unknown adults added to
   groups, forwarding, and encryption meaning nobody at the platform sees
   it. Judgement call: when to leave a group that turns nasty, and saying
   so out loud. Settings: privacy, groups, block and report.
   Source: https://faq.whatsapp.com/323781503786199
2. YouTube: risk shape is the recommender: rabbit holes and the Shorts
   loop. Judgement call: noticing when you chose the video and when the
   feed chose it. Settings: supervised teen experience, bedtime and take a
   break reminders, the Shorts daily limit.
   Sources: https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/15252891 and
   https://www.youtube.com/myfamily/
3. TikTok: risk shape is the For You loop: watch time is the vote, viral
   challenges, beauty ideals. Judgement call: what did my watch time just
   teach the feed? Settings: teen defaults (private account, 60 minute
   default, notification curfews), Family Pairing, keyword filters.
   Sources: https://www.tiktok.com/safety/en/guardians-guide and
   https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/family-pairing
4. Instagram: risk shape is comparison and DMs: appearance pressure,
   strangers in requests, the highlight reel. Judgement call: who deserves
   a follow, and what does this feed do to how I feel? Settings: Teen
   Accounts defaults, Family Center supervision, time limits.
   Sources: https://familycenter.meta.com/ and
   https://familycenter.meta.com/supervision/
5. Snapchat: risk shape is the disappearing myth: screenshots keep
   everything, streak pressure, Snap Map location, a known sextortion
   route. Judgement call: nothing truly disappears, so what would this
   look like saved? Settings: contact me defaults, Family Center, location
   sharing off. Sources: https://parents.snapchat.com/ and
   https://parents.snapchat.com/family-center
6. Gaming chat and Discord: risk shape is open servers: adults and
   children mixed, voice chat, and the grooming path from public channel
   to private DM. Judgement call: the moment a chat moves private, that is
   the moment to tell someone. Settings: Family Center activity view, DM
   controls, sensitive content filter.
   Sources: https://discord.com/safety-family-center and
   https://support.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/14155043715735
7. AI chatbots: risk shape is the always agreeing companion: confident
   wrongness, over sharing personal life, romantic roleplay pull. Judgement
   call: this is a tool, not a friend, so what do I never tell it?
   Settings: ChatGPT parental controls (linked accounts, quiet hours,
   memory off, training opt out), and the Character.AI change of November
   2025 closing open ended chat to under 18s.
   Sources: https://openai.com/index/introducing-parental-controls/ and
   https://help.openai.com/en/articles/12315553-parental-controls-on-chatgpt-faq

Each is a normal lessons row (stage_id shaper, strand privacy or self
image as fits, category social) in the section 2 format, so the player,
completions and DiGi handoff all work with zero new code.

## 4. ASSESSMENT: the gauge

Becoming digitally literate is measured without it ever feeling like
school. Three layers, two of which already exist.

Layer 1, lesson quick checks (exists): the choice slides. Recorded today in
lesson_completions (hub lessons), parent_lesson_completions with
quiz_right_first_try (co watch set), and kid_lesson_missions score_correct
and score_total (lessons sent to the child's phone). No change.

Layer 2, real life signal (exists): moment_completions (daily moments
done), digi_memory and digi_safety_flags (what the family actually asked
and what came up), wellbeing_checkins (the monthly parent picture). No
change.

Layer 3, the stage review chat (new): when a stage's lessons are done,
DiGi invites the family to a short chat that references the child's REAL
moments and past issues ("you told me the group chat thing got better,
what changed?"). Behavioural and warm for Foundation through Explorer.
More formal at Shaper: a readiness review before each platform gets a yes,
walking the settings together. Never a test a child can fail: the verdict
is a calibrated pathway, always a next step, never allow or deny.

ONE new table:

    create table public.stage_reviews (
      id           uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
      child_id     uuid not null references public.children(id) on delete cascade,
      stage_id     integer not null,          -- 1..5
      review_type  text not null check (review_type in ('stage_chat','readiness','wellbeing')),
      summary      text,                      -- DiGi's plain words for the parent
      evidence     jsonb not null default '[]'::jsonb, -- lesson codes, moment ids, flags referenced
      verdict      text not null check (verdict in ('ready','nearly_there','keep_going')),
      next_step    text,                      -- the calibrated pathway line, never empty on nearly_there or keep_going
      created_at   timestamptz not null default now()
    );

RLS: parent reads and writes own children only, same pattern as
parent_lesson_completions. A 'ready' verdict at a stage plus all its
lessons complete awards the existing stage_passports row.

What "ready" means per stage (DiGi judges against this, in the review
prompt, stored in digi_prompts so it lives in the DB):
- Foundation: child tells, unprompted, when something feels wrong; screen
  endings without meltdown are normal week to week.
- Builder: bedroom rule holds; child can retell the telling plan in their
  own words.
- Explorer: child can explain the algorithm to the parent (the parent
  confirms this in the review); permissions checked together once.
- Shaper: platform readiness lessons done for every platform in use;
  settings walked through together; first call plan said back unprompted.
- Independent: the young person sets the terms of the review themselves.

Post 16: the gauge becomes a monthly DiGi wellbeing check in (a
stage_reviews row with review_type 'wellbeing'), judged by DiGi against the
wellbeing_checkins trend, not against a curriculum. The question shifts
from "do you know it" to "is your digital life serving you".
