# Characters that grow with the child: the maturation ladder

Ideas and plan only, 22 July 2026. Nothing generated. Inspired by The Color
Monster (a creature whose colour is its state) and the Jigsaw PSHE monster cast
Justin shared, translated into our own world so it is never a copy. No dashes in
any copy. DiGi stays the golden star, always (confirmed canon, never the robot
or the owl).

## What we have today, so this evolves rather than replaces

- **DiGi**, the golden star: the constant guide for parent and child across every
  stage. Stays exactly as is.
- **DiGi Junior**: the same star, small, the mid lesson pause beat. Stays.
- **UK animal stage guides** (Hog, Robin, Scout, Brock, Vix): already a per stage
  guide idea, but the age bands drifted from our canonical 4-7 / 8-10 / 11-13 /
  13-15 / 16+, and animals do not "grow up" cleanly across species.
- **Human topic heroes** (Oliver, Zara, Sofia): superheroes tied to a topic and a
  fixed age, not to a stage that matures.

The gap: nothing in the cast visibly grows up alongside the child, so a single
cute mascot that delights a five year old is exactly what a fifteen year old
rejects. That is the problem this fixes.

## The principle: a maturing near peer beats one fixed mascot

Grounded, not decoration:

- **Near peer modelling (Vygotsky's zone of proximal development, Bandura's
  social learning).** Children bond hardest with, and learn most from, a
  character a little older than themselves, the "more knowledgeable other" who
  models the next step. So the guide should always read as slightly ahead of the
  child, and therefore must age as the child ages.
- **The baby schema gradient (Lorenz's Kindchenschema, the lever Disney and Pixar
  use deliberately).** Rounder, bigger headed, bigger eyed reads younger and
  cuddlier; leaner, balanced proportions read older and cooler. Walking a single
  creature family down that gradient is exactly how you make a character "grow
  up" on the page.
- **The Color Monster device.** In the book, colour is the creature's inner
  state, sorted into jars. Our version: each stage has its own colour and its own
  creature, and that colour is a mastered digital state. This maps straight onto
  our five stage colour tokens and onto the passport stamps, one colour per
  stamp.
- **Collectibility.** A fixed cast of five, one per stage, is a set to complete.
  That is the hook for the physical layer (charms, stickers, cards, one per
  stage) in plans/physical-rewards-plan.md. A single mascot cannot be collected.

## Recommended concept: DiGi and the Sparks

DiGi stays the constant golden star, the north star for the whole journey. Around
DiGi live five **Sparks**, small friendly creatures made of DiGi's own light, one
born at each stage. They are not chosen by the child. The child meets the next
Spark as they reach that stage, so the cast literally grows up beside them, each
one a little older, a little cooler, a little more capable than the last.

The naming arc mirrors the maturation: a tiny spark grows all the way to a nova.
Candidate set (pick later): **Pip, Glim, Vela, Ember, Nova.**

### The maturation gradient (the rule that makes them "grow up")

Applied consistently across the five so they read as one family ageing, not five
unrelated mascots:

- Head to body ratio shrinks: nearly all head and eyes at Foundation, balanced by
  Independent.
- Palette deepens: soft and pastel early, richer and more confident later.
- Line and shape firm up: wobbly and huggable early, clean and self assured late.
- Props grow up: a comfort blanket at Foundation, a satchel of real tools at
  Independent.
- Energy shifts: giggly and wide eyed early, understated and quietly capable late,
  so a teen is met by a peer, never a baby.

### The five

| Stage | Ages | Colour token | Spark reads as | Its state / skill | Design cue |
|-------|------|--------------|----------------|-------------------|------------|
| 1 Foundation | 4 to 7 | stage-1 (butter) | a wide eyed little one | screens go off calmly, tell a grown up | almost all head, a comfort blanket, huggable |
| 2 Builder | 8 to 10 | stage-2 | a springy, tinkering kid | healthy habits, spot the trick | little arms, building bits, playful stance |
| 3 Explorer | 11 to 13 | stage-3 | a curious tween | the feed, the algorithm, comparison | leaner, a explorer's lamp, brave and gangly |
| 4 Shaper | 13 to 15 | stage-4 | a cool, understated teen | platforms, footprint, pressure | angular, minimal, less cute, quietly confident |
| 5 Independent | 16+ | stage-5 | a near adult mentor | an adult digital life, building with AI | sleek and simple, a tool satchel, older sibling energy |

Each Spark carries the stage's real skill, so the character IS the curriculum,
not decoration. Colour equals stage equals a mastered state, exactly the Color
Monster idea, and exactly the passport stamp colours.

## How they show up across the product

- **Lesson intros**: the stage's Spark opens that stage's lessons (the
  CharacterIntro pattern already exists), so the guide always matches the child's
  age. DiGi Junior still runs the pause beat.
- **The road to 16**: each stage node on the road can carry its Spark, so the
  child sees the next, slightly older Spark waiting up the path, a visible pull
  forward.
- **The passport**: each stamp page shows its Spark in its colour. Filling the
  stamp settles that Spark, the Color Monster "sorted into its jar" moment.
- **The physical layer**: the five Sparks are the natural set for the Croc charms,
  the stickers, and the trading cards. Collect all five on the way to 16.
- **DiGi throughout**: the parent's guide and the child's north star, unchanged,
  tying the whole set together.

## What happens to the current cast

- **DiGi and DiGi Junior**: unchanged, the through line.
- **UK animals**: retire as the stage guides in favour of the Sparks (cleaner
  maturation, native to DiGi's world), or keep them as "wild friends" cameos
  inside lessons. Recommend the Sparks lead.
- **Oliver, Zara, Sofia**: keep as the human friends who appear in specific topic
  lessons (screen habits, spotting fakes, privacy). They complement the Sparks
  rather than compete: Sparks are the stage companions, the humans are topic
  guests.

## Alternatives, if the full cast is too much

- **DiGi ages.** One identity, five forms: the star starts round and simple and
  gains facets and cool as it climbs. Simplest, fewest assets, but not
  collectible and loses the richness of a cast.
- **Evolve the animals.** Keep the UK animals but re map them to the canonical
  bands and draw them down the same maturation gradient. Warmer and British, but
  mixed species reads as different characters, not one growing up.
