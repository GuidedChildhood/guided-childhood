# The marketing message — the core promise

**Written 9 Jul 2026 · The one thing we say · No dashes, Justin's voice**

The promise, in one line: **ten minutes a day turns the cliff edge at 16 into a gentle ramp.**

## The core message (headline plus sub)

**Ten minutes a day. A childhood ready for the phone, not broken by it.**

Follow the daily path for a few minutes a day and you protect, teach and build the habits that get your child to 16 ready, not thrown off a cliff. The settings relax as they earn it, you are reminded of the week's tasks, the games send them outside and back to real people, and the lessons grow their judgement so that when social media is finally allowed, they can handle it.

## The shift we sell (the picture)

Most families hit **16 as a cliff edge**: nothing, nothing, nothing, then suddenly everything, all at once, unprepared. Guided Childhood turns that cliff into a **ramp**. A little more freedom at each stage, earned, guided, with the settings easing as the judgement grows, so full independence at 16 is a step, not a fall.

## What the ten minutes actually does (the proof, in plain words)

- **Protects.** Device settings set once across every screen, then relaxed on purpose as your child grows, never a free for all and never a lockdown.
- **Reminds.** You get a nudge for the week's real jobs, the PE kit, the routine, the moment worth handling tonight, so nothing important slips.
- **Sends them out.** The games and quests pay the most for outside play, making, and real conversation, so screen time earned is screen time that already did its job.
- **Teaches.** Short lessons grow real digital judgement, spotting the advert, the fake, the pull of the feed, so they are ready for the world they are walking into.
- **Guides you.** DiGi is there for the eleven at night question, with a real answer for your child and your stage, never a lecture.

## The one sentence for the top of the site

**The calm, guided path from your child's first screen to a sixteen year old who does not need you to watch over their shoulder.**

## Rules for using this

- Lead with the ramp versus the cliff. It is the idea no competitor owns.
- Always name the ten minutes. Small enough to keep is the whole reason it works.
- Never fear first. We sell readiness and calm, not panic.
- No dashes, ever. Justin's voice, warm, plain, direct.
