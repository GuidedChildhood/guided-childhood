# Physical rewards: the collectible layer that mirrors the online journey

Ideas and plan only, 21 July 2026. Nothing generated yet. The principle: every
physical thing maps one to one onto the digital road to 16, so a child collects
in the real world exactly what they fill on screen. Collectible pulls the child,
tangible reassures the parent, personalised makes it feel theirs. No dashes in
any of the suggested copy. Only ever a first name and an age band on anything,
matching the privacy policy's minimal data stance.

## The anchor: the physical passport booklet

The hero object. A real little passport that mirrors the digital one exactly.

- The same burgundy and gold cover as the on screen passport, with the child's
  first name foiled on the front, "the journey to 16" beneath.
- One page per stage, Foundation to Independent, in that stage's colour.
- The child collects a stamp or sticker on each page as they fill that stamp
  online. The physical book fills as the digital circle fills.
- Two tiers:
  - Home print PDF, free, in the starter pack: print, fold, staple.
  - Professionally printed saddle stitched booklet, a paid add on or a founder
    gift, posted to them.

## What fills it: stamps and stickers

- A foil sticker sheet, one collectible stamp sticker per stage, unlocked to
  print the moment the online stamp fills.
- A premium kit could carry a real self inking rubber stamp per stage.
- Small milestone stickers for the sub steps: first lesson done, first script
  used, bedroom rule kept a week.
- Three sticker themes to collect across the journey: the passport stamps (one
  per stage), stars (the reward currency made tangible), and DiGi in different
  moods and poses. Sold or unlocked as sheets, they decorate the passport, a
  water bottle, a laptop, or the Croc charms below.

## Wearables

- **DiGi wristband.** A silicone or woven band with DiGi and a catchphrase, in
  the stage colour. One per stage, so the wrist itself shows how far they are.
  Collect all five by 16.
- **Member card.** A personalised DiGi card: first name, "member since", their
  current stage. The GoHenry style keepsake that makes membership feel real. The
  single strongest brand and retention object on this list.
- **Enamel pins or cloth patches.** One per stamp, for a school bag, a lanyard,
  or a patch blanket that fills over the years.
- Lanyard and card holder to carry the member card and pins.

## Collectibles, the pull for the child

- **Digi squad trading cards.** Teo, Olga, Alma, DiGi Junior and the UK animals
  as a collectible set, one earned per lesson block or stage, each card's
  "power" being that lesson's idea. Kids collect, swap, complete the set.
- **Croc charms, the clip in shoe charms.** DiGi and the stage stamps as rubber
  charms that clip into Crocs: one per stage, plus the digi squad characters and
  a star. A highly visible playground collectible where the child wears their
  progress on their feet and completes the set on the way to 16. Cheap to make,
  strong pull, and it puts the brand in front of every other child at school.
- Stage sticker packs.

## Home and room, parent visible every day

- **The road to 16 wall poster and growth chart.** The same five stage road as
  a tall poster the child adds stickers to as they progress, doubling as a
  height chart. Keeps the journey on the wall, not only in the phone.
- **Bedroom door hanger.** A DiGi "screens sleep outside this door" hanger, tied
  to the bedroom rule habit.
- Fridge chart for the daily habit and stars.

## Milestones and the finish

- **Stage certificates.** A print ready certificate per stage, worded age
  honestly to match the digital celebration: "The Foundation habits are in place
  for their age." Never a blanket safety claim.
- **The 16 ready keepsake.** At the end of the whole journey, a proper rite of
  passage object: a medal, a hardback passport, or a framed certificate. The
  thing a parent keeps.

## Star economy tie in

- Physical star tokens or coins for a reward jar, matching the digital stars, for
  families who want a tangible cash in alongside the screen time timer.

## Print and delivery model (send to proper printers, the starter pack)

- Every reward is a print ready PDF: A size, 3mm bleed, CMYK, 300dpi, generated
  from the same house art and stored alongside the existing printables registry.
- Two routes to a physical object:
  1. Print at home from the app, free or low cost.
  2. Order the kit: a professionally printed, shipped welcome pack via a UK print
     on demand and fulfilment partner, so there is no stock or warehousing risk
     at launch.
- **The starter pack welcome kit** (paid add on or founder gift): the printed
  passport booklet, the stage one sticker sheet, a DiGi wristband and the member
  card, posted when they join. The physical unboxing that mirrors the digital
  onboarding.
- **Personalisation** pulls the child's first name and age band straight from the
  account, which is all the app holds and all that ever goes on an object.
- **Digital to physical sync.** Filling a stamp online instantly unlocks the
  matching printable (certificate plus sticker). The premium tier can trigger a
  fulfilment order to post the next patch or sticker. Minimum viable version is
  simply unlocking the PDF.
- **Data care.** Only first name and age band on anything. A shipping address is
  collected only if a family orders a physical kit, and it lives with the
  fulfilment partner, not in the general profile.

## Phased rollout

1. **Launch now, zero fulfilment.** Home print PDFs only, produced by the
   printables engine we already have: the passport booklet PDF, plus stage
   sticker and certificate PDFs that unlock as the online progress fills. Proves
   the collectible loop with no logistics.
2. **After the first testers.** The printed welcome kit via a print on demand
   partner: passport, wristband, member card. Founder gift or paid add on.
3. **Later.** The collectibles (trading cards, pins, patches) and the 16 ready
   keepsake.

## Build notes for when we start

- Reuses the `printables-engine` skill for the print ready PDFs and the digi
  squad art for characters, so the house look carries straight through.
- The digital to physical unlock rides the same per stage progress blend the
  passport, road and DiGi already read, so a filled stamp unlocking a printable
  is a small addition, not a new system.
- Phase 1 is genuinely near term because the printables plumbing exists; phases
  2 and 3 need a fulfilment partner decision first.
