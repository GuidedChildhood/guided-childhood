# Script session: Real, pretend, or made by a computer (5 to 7)

**Band:** 5 to 7 (parent first, then co watch together)
**Module:** Real, pretend, or made by a computer (`ks1-03-real-pretend-computer`)
**Cast:** Sofia + DiGi Junior, DiGi opens and closes
**Single action outcome:** When a picture surprises me, I ask the magic question, is this real? and I check with my grown up.

---

## Track P — the parent session (60 to 90 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 12s)**
Your six year old shows you a video of a cat doing something impossible and asks, is that real? And you realise you are not completely sure yourself. That question is about to become the most important one in their childhood.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 25s)**
Here is the pathway view. Children this age already understand pretend beautifully. Cartoons are pretend, dressing up is pretend, and pretend is wonderful. What is new is a third box: pictures and videos a computer made of things that never happened, and they can look exactly like photos. You do not need to teach a five year old to spot a fake. Nobody can spot every fake, including you and me. You need to teach one habit: when a picture surprises you, ask is this real? and check with a grown up. Asking is the skill. The answer can come from you.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
Tonight, play the magic question game with anything on a screen. A cartoon: is this real? No, pretend, and pretend is brilliant. A photo of Grandad: is this real? Yes! Something amazing and odd: is this real? Hmm, let's wonder together. Make asking fun, never scary. Then watch the kids version together, Sofia teaches it and they earn stars.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Seen something and not sure yourself if it is real? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Three kinds of pictures*
Heading: Real, pretend, or made by a computer.
Body: Some pictures are real, like a photo of your class. Some are pretend, like cartoons and story books, and pretend is brilliant, we LOVE pretend. And some pictures are made by a computer. A computer can draw a picture of something that never happened, and it can look just like a photo. That is not naughty and it is not always bad. It just means clever eyes ask a question before they believe.

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The amazing cat*
A video pops up: a cat riding a bicycle down the street, wearing sunglasses. It looks SO real. Everyone is laughing. Ask it out loud right now, you and your grown up together: is this real? What do you both think? (There is no way to be sure just by looking. That is exactly why we ask!)

**SLIDE (keywords)** — *Magic question words*
real: it truly happened · pretend: made up for fun, like cartoons, and lovely · made by a computer: a picture of something that never happened · check: asking your grown up to wonder about it with you

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *The dinosaur at the shops*
A picture shows a dinosaur walking past your local shops. What does a clever guardian think?
· Dinosaurs are back! Amazing! (tap to reveal: it does look amazing! But surprising pictures are exactly the ones to ask about.)
· Ask the magic question, is this real? then check with my grown up (tap to reveal: yes! Ask, then check. Your grown up can help you wonder. ⭐)
· Pictures are always real if they look real (tap to reveal: here is the secret: a computer can make a picture that LOOKS real. Looking is not enough. Asking is the trick.)

**SLIDE (interactive: verdict-sort)** — *The three baskets*
Five pictures float in one at a time. Sort each one into a basket: REAL, PRETEND, or ASK A GROWN UP. Do it together, no rushing.
1. A photo of your school hall on sports day (real)
2. A cartoon rabbit having a tea party (pretend, and lovely!)
3. A photo looking picture of a duck riding a skateboard (ask a grown up)
4. A drawing in your favourite story book (pretend)
5. An amazing photo of a shark in a swimming pool (ask a grown up)
Reaction lines: right sorts get Sofia's "clever eyes!", the ask sorts get "perfect. When you are not sure, ASK is always a winning answer. Nobody can tell just by looking, not even grown ups."

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The picture of you*
One more wonder, guardians, a gentle one. Computers can even make pictures of people. If you ever see a picture of someone you know doing something odd, and it seems not quite right, that is a perfect time for the magic question and a grown up check. You will not be in trouble. Wondering out loud is exactly what clever guardians do.

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
A video looks completely real, but it shows something very surprising. Can you tell if it is real just by looking really hard?
· Yes, if I squint my eyes (reaction: even the squintiest eyes cannot always tell. Computers are that good at drawing!)
· No, so I ask the magic question and check with my grown up (reaction: magic! Ask, then check. That is the whole trick. ⭐)
· Only teachers can tell (reaction: not even teachers can always tell by looking. Everyone has to check, and that is okay.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
Cartoons and story books are pretend. Is pretend bad?
· Yes, pretend is a trick (reaction: pretend is not a trick, it is play! The clever part is just KNOWING it is pretend.)
· No, pretend is brilliant fun, I just like knowing which is which (reaction: exactly right, guardian. Real, pretend, or made by a computer, you know the game now. Stars incoming! ⭐)
· Pretend does not exist (reaction: tell that to the cartoon rabbit at the tea party!)
