# Script session: Screen routines that work (5 to 7)

**Band:** 5 to 7 (parent first, then co watch together)
**Module:** Screen routines that work (`ks2-04-screen-routines`)
**Cast:** Sofia + DiGi Junior, DiGi opens and closes
**Single action outcome:** Tonight I help put the screens to bed before my own bedtime, because screens go to bed first.

---

## Track P — the parent session (60 to 90 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 12s)**
It is bedtime, and the tablet handover turns into a negotiation, then a protest, then tears. Same time tomorrow. The nightly screen battle is one of the most common flashpoints in every house with a five year old in it.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 25s)**
Here is the pathway view. The battle happens because the ending is a surprise and the child is on the losing side of it. A routine fixes both. Not a rule you enforce, a rhythm the whole house follows: screens go to bed first, every night, before the child does. When the ending is the same every night, there is nothing to negotiate, and when the child is the one who puts the screen to bed, they switch sides. They stop being the person screens are taken from and become the person in charge of the goodnight. Small children love a job, and this one is theirs.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
Pick the screen's bedtime, some time before your child's own, and give your child the job. Borrow these exact words: screens go to bed first in our house. Can you tuck the tablet in? A drawer, a basket, a spot on the shelf, anywhere that counts as its bed. Same place, same time, every night. Then watch the kids version together, Sofia teaches it and they earn stars.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Bedtime still bumpy after a week? Tell me how it went and we will tweak it together.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Screens need bedtimes too*
Heading: Screens go to bed first.
Body: Your body has a bedtime because sleep makes you grow, dream and feel good tomorrow. Screens are bright and busy and exciting, and busy exciting things right before bed make it hard for your brain to get sleepy. So in guardian houses there is one gentle rule: the screens go to bed BEFORE you do. Every night. Same time, same spot. And the best bit? YOU are the one who tucks them in.

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The screen's bed*
Time to choose, you and your grown up together, right now: where will your screen sleep? A drawer? A basket? A special spot on a shelf? Talk about it and pick the spot. That spot is now officially the screen's bed. You can even give it a goodnight thing to say. Night night, tablet. See you tomorrow.

**SLIDE (keywords)** — *Sleepy words*
routine: doing things the same way each time, so nobody has to argue · wind down: the slow, calm, cosy time before sleep · screen's bed: the spot where the screen sleeps every night · tuck in: your special job, putting the screen to bed

### BEAT 2 — CONCEPT (identity beat, 10s, silent render + cloned voice)
```
CHARACTER: Sofia
SETTING:   same classroom, the board now shows a simple picture strip: a
           tablet in a bed, then a book, then a sleeping child
BOARD:     SCREEN, THEN STORY, THEN SLEEP
MOOD:      calm, cosy, the goodnight secret

SHOT 1 (0 to 10s)
  ACTION:  Sofia walks her fingers along the picture strip, screen to book
           to sleeping child
  VOICE:   "Here is the whole routine in one breath. Screen goes to bed,
            then something calm, like a story or a cuddle, then you.
            Screen, then story, then sleep. That is it!"
```

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *One more episode?*
It is the screen's bedtime, but you REALLY want one more episode. What does a guardian do?
· Beg and beg until someone gets cross (tap to reveal: begging makes bedtime feel yucky for everyone, and the screen still goes to bed.)
· Tuck the screen in, because that is my job, and pick a story instead (tap to reveal: yes! You are the boss of the goodnight. The episode will still be there tomorrow, promise. ⭐)
· Hide the tablet under my pillow (tap to reveal: screens are terrible sleepers under pillows. They buzz and glow and wake your brain up. Real bed, real sleep, for both of you.)

**SLIDE (interactive: star-breath)** — *The wind down star*
This is the calm part of the routine, and you can use it every single night. DiGi Junior's star grows and shrinks. Breathe with it, you and your grown up together. In as it grows, one two three. Out as it shrinks, one two three. Three slow times. This is what wind down feels like: your body getting soft and ready for sleep. Try it tonight, right after the screen is tucked in.

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The first night*
Picture tonight. The screen's bedtime arrives. You carry it to its spot, tuck it in and say night night. Then something calm with your grown up: a story, a cuddle, a star breath, or all three. It might feel strange the first night. New routines always do. By night three or four, it will just be how bedtime goes in your house, and YOU made that happen.

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
Who goes to bed first in a guardian house?
· Me first, screens stay up late (reaction: other way round! Screens are the first ones tucked in.)
· The screens go to bed first, and tucking them in is my job (reaction: exactly right, guardian of the goodnight! ⭐)
· Nobody, bedtime is different every night (reaction: same time, same spot, every night. Routines only work when they repeat!)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
What comes between the screen's bedtime and YOUR bedtime?
· More screens, just quieter ones (reaction: sneaky! But no. Once screens are in bed, they stay in bed.)
· Nothing, straight to sleep, lights off, zoom (reaction: nearly! Your brain likes a soft landing first.)
· Something calm, like a story, a cuddle or a star breath (reaction: screen, then story, then sleep. You know the whole routine now. Stars incoming! ⭐)
