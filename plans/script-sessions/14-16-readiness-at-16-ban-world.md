# Script session: Readiness at 16, the ban world (14 to 16)

**Band:** 14 to 16 (parent briefing, near adult child version)
**Module:** Readiness at 16: the ban world (module 19, KS4, the crown module)
**Cast:** Vix the fox leads (street smart register, still 8365a8ff, first motion beats are a build task), DiGi opens and closes, DiGi pause
**Single action outcome:** I can plan how I will handle full access when it arrives, because I arrive with skills, not just a birthday.
**Ban world context:** the under 16 social media access ban is confirmed for Spring 2027. At 16 it lifts and the accounts open. This module is the handover: the licence moment, not the floodgate moment.

---

## Track P — the parent briefing (about 70 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 10s)**
Sixteen. The ban lifts, the accounts open, and years of arguments about social media end overnight because suddenly it is all legal and all theirs. Every parent of a 15 year old is quietly counting down to the same morning.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 25s)**
Here is the thing the ban never claimed to do: build judgement. It removed the apps for a few years, and that is all it did. What actually protects your teenager on day one is everything the pathway has been building since they were small: the three checks before believing anything, the future you test before posting anything, steering the feed instead of being steered, and knowing when to walk away. If they arrive at 16 with those skills, the ban lifting is a licence being issued to a trained driver. If they arrive with nothing but the birthday, it is car keys handed over at midnight. The difference is not the date. It is the preparation, and the preparation is exactly what you have been doing.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 25s)**
Ask them one question, with genuine curiosity: when your accounts open, what will you do differently from everyone else in your year? Let them talk. You are not approving or vetoing, you are helping them notice they have a plan forming. Then send the session. At this age it speaks to them like the adult they nearly are, and Vix hands over the licence with the respect an earned thing deserves.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Questions about the ban lifting, or the first week after it? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (concept)** — *What the ban did and did not do*
Heading: The ban removed the apps. It never built judgement.
Body: The Spring 2027 ban was a delay, not a defence. When it lifts at 16, the feeds, the DMs, the group dynamics and the pressure all arrive exactly as they always were, and the only variable is the person holding the phone. Some people meet that moment with nothing but excitement and an empty toolbox. You are not one of them, because everything the pathway taught was aimed at this exact morning. The ban world was never the point. The readiness was.

**SLIDE (concept)** — *The readiness checklist*
Heading: You already own the four tools this moment needs.
Body: The three checks: who made this and how do they know, what do other places say, how is it trying to make me feel. They work on posts, ads, AI content and arguments alike. The future you test: what you post can stay and can be copied, so future you gets a vote before it goes up. Feed steering: the algorithm follows your attention anywhere you point it, so you tune it or it tunes you. And boundaries: knowing the tells of a scam, the price of a workaround, the moment a group chat turns, and how to walk away from all of it. That is the checklist. Notice it is skills, not rules. Rules expire at 16. Skills do not.

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *Day one*
The morning the accounts open. The feed arrives hungry and completely blank: it knows nothing about you yet, and everything you pause on in the first week becomes its opening theory of who you are. The DMs open too, and so do the group invites, the recommendations, the offers. Question underneath: the feed is about to be trained by somebody. Who would you rather it was: a version of you chosen on purpose, or whoever you happen to be at your most tired and curious?

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *The first week*
Accounts open Monday. What is the strongest opening move?
· Follow everything, let the feed work out who you are (tap to reveal: the feed's first theory of you is built from your first pauses, including the doomscroll ones. Train it by accident and you will live inside the accident for months.)
· Choose what you follow on purpose, and let the feed learn from a version of you that you picked (tap to reveal: that is the fox move. The first week is the cheapest feed tuning you will ever do. Spend it deliberately. ⭐)
· Post everything from the last three years to catch up (tap to reveal: run the future you test on that plan. The catching up feeling is real, but the archive dump is exactly the post pile future you would want a vote on.)

**SLIDE (interactive: verdict-sort)** — *The licence test*
Five day one moments slide in, one at a time, and you sort each onto the tool that handles it: THREE CHECKS, FUTURE YOU, TUNE THE FEED, or WALK AWAY. A shocking story everyone is sharing (three checks). A photo from a party that would be very funny for about a week (future you). A feed that has turned into a wall of rage bait by Thursday (tune the feed). A DM offer of a verified badge for a small fee (walk away, and it is a scam on every tell). A group chat pile on gathering speed (walk away, and the exit is always allowed). Reaction line: five situations, zero new tools needed. You have had the licence for years. Sixteen is just the date it activates.

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (discussion)** — *Talk it out*
Some adults say sixteen is still too young, and some say the ban should never have existed at all. What do you think the ban actually changed? Talk about it. (Steer: both camps argue about the date, and the date was never the interesting part. Access without skills is risky at any age, and skills make access workable at sixteen. The honest answer is that readiness was always the variable that mattered, and readiness is buildable. That is the whole pathway in one argument.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
The ban ends at 16, so 16 is when you are ready. What is true?
· True, the law picked the age because that is when readiness happens (reaction: this is the misconception the whole module exists to retire. A date on a calendar builds nothing. People arrive at 16 ready or not ready based on what they practised, not on the birthday itself.)
· Readiness is the skills, and the ban lifting is just the date your licence activates (reaction: exactly. Same morning, very different arrivals. You are walking in trained. ⭐)
· Nobody is ever really ready, so the safest move is staying off everything forever (reaction: nobody here deals in bans, including self imposed ones. The pathway position is calibrated access with real skills, and you have them.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
What makes full access at 16 an earned licence rather than a floodgate?
· Nothing, everyone gets the same access on the same day (reaction: the access is the same. The arrival is not. One person meets the feed with a toolbox, another meets it empty handed, and the feed treats them very differently within a week.)
· Turning up with the checks, the future you test, feed steering and the walk away move, and using them from day one (reaction: that is the licence. Access plus skills plus intent. The floodgate only exists for people who never built the gate. ⭐)
· Having read the platform terms and conditions (reaction: noble, nearly nobody does, and it is not the protection. The skills are.)
