# Script session: Kindness on screens, real and not real (5 to 7)

**Band:** 5 to 7 (parent first, then co watch together)
**Module:** Screens and kindness, real and not real (`eyfs-01-screens-kindness`)
**Cast:** Sofia + DiGi Junior, DiGi opens and closes
**Single action outcome:** If something on a screen feels wrong or someone is unkind, I tell my grown up. Telling is never naughty.

---

## Track P — the parent session (60 to 90 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 12s)**
Your six year old is watching something, giggling away, and then goes quiet. You ask what happened and get a shrug. Or they show you a comment under a video and ask, why did they say that? Small children meet unkindness on screens before they have the words for it.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 25s)**
Here is the pathway view. Kindness on a screen is the same lesson as kindness in the playground, with one difference: on a screen you cannot see the other person's face, and sometimes the other person is not who they say they are. Your child does not need a lecture about danger. They need two things settled early: most people are kind, and you are the person they tell when someone is not. At this age the goal is not a child who spots every trick. It is a child who tells you everything, because telling has only ever gone well.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
At teatime or bedtime, borrow these exact words: did anything on a screen make you feel warm today? Did anything make you feel funny? Then just listen. Whatever they say, the answer is thank you for telling me, never a punishment and never less screen. Telling must always pay off. Then watch the kids version together, Sofia teaches it and they earn stars.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Not sure what to say if they tell you something worrying? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The warm feeling*
Grandma sends a video message: "I saw a duck today and thought of you!" A friend from school sends a picture of their new puppy. Question underneath, for you and your grown up to answer together: how do messages like these make you feel inside?

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Most people are kind*
Heading: Screens are full of kind people.
Body: Most people on screens are kind, just like most people in your street and your school. But here is something guardians know. Some people can be unkind. And some people pretend to be someone they are not, like wearing a dressing up costume you cannot see. That is never, ever your fault. Your job is easy: if anything feels wrong, you tell your grown up. And guess what? They already know all about it, because they watched a lesson too.

**SLIDE (keywords)** — *Guardian words*
kind: words and pictures that make hearts feel warm · unkind: words that make hearts feel sore · pretend: acting like someone you are not · tell: sharing it with your grown up, which is always the right move

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *What would a guardian do?*
Someone in a game says something mean about your player. What does a guardian do?
· Say something mean back (tap to reveal: mean words back make two sore hearts instead of one. Not this one.)
· Keep it a secret so nobody is cross (tap to reveal: guardians never have to carry sore feelings alone. Nobody will be cross with YOU.)
· Tell your grown up, even later, even at bedtime (tap to reveal: yes! That is the guardian move. Telling is never naughty. ⭐)

**SLIDE (interactive: verdict-sort)** — *The guardian sort*
Four messages float in one at a time. Sort each one onto a wall: WARM HEART or TELL A GROWN UP. Do it together, your grown up can help you read.
1. "You played brilliantly today!" (warm heart)
2. "Nobody likes you." (tell a grown up)
3. "Happy birthday!! 🎂" (warm heart)
4. "Do not tell anyone we talked." (tell a grown up)
Reaction lines: warm sorts get Sofia's "that one makes hearts glow!", tell sorts get "good spotting, guardian. Anyone who says keep it secret is someone to tell about. And remember, it is never your fault."

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The funny feeling*
You are watching videos and one pops up that makes your tummy feel funny. You did not do anything wrong, it just appeared. You look at your grown up. What could you say? (Try it out loud right now: "Can I show you something?")

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
A message made your tummy feel funny, but you are worried your grown up might be cross. What is true?
· Telling gets you in trouble (reaction: never. Telling is never naughty, and it is never your fault. Your grown up just promised, ask them!)
· Guardians keep worries secret (reaction: guardians are brave, and brave means telling.)
· Telling is always the right move, and nobody will be cross with you (reaction: yes, guardian! That is the whole rule. ⭐)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
Someone on a screen is unkind to you. Whose fault is it?
· Mine, I must have done something wrong (reaction: no, sweetheart, never. Unkindness is never your fault. Not once, not ever.)
· It is never my fault, and I tell my grown up (reaction: exactly right. You are a true Kindness Guardian. Stars incoming! ⭐)
· Nobody's, unkind words do not matter (reaction: your feelings do matter. That is why we tell.)
