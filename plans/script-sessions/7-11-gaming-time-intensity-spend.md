# Script session: Gaming, time, intensity and spend (7 to 11)

**Band:** 7 to 11 (parent first, child version via Star Lesson)
**Module:** Gaming: time, intensity and spend
**Cast:** Oliver leads (screen time boss, squad register with capes and football), DiGi opens and closes, DiGi Junior pause
**Single action outcome:** I decide my finish line before I press play, and anything in a game that costs money goes past my grown up first.

---

## Track P — the parent session (60 to 90 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 12s)**
Dinner is ready. You call once. You call twice. Your nine year old is mid match and stopping now means letting the whole team down. By the third call you are both shouting, and the evening is gone.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 25s)**
Here is the bit nobody tells you. Football has a whistle. Games do not. The next round starts by itself, the streak begs you to come back tomorrow, and the mystery box counts down so there is no time to think. Your child is not addicted and not defiant. They are playing something built to remove every natural stopping point, because the makers earn more the longer they play. So the fix is not fewer minutes shouted from the kitchen. The fix is putting the whistle back, and handing it to your child before the game starts.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
Before the next session, agree the finish line together in the game's own units. Two matches. One level. Game units beat clock minutes every time, because nobody wants to stop mid match. Then one house rule, said warmly: anything in a game that costs money comes to me first, every time, and you will never be in trouble for asking. When you are ready, send the kids version. Oliver teaches it as a match plan and your child earns stars for playing like a boss.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Questions about a game your child loves? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *One more round*
You are mid game. Dinner gets called. On the screen a countdown appears: next match starts in five, four, three. Your legs know it is dinner time. Your thumbs are already pressing play. What is your body doing right now, and who pressed play, you or the game?

**SLIDE (concept)** — *The missing whistle*
Heading: Games are built to keep you playing.
Body: Games are brilliant. That is not the trick. The trick is that the makers earn more the longer you stay, so they took out the stopping points. The next round starts by itself. The streak begs you back tomorrow. Losing says one more try and winning says one more win. None of that is an accident, and none of it makes you weak. It means the whistle is missing, so a boss brings their own.

**SLIDE (concept)** — *The mystery box trick*
Heading: Surprise prizes you pay for before you know what is inside.
Body: Some games sell boxes and packs where you pay first and find out after. The maybe is the exciting bit, and it is exciting on purpose. Most boxes hold the common stuff, and the game never owes you the rare thing, no matter how many you open. Games earn a lot of money this way. A boss knows the trick, so the trick stops working.

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *The streak test*
Your two matches are done, and the game flashes: one more win keeps your streak alive! What is the boss move?
· Play on and keep it quiet (tap to reveal: that is the game moving your finish line, not you. Secret extra time means the game is the boss.)
· Stop. Let the streak go (tap to reveal: boss move. The streak is the game's rope pulling you back. A boss can drop the rope. ⭐)
· Ask your grown up out loud if the finish line can move (tap to reveal: also a boss move. Asking out loud is allowed. Deciding secretly is not. The boss talks to the team.)

**SLIDE (interactive: signal-meter)** — *The one more go meter*
A meter slides from CALM, I CAN STOP to ITCHY, MUST KEEP PLAYING. Three moments appear one at a time. Set the meter for each:
1. You just finished a match and your friend is calling you outside. (low)
2. The game says your streak ends at midnight. (rising)
3. A mystery box with a countdown timer appears mid game. (high)
The higher the needle, the more the game is doing the deciding. Reaction line: the big itchy feeling is not a reason to keep playing. It is the exact moment for the whistle.

**SLIDE (diagram)** — *The boss loop*
One loop path: PLAN IT (say the finish line out loud) → PLAY IT (enjoy every minute, guilt free) → WHISTLE (stop where you said) → NEXT THING (snack, outside, friend). One side branch off PLAY IT: does it cost money? → show your grown up FIRST, then decide together.

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The countdown box*
Your favourite game flashes a special mystery box. A rare prize MIGHT be inside. It costs coins, and coins cost real money. A timer is counting down: gone in ten minutes! Your heart is going fast. Freeze right there. What is the timer for?

**SLIDE (discussion)** — *Talk it out*
Why do you think the box has a countdown timer on it? Talk about it. (Steer: timers rush your brain past the thinking part. A fair deal never needs you to hurry. When something rushes you, that is your clue to slow down.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
You have opened four mystery boxes and got common prizes every time. Your friend says the rare one HAS to be next. Is your friend right?
· Yes, after four common ones the rare one is due (reaction: this is the trick that catches even grown ups. Every box is a brand new maybe. The game never owes you the rare one, not after four boxes, not after forty.)
· No, every box is the same maybe, no matter how many you open (reaction: boss brain. That is the whole trick, and you just switched it off. ⭐)
· Yes, if you pay real money instead of coins (reaction: paying more just means paying more. The maybe stays exactly the same size.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
When is the best time to decide when you will stop playing?
· Before you press play, said out loud (reaction: plan it, play it, stop it. You are the boss and the whistle is yours. ⭐)
· In the middle of a match, when it feels right (reaction: mid match is when the game shouts loudest. Nobody hears the whistle then.)
· Never. Stopping is for players who are losing (reaction: even champions leave the pitch. Leaving on your own whistle IS the win.)
