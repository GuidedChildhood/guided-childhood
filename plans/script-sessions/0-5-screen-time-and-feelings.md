# Script session: Screen time and how devices make a small child feel (0 to 5)

**Band:** 0 to 5 (parent only, the child never watches)
**Module:** gap. Nothing in the 21 modules covers this band; this session helps define the new 0 to 5 parent only strand named in plans/parent-video-research-2026-07.md.
**Cast:** DiGi only (golden star). No child track at this band.
**Single action outcome for the parent:** I can end screen time without a battle by bridging the transition and naming the feeling.

---

## Track P — the parent session (60 to 90 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 12s)**
The episode ends. You say all done, and your two year old melts down like you switched off the sun. Every parent of a small child knows this exact thirty seconds. It is not just your house, and it is not just your child.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 30s)**
Here is the pathway view. That meltdown is not naughtiness and it is not addiction. A small child's brain cannot yet manage two things at once: leaving something wonderful, and the big feeling that comes with leaving it. Screens are built to hold attention, so the leaving is genuinely hard. Your child is not giving you a hard time. They are having a hard time. And the research is on your side here: a 2025 review in JMIR Pediatrics found that when parents build knowledge and skills around screens, screen time came down for parents and children both. What you learn tonight actually moves the needle.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
One move, two parts. First, the bridge: two minutes before the end, get down to their level and say one more, then we wave bye bye to the tablet. Let them press the off button, small hands love a job. Second, name the feeling as it lands: you loved that, and it is hard to stop. You are sad. That is okay. You are not fixing the feeling. You are teaching them it has a name and it passes. That is the whole lesson, and at this age the lesson is always yours, not theirs.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Rough evening? Tell me what happened and we will work out tomorrow together.
