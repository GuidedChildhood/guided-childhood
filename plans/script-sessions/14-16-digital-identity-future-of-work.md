# Script session: Digital identity and the future of work (14 to 16)

**Band:** 14 to 16 (parent briefing, near adult child version)
**Module:** Digital identity and the future of work (module 21, the final module on the pathway)
**Cast:** Vix the fox leads (street smart register, still 8365a8ff, first motion beats are a build task), DiGi opens and closes, DiGi pause
**Single action outcome:** I can author my name online on purpose, because a portfolio beats gossip every time.

---

## Track P — the parent briefing (about 70 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 10s)**
Type your teenager's name into a search engine tonight. Whatever comes up, that is the front page of their CV now, and either they wrote it or the internet wrote it for them. Most sixteen year olds have never once looked at their own front page.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 25s)**
The old conversation about their name online was all brakes: do not post that, it will follow you. True, and only half the story. Admissions tutors and employers do look people up, and here is what actually decides how that search lands: not the absence of embarrassment, the presence of evidence. A small portfolio, one project page, a team listing, work with their name on it, tells a stronger story than fifty rumours and outranks the party photo in the story a stranger walks away with. Your teenager's name is an asset they can author, starting now, and the ones who learn this at sixteen walk into every application ahead. That is the reframe: from fear about the past to authorship of the record.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 25s)**
Search their name together, and keep it light, no ambush. One question: is this the version of you that you would pick? Then the better question: what is one thing you have made or done this year that deserves to be findable? Then send the session. It is the last stop on the whole pathway, and Vix hands them the pen with the respect that moment deserves.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Questions about portfolios, applications, or what a search says about anyone in your house? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (concept)** — *What the search actually shows*
Heading: Admissions tutors and employers do look. Here is what they see.
Body: When a stranger with one place to offer searches a name, they find whatever is public: posts, tags, team pages, comments, things made and shared. What lands well is simple: evidence of making things, consistency over time, real interests pursued properly. What lands badly is just as simple: cruelty, and things posted in anger. And a completely blank page? Not a disaster, but a missed open goal: the one moment a stranger went looking for your story, and nothing was there to tell it. The search happens either way. The only question is what it finds.

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Portfolio beats gossip*
Heading: One made thing outweighs fifty rumours.
Body: Here is the mechanism, and it is good news. The story a stranger takes away from a search is dominated by the strongest signal on the page, and work is a stronger signal than noise. One project written up, one team listing, one piece of code or art or analysis or sport or music with your name on it, and the whole search reorganises around it. Gossip has no answer to a portfolio. This is also the grown up version of a tool you already own: at eleven the future you test was a brake, would future you thank you for posting this. From here it is also an engine: what would future you want findable?

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *Two searches, one place*
Two candidates, same grades, one offer left. Search the first name: an old gamertag, a few tagged party photos, nothing else. Search the second: a short blog of match analyses that got better over two years, a school robotics team page, a small photography account with a consistent eye. Nobody famous, nothing viral, just signal. Question underneath: the second candidate never went viral and never tried to. What did they do instead, and when did they start?

### BEAT 2 — CONCEPT (identity beat, 10s, silent render + cloned voice)
```
CHARACTER: Vix the fox
SETTING:   same staging, the board now reads three words stacked:
           SIGNAL, NOISE, SMOKE
BOARD:     BUILD SIGNAL ON PURPOSE
MOOD:      confiding, counting like a professional

SHOT 1 (0 to 10s)
  ACTION:  Vix taps each word once, top to bottom
  VOICE:   "Everything with your name near it is one of three things.
            Signal: work, effort, evidence. Noise: harmless filler that
            says nothing. Smoke: the stuff that works against you.
            Authors build signal, cut smoke, and let the noise go."
```

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *The strategy question*
You have one hour a week to spend on your name online. Where does it go?
· Chasing a viral moment, because one big hit fixes everything (tap to reveal: virality is a lottery ticket, and strangers reading your search do not want a lottery winner, they want a pattern. One viral clip says luck. Two years of steady work says character.)
· Making one small real thing and posting it with your name on it, week after week (tap to reveal: that is authorship. Signal compounds, and in two years the search result is a body of work nobody can gossip over the top of. ⭐)
· Deleting everything and going fully invisible (tap to reveal: invisibility is not control. Tags, team lists and other people's posts mean the page about you gets written anyway, just without you holding the pen. Blank is not safe, it is silent.)

**SLIDE (interactive: verdict-sort)** — *Signal, noise or smoke*
Six items connected to one name slide in, one at a time. Sort each: SIGNAL, NOISE, or SMOKE. A write up of a design project with photos of the process (signal). A meme reply on a friend's post (noise). An angry pile on comment from two years ago, still public (smoke, and it is deletable tonight). A school concert programme with your name in the cast (signal, and you did not even post it). A daily streak of selfies (noise, mostly, unless photography is the craft). A fake account kept for saying things you would not sign (smoke, and the accounts people think are anonymous have a long history of not staying that way). Reaction line: notice the split. Signal takes effort, smoke takes seconds, and the search engine treats them exactly the same. That is why authors audit.

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (discussion)** — *Talk it out*
AI is changing what jobs look like faster than any careers advice can keep up. So what is actually worth building now? Talk about it. (Steer: the search result is really a record of the things machines are worst at: judgement, taste, persistence, teamwork, the ability to finish. A portfolio does not just show what you made. It shows you are the kind of person who makes things, and that signal survives every wave of new tools. Build the record of being that person.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
The safest plan is to post nothing, ever, so there is nothing bad to find. What is true?
· Correct, blank is bulletproof (reaction: this is the misconception that feels wise and costs the most. You do not control blank: tags, team pages and other people's posts write your page anyway, without you. And when the one stranger with one offer goes looking, blank tells them nothing at the exact moment a portfolio would have spoken.)
· Authorship beats invisibility: build signal on purpose, cut smoke, and let your work do the talking (reaction: that is the whole module in one line. The page gets written either way. Hold the pen. ⭐)
· Post constantly, volume is what wins (reaction: volume without signal is noise, and noise buries your own best work. One made thing a month beats thirty empty posts a day.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
What actually builds the search result you want a stranger to find?
· Followers and likes, the numbers are the CV (reaction: a stranger with one offer barely reads the numbers. They read the work. Ten followers and a great project page beats ten thousand and nothing made.)
· Real work with your name on it, posted steadily, plus the future you test on everything else (reaction: signal, built on purpose, smoke cut before it sets. That is authoring your name, and it compounds from the day you start. ⭐)
· A witty bio (reaction: enjoyable, and it is furniture. The work is the signal. Write the bio after you have something for it to introduce.)
