# Script session: Bodies, image and pressure online (11 to 14)

**Band:** 11 to 14 (parent primer short, child version leads)
**Module:** Bodies, image and pressure online (module 14, KS3, self image strand per the Part B table; sensitive adjacent, register stays calm and steady throughout)
**Cast:** Brock the badger leads (wellbeing register, still 4edb2fc5, first motion beats are a build task), DiGi anchors with an extra steadying slide, DiGi Junior pause
**Single action outcome:** I can name one way images online are made to make me feel worse, and I can say it out loud to someone.
**Register note:** never graphic, never a lecture, zero shame. The tell someone route is normalised as ordinary, not dramatic.

---

## Track P — the parent primer (about 60 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 10s)**
Your 13 year old comes off their phone quieter than they went on. Nothing happened, they say. And technically nothing did. Forty minutes of other people's best angles happened.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 20s)**
Here is what is worth knowing. The faces and bodies in their feed are a highlight reel run through filters and editing, and the feed itself learns what they linger on, including the posts that make them feel worse, and quietly serves more of the same. That is not your child being fragile. That is a system doing what it is tuned to do. Naming that machinery out loud is the single most protective thing this module teaches, and it works better coming from the lesson than from you.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
Do not open with how do you feel about your body. Open sideways: do you reckon that person actually looks like that in real life? Let them be the expert, they know more about filters than you do. Then send the lesson. At this age the child version leads, send it and they earn stars, and Brock keeps it calm, honest and completely unembarrassing.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: If something in a feed is getting to your child, or to you, ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (concept)** — *How the image got made*
Heading: What you scroll past is built, not caught.
Body: Behind most polished posts sits a production line: the best of dozens of takes, chosen lighting, a chosen angle, a filter that reshapes skin, eyes and jawlines in real time, and sometimes editing on top of that. The person posting it often would not be recognised from it across a room. None of this makes them bad. It makes the image a piece of work, like a film poster. You would never compare your Tuesday to a film poster on purpose. The feed asks you to do it by accident, hundreds of times a day.

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *Forty minutes on a Tuesday*
A feed scrolls slowly in the frame: a gym transformation, a perfect skin routine, a holiday body, a jawline video, what I eat in a day. Nothing shocking. Nothing unkind. Just post after post of people at their absolute best. Question under the feed: you have been scrolling this for forty minutes. Without noticing, what has it been teaching you about normal?

**SLIDE (concept)** — *The comparison trap*
Heading: You compare your everything to their highlight.
Body: Here is the trap in one line: you know your own worst angles, bad days and unedited mornings, and you know nobody else's. So every comparison online runs your full self against someone else's selected best, judged by a brain that thinks it is looking at normal life. It is not a fair fight, and it was never meant to be one. The trap is not that you are weak. The trap is that the scoreboard is rigged.


### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *The pause that teaches the feed*
You keep pausing on transformation posts that leave you feeling flat about yourself. The feed starts serving you more of them. What is actually going on?
· The feed knows what you need to see (tap to reveal: the feed knows what holds your attention. Need never came into it. Those are very different things.)
· Pausing reads as interest, so the feed is feeding the exact thing that stings (tap to reveal: that is the machinery, named. The feed is not judging you. It is measuring you, and the measurement cannot tell interest from hurt. ⭐)
· It is a coincidence (tap to reveal: it is the opposite of a coincidence. It is the system working exactly as designed.)

**SLIDE (interactive: feed-loop)** — *Tune the feed yourself*
A practice feed appears. Round one: linger on the posts that would sting, and watch the feed retune toward them in three swipes. Round two: same feed, but this time follow two accounts that make things (art, cooking, sport clips) and linger there instead. Watch it retune the other way in three swipes. Reaction line: the feed has no opinion of you. It follows your attention anywhere you point it, which means the pointing is power, and the power is yours.

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (digi)** — *DiGi anchor: the ordinary move*
DiGi holds the room for one steady slide.
Bubble 1: One thing before the finish, and it is the most ordinary thing in this lesson.
Bubble 2: If a feed has been getting to you, saying it out loud to someone is not a big dramatic step. It is maintenance, like drinking water. A parent, a mate, a teacher, me. People who say it out loud feel lighter, usually within minutes.
Bubble 3: And if a friend says it to you, you do not need answers. You just need to not make it weird. That is the whole job.

**SLIDE (discussion)** — *Talk it out*
Someone says just stop caring what people look like online. Is that realistic? Talk about it. (Steer: caring how you come across is human and it is fine. The skill is not caring less, it is knowing what you are looking at: built images, a rigged comparison and a feed tuned to your pauses. Seeing the machinery is what takes its power, not pretending you are above it.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
Scrolling leaves you feeling worse about how you look. What does that mean about you?
· Something is wrong with you, everyone else scrolls fine (reaction: this is the belief that keeps the whole trap running, and it is false. The feed is tuned to hold attention, and stinging holds attention. Feeling it means the system works, not that you are broken.)
· Nothing about you, plenty about the machinery: built images, rigged comparison, a tuned feed (reaction: exactly. Name the machinery, retune the feed, say it out loud if it is heavy. All three are yours to do. ⭐)
· You should delete everything and never scroll again (reaction: nobody here deals in bans. The pathway is seeing clearly and tuning what you follow, and it works.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
A friend tells you their feed has been making them feel rubbish about their body. What is the move?
· Change the subject so it is not awkward (reaction: it only becomes awkward if you make it weird. Staying ordinary is the gift.)
· Hear them, do not make it weird, and back them telling an adult if it is heavy (reaction: that is it exactly. Ordinary voice, real listening, and the tell someone route stays open. That is what strong friends do. ⭐)
· Tell them everyone feels like that so it is fine (reaction: half true and it lands as be quiet. Hear them first. Common and worth saying out loud can both be true.)
