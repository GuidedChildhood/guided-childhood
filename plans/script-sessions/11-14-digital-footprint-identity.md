# Script session: Digital footprint and identity (11 to 14)

**Band:** 11 to 14 (parent primer short, child version leads)
**Module:** Privacy and digital reputation (module 7 voiced up to the KS3 register per the Part B table: digital footprint and identity is this band's register of that module)
**Cast:** Vix the fox leads (street smart register, still 8365a8ff, first motion beats are a build task), Zara takes the concept beat (truth register, canonical still e29b139c), DiGi opens and closes, DiGi Junior pause
**Single action outcome:** I can run the future you test before I post, because what I post can stay and can be copied.
**Modal wording rule:** always can stay and can be copied. Never everything is permanent forever. The honest modal claim is the strong claim.

---

## Track P — the parent primer (about 60 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 10s)**
Your 12 year old posts constantly and thinks about none of it, or posts nothing and watches everything. Either way, a version of them is already forming online, and right now nobody is steering it.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 20s)**
The honest framing matters here. Not everything they post is online forever, that line gets laughed out of the room because they know deleted posts vanish. The true version is stronger: anything posted can stay and can be copied, and can is enough. Between now and their twenties, coaches, sixth forms and employers will type their name into a search bar. The goal is not a child too scared to post. It is a teenager who posts like someone building a name they will want to keep.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
Search their name together tonight, and let them search yours. Whatever comes up, keep it light: that is the current version of you online, is it the one you would pick? Then send the lesson. At this age the child version leads, send it and they earn stars, and Vix teaches the future you test far better than a worried parent can.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Found something online you wish was not there? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (concept)** — *The trail you are already leaving*
Heading: Your footprint is everything with your name near it.
Body: Posts, comments, usernames, tagged photos, the clip a mate uploaded with you in the background, the reply you left on a stranger's video two years ago. Some of it you chose. Plenty of it you did not. Together it forms the searchable you, and it is being read by whoever looks: new friends, new schools, and later the people deciding whether to give you a trial, a place or a job. You cannot make the trail disappear. You can absolutely decide what it says from today.

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *Two search results*
Two search screens side by side, both Year 9 students, both real kinds of results. Left: a furious comment thread from Year 7, a username that seemed hilarious at eleven, a tagged photo mid rant at a match. Right: a goal compilation their mate posted, a school robotics win, a comment thread helping someone fix a game bug. Neither of them planned their page. One of them got lucky. Question under the screens: a trials coach has sixty seconds and both names. Which callback gets made, and why should luck have decided it?

**SLIDE (keywords)** — *Fox words*
footprint: everything online with your name near it · searchable: findable by anyone who types your name · copied: saved by someone else, beyond your delete button · the future you test: would the sixteen year old version of you thank you for posting this?

### BEAT 2 — CONCEPT (identity beat, 10s, silent render + cloned voice)

```
           CAN STAY. CAN BE COPIED.
BOARD:     CAN STAY. CAN BE COPIED.
MOOD:      focused, precise, a detective stating exactly what is provable

SHOT 1 (0 to 10s)
  ACTION:  Zara underlines each line once, then holds up two fingers

```

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *The delete button*
You posted something last year that makes you cringe now. You delete it. Sorted?
· Sorted, deleted means gone (tap to reveal: your copy is gone. Whether anyone saved, screenshotted or reposted it first is the part you cannot know. That is what can be copied means.)
· Probably sorted, nobody screenshots boring posts (tap to reveal: mostly true! Most posts die quietly. But you are betting, not knowing, and the odds get worse the more interesting the post was.)
· Your copy is gone, other copies can exist, which is why the pause beats the delete (tap to reveal: exactly right, in exactly the honest words. Delete is a mop. The pause is a lock. ⭐)

**SLIDE (interactive: verdict-sort)** — *The future you wall*
Five posts slide in one at a time. Run the future you test on each and sort it: POST IT, THINK FIRST, or KEEP IT OFFLINE.
1. Your best save from Sunday's match. (post it)
2. A furious reply to someone who wound you up ten seconds ago. (keep it offline, or at least outlast the ten seconds)
3. A joke about a teacher with the school named. (think first, jokes travel without their tone)
4. Your mate mid faceplant, they have not seen it yet. (think first, it is their footprint too, ask them)
5. The robotics build you finished last night. (post it, that is exactly what a name is built from)
Reaction lines: correct sorts get Vix's "sharp", wrong sorts get "run the test again: does the sixteen year old you thank you for this?"

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Owning your name*
Heading: The best defence is a good offence.
Body: Here is the fox secret nobody tells you: the strongest footprint is not an empty one. If the search finds nothing, strangers fill the gap with guesses. The move is to own your name: post the things you make, the games you win, the stuff you fix and build and help with. Every good thing you put up is a brick in a wall you chose, and it pushes the accidental stuff down the page. You are not hiding from the search. You are writing what it finds.

**SLIDE (discussion)** — *Talk it out*
Is it fair that a post from when you were eleven can follow you at sixteen? Talk about it. (Steer: probably not fair, and worth saying so. But fair is not the test the search bar runs. Two moves follow: give your past self some grace, everyone has an eleven year old chapter, and give your future self a head start, because from today the trail is yours to steer.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
Deleting a post removes it from the internet, so posting is always reversible. What is the detective correction?
· No correction, delete works (reaction: this is the one that catches nearly everyone. Delete removes YOUR copy. A post can stay and can be copied, and the copies never asked your permission. Reversible is a bet, not a fact.)
· Anything you post can stay and can be copied, so the pause comes before the post (reaction: the exact honest words, and the whole lesson in one line. ⭐)
· Nothing can ever be deleted, everything is online forever (reaction: too far, and detectives do not overclaim. Plenty of posts do vanish. Can stay is the truth, and can is enough.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
You want a footprint that works FOR you when someone searches your name. What is the fox strategy?
· Post nothing, ever, an empty search is a safe search (reaction: an empty search is a blank page a stranger writes for you. Foxes do not leave the pen on the table.)
· Post the things you make and do well, and run the future you test on the rest (reaction: that is owning your name. You write what the search finds. ⭐)
· Make everything private and it stops mattering (reaction: settings help and are worth doing, but tagged photos, screenshots and mates' posts live outside your settings. The test travels everywhere. Settings do not.)
