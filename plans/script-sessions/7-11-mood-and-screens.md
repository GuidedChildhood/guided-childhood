# Script session: Mood and screens (7 to 11)

**Band:** 7 to 11 (parent first, child version via Star Lesson)
**Module:** Mood and screens (media balance and wellbeing)
**Cast:** Oliver leads (screen time boss, squad register with capes and football), DiGi opens and closes, DiGi Junior pause
**Single action outcome:** I notice how I feel after screen time, and I run a cool down lap when I need one.

---

## Track P — the parent session (60 to 90 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 12s)**
Some days the tablet goes off and your child bounces away happy. Other days the same thirty minutes ends in a slammed door and tears. Same child, same tablet, same amount of time. You cannot see the difference, and neither can they.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 25s)**
It is rarely the minutes. It is what filled them, and how they ended. An hour building a world with a friend and an hour of videos they barely remember leave a body in two completely different states, even though the clock says the same thing. So the skill to teach is not watching less, it is noticing more: how do I feel when the screen goes off, filled up or emptied out? A child who can read their own after feeling has a tool for life, and it never once requires you to say screens are bad, because some screen time genuinely fills them up.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
Tonight when the screen goes off, skip what did you watch and ask this instead: how do you feel right now, filled up or emptied out? No lecture after the answer, whatever it is. Just notice together, then give them two minutes of landing space before the next thing. When you are ready, send the kids version. Oliver teaches the cool down lap and your child earns stars for running it.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Wondering about your child's moods around screens? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (concept)** — *The after feeling*
Heading: Every screen thing leaves an after feeling.
Body: When the screen goes off, your body gives you a reading. Making things, playing with friends, a video call with your cousins: those often leave you filled up, full of energy and ideas. Endless videos you barely remember, watching stuff you do not even like, losing on repeat: those often leave you emptied out, flat and grumpy. This is not good screens versus bad screens. It is YOUR body's readout, and everyone's readout is different. The superpower is noticing yours.

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *Two Saturdays*
Saturday one: you spend an hour building an amazing world with your friend. You come off buzzing, and you tell everyone at dinner about it. Saturday two: you spend an hour on videos you cannot even remember. You come off snappy and bored. Here is the mystery: it was the SAME hour on the SAME tablet. So what was different?

**SLIDE (keywords)** — *Boss words*
after feeling: how your body feels when the screen goes off · filled up: energy, ideas, wanting to tell someone about it · emptied out: flat, grumpy, wanting more but enjoying it less · cool down lap: the footballer's trick for landing gently

### BEAT 2 — CONCEPT (identity beat, 10s, silent render + cloned voice)

```
SHOT 1 (0 to 10s)
  ACTION:  Oliver jogs a slow little circle on the spot, mimes a drink,

  VOICE:   "The cool down lap in one breath. Screen goes off, do not jump

            look out of a window. Two minutes. Then ask: filled up or
            emptied out? Footballers never stop dead. They lap."
```

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *The flat feeling*
You have been watching videos for a while, and you notice you feel flat and a bit grumpy. But your thumb keeps scrolling anyway. What is the boss move?
· Keep scrolling, maybe the next video fixes it (tap to reveal: the flat feeling almost never scrolls away. That is the emptied out signal talking, and it is worth listening to.)
· Screen down, run the cool down lap, then pick something that fills you up (tap to reveal: boss move. Two minutes of lap, and you get to choose what happens next instead of your thumb choosing. ⭐)
· Decide videos are terrible and never watch anything again (tap to reveal: videos are not the enemy. Some fill you right up! The skill is noticing which ones.)

**SLIDE (interactive: star-breath)** — *The cool down breath*
DiGi's golden star sits in the middle of the screen. Breathe in for four counts as the star slowly grows, breathe out for four counts as it shrinks. Three rounds. Reaction line: that was lap one of the cool down. Feel that? Your body slowed down, and the after feeling just got easier to hear.

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Swaps that help*
Heading: The refill list.
Body: When you land emptied out, small swaps refill you fast. Kick a ball against a wall. Help make the snack. Tell someone the best bit of what you watched. Draw the thing you just built. Put music on and be ridiculous for one song. A swap is not a punishment for screen time. It is the refill after it, and it makes the NEXT screen time better too.

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (discussion)** — *Your two lists*
What screen things genuinely fill YOU up? What leaves you flat? Make your two lists and compare with a neighbour. (Steer: everyone's lists are different, and there are no wrong answers. The kid next to you might get filled up by the exact thing that empties you out. That is why only YOU can read your own meter.)

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The tricky bit*
Here is something strange: the emptied out things are often the hardest ones to stop, because they keep whispering one more, one more. That is not you being weak. That is how they are built, remember the missing whistle? But you have something the game does not know about: an after feeling. It is like having your own private referee, and the referee never lies to you.

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
You feel flat and grumpy after screen time. What does that mean?
· Screens are bad and you should feel bad (reaction: nobody is bad here, not you and not the screen. Flat is just a signal, like feeling thirsty. Signals are for using, not for feeling bad about.)
· It is a signal: run the cool down lap and pick a refill swap (reaction: boss move. Notice, lap, swap. ⭐)
· Nothing, ignore it and power through (reaction: your body is the referee, and good players listen to the referee.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
The cool down lap is...
· A punishment for too much screen time (reaction: never a punishment. Champions lap because it makes the next thing better.)
· Two minutes of move, water and window between the screen and the next thing (reaction: that is the lap. Land gently, then choose what is next. ⭐)
· Another word for stopping screens forever (reaction: nobody is stopping forever. We are just landing instead of crashing.)
