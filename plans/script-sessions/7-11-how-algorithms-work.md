# Script session: How algorithms work (7 to 11)

**Band:** 7 to 11 (parent first, child version via Star Lesson)
**Module:** How algorithms work (safe search lives here)
**Cast:** Zara leads (truth finder, squad register with capes), DiGi opens and closes, DiGi Junior pause
**Single action outcome:** I know my feed is testing what keeps me watching, and I can steer it on purpose.

---

## Track P — the parent session (60 to 90 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 12s)**
You glance over your child's shoulder. Twenty minutes ago it was funny animal videos. Now it is something you would never have chosen for them, and here is the strange part: they never searched for any of it. The feed chose. Nobody asked it to.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 25s)**
The feed is not a TV channel someone programmed. It is a test that never stops running. Every video measures one thing: did your child keep watching? Time on the screen is the loudest signal it reads, and here is the catch, even a horrified stare counts as yes, more please. So the answer is not grabbing the phone every time something odd appears. The answer is showing your child the test, because the moment children learn the feed is watching them, most of them find it thrilling that they can train it back.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
Tonight, sit next to them and ask one question: why do you think it showed you that? Then train the feed together. Skip fast past the junk, sit with the good stuff, tap not interested once. While you are in the settings, switch safe search on. Two minutes, done together, no lecture. When you are ready, send the kids version. Zara teaches it as a detective case and your child earns stars for taking the wheel.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Wondering why the feed showed your child something? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (concept)** — *The feed is a test*
Heading: Every video is a question.
Body: Your feed is not a list somebody made for you. It is a test that runs every second. Each video quietly asks: will you keep watching? If you stay, the answer is yes, and the feed lines up more like it. If you skip, the answer is no. Here is the detective clue: the feed never asks if a video is good, true or kind. It only asks if you kept watching.

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Watch time is the loudest signal*
Heading: Your seconds are votes.
Body: The feed cannot see your face and cannot read your mind. It counts seconds. Every second you stay on a video is a vote for MORE OF THIS. And here is the trap: watching something because it is shocking, weird or annoying still counts as a yes vote. The feed cannot tell delighted from disgusted. It just counts.

**SLIDE (keywords)** — *Detective words*
algorithm: the recipe that picks what you see next · signal: a clue you give the feed, like watching, liking or sharing · watch time: how long you stay, the loudest signal of all · steer: training your feed on purpose

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *The ugh video*
A video pops up that makes you go ugh. But it is so weird you watch it all the way to the end. What did you just tell the feed?
· Nothing, because I did not like it (tap to reveal: the feed cannot hear like. It counts seconds, and you gave it every single one.)
· More weird ugh videos please (tap to reveal: case cracked. Watching to the end is the loudest yes there is, even when your face says no. ⭐)
· The feed knows I hated it (tap to reveal: it cannot know. It counts time, not feelings. That is why detectives skip fast.)

**SLIDE (interactive: feed-loop)** — *The loop machine*
A pretend feed fills the screen with a mix: football clips, drawing videos, weird junk, animal facts. Tap a video to watch it and see the feed refill with more of the same. Skip one fast and watch that kind fade away. Three rounds: build a feed you would actually love, on purpose. Reaction line: you just did in thirty seconds what the feed does to you all day. Now you know the recipe.

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Safe search, the detective's filter*
Heading: A good tool for a good detective.
Body: Safe search is a setting that keeps the yucky stuff out of your search results before you ever see it. It is not a cage and it is not because anyone is in trouble. It is a filter, like a goalkeeper for your screen. Turn it on WITH your grown up, and it quietly does its job while you get on with your case.

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The rabbit hole*
One night you watch ONE spooky story video before bed. Just one. A week later your whole feed is spooky stories, and you do not even like them anymore. Nobody did this to you on purpose. So how did it happen, and what would a detective do about it?

**SLIDE (discussion)** — *Talk it out*
If the feed only ever shows you more of what you already watched, what might you never get to discover? Talk about it: who decides what you find, you or the recipe? (Steer: the feed narrows the world. Searching for something new on purpose widens it again. Detectives search on purpose.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
Your feed is full of scary videos you do not want. What is the detective steer?
· Keep watching them so the feed gets bored and stops (reaction: this is the trap. Watching is a yes vote, so it will send MORE. The feed never gets bored before you do.)
· Skip fast, tap not interested, then go watch things you actually love (reaction: case cracked. Every second on the good stuff retrains the recipe. ⭐)
· Hide the tablet in a drawer forever (reaction: you do not have to quit. You have the steering wheel now, so steer.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
The feed shows you a video first because...
· It is the truest and best video there is (reaction: the feed never checks true. That job belongs to you.)
· It is the video most likely to keep YOU watching (reaction: that is the whole case, detective. Stars incoming. ⭐)
· A person at the company picked it just for you (reaction: no person picks. A recipe picks, and your seconds are the ingredients.)
