# Script session: Social media, group chats and the workarounds (11 to 14)

**Band:** 11 to 14 (parent primer short, child version leads)
**Module:** Social media, group chats, and the workarounds (module 11, KS3)
**Cast:** Vix the fox leads (street smart register, still 8365a8ff, first motion beats are a build task), DiGi opens and closes, DiGi Junior pause
**Single action outcome:** I can name what a workaround costs before I take it.
**Ban world context:** the under 16 social media access ban is confirmed for Spring 2027. Messaging apps stay. Group chats are the part of their social world that does not go away, and the workaround question is already live.

---

## Track P — the parent primer (about 60 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 10s)**
It is 9.40pm on a Tuesday and your 12 year old's phone shows 214 unread messages in one group chat. You say something. They say you do not get it. They are right, and so are you.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 20s)**
That chat is not noise to them. It is where their social world lives, and when the under 16 ban lands in Spring 2027 the feeds go but the group chats stay. So the pressure moves there, and so does the workaround talk: the VPN everyone supposedly has, the borrowed account, the birthday that adds three years. You cannot referee any of it from outside. What you can be is the person they price a workaround with instead of the person they hide it from.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
One question at dinner, then nothing but listening: what is the worst thing about your group chats? Do not fix anything. The point is that chat trouble is now a thing you two talk about. Then send the lesson. At this age the child version leads, send it and they earn stars. Vix teaches what a workaround really costs, and it lands better from a fox than a parent.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Questions about the ban, a chat blowup or a workaround you overheard? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *Tuesday night, 214 unread*
A group chat fills the frame: Year 8 (real one), 34 members. Maya sent a voice note that got clipped and reposted. Now it is 60 messages of piling on, three crying laughing reactions a second, and two people typing at all times. You were not even part of it. You just watched it happen in real time. Question under the chat: what is your thumb doing right now?

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Leaving loudly and muting quietly*
Heading: There are three exits from a pile on.
Body: Exit one: join in. Costs you nothing tonight, costs Maya plenty, and the chat remembers who piled on. Exit two: leave loudly. Person left the group appears in front of 34 people and now YOU are the story. Exit three: mute quietly. Nobody sees it. The chat carries on without your attention, you check it when you choose, and you have taken no side except your own. The strongest move in a group chat is often the one nobody can see.

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The screenshot that travels*
Dan sends Leah a private message about someone in the chat. Just between us. Leah screenshots it. By Thursday it is in three other chats, including one with the person it was about. Nobody hacked anything. One tap did it. A message is only as private as the least careful person who can see it.

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Pricing the big three*
Heading: What the workarounds actually cost.
Body: The VPN: the app still thinks you are an adult, so it serves you the adult version. Adult feed, adult strangers, none of the guardrails, and no one to tell because you are not supposed to be there. The borrowed account: you live under someone else's name, their settings, their contacts, their mess. Anything that happens is attached to them and hidden from everyone who could help you. The fake birthday: three added years follow you around the platform, deciding who can contact you and what gets shown to you. None of this is a lecture. It is a price list.

**SLIDE (choice)** — *The offer*
Someone in the chat posts: everyone is using this VPN to get round the ban, takes two minutes, want the link? What is the fox move?
· Take the link, everyone else has (tap to reveal: everyone is doing the classic seller line, and notice the price never came up. Ask the price question first.)
· Ask yourself what it costs before you decide anything (tap to reveal: that is the fox rule working. Adult feed, no guardrails, nobody to tell. Now you are deciding with your eyes open. ⭐)
· Report the person who posted it (tap to reveal: they are not your enemy, they are pricing it wrong too. The rule is for you, not against them.)

**SLIDE (interactive: spread-race)** — *The screenshot race*
The screenshot from Dan's message enters the race. Tap once per share and watch it travel: 1 chat becomes 3, 3 become 9, 9 become 27 by Friday. Then try the same race after Dan never sends it. The fastest way to keep a message private stays the same in every round: do not put it where a screenshot can reach it. Reaction line at the end: nothing spreads slower than the thing you did not send.

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The quiet mute in action*
Thursday. The chat kicks off again, different target this time. Josh does not post anything. He does not leave. He mutes it, finishes what he was doing, and reads it in the morning when the storm is over and the pile on looks as ugly as it always looked. Nobody knows he did it. That is the point.

**SLIDE (discussion)** — *Talk it out*
Someone says muting a pile on is the same as letting it happen. Are they right? Talk about it. (Steer: muting protects your attention, it does not settle the question of Maya. If someone is getting hurt, telling an adult is a separate move, and it is not snitching, it is the only move that actually stops it. You can mute the noise AND flag the harm.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
A VPN gets you into a banned app without anyone knowing. So the workaround is free. What is wrong with that?
· Nothing, invisible means free (reaction: this is the one the seller wants you to believe. The app is not fooled into protecting you, it is fooled into treating you as an adult. Adult feed, adult strangers, zero guardrails, and no one to tell. That is the price.)
· The VPN might be slow (reaction: speed is not the cost that matters here. Look at what the app now thinks you are.)
· It is not free, you pay in what you see, who can reach you, and having nobody to tell (reaction: priced like a fox. Take it or leave it, but now you know the bill. ⭐)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
The chat is piling on someone and it will not stop. Which pair of moves actually works?
· Post that everyone should stop, then leave the group so they see you go (reaction: now there are two storms and one of them is about you. There is a quieter pair.)
· Mute the chat, and tell an adult what is happening to the person (reaction: attention protected, harm flagged. The strongest moves are the ones the chat never sees. ⭐)
· Screenshot the worst messages and post them in another chat so people know (reaction: that is the screenshot race with you holding the baton. It spreads the pile on, it does not stop it.)
