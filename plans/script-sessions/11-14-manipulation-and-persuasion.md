# Script session: Manipulation and persuasion (11 to 14)

**Band:** 11 to 14 (parent primer short, child version leads)
**Module:** Manipulation and persuasion (module 15 voiced down to the KS3 register per the Part B table, which places the topic in this band)
**Cast:** Zara leads (truth register, canonical still e29b139c, detective energy dialled to Y8), DiGi opens and closes, DiGi Junior pause
**Single action outcome:** I can name the technique being used on me.
**Carries up from module 12:** the third check, how is it trying to make me feel, in its locked wording, now aimed at design tricks and creators as well as posts.

---

## Track P — the parent primer (about 60 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 10s)**
Your 13 year old has to open the app today or the streak dies. Has to catch the drop at 7pm. Has to watch to the end because the creator said do not skip this. None of it feels optional to them, and that is not an accident.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 20s)**
Every one of those pressures is a designed technique with a name: streaks, countdowns, engineered outrage, the creator who calls their audience family right before the merch link. Your teenager is not weak willed for feeling the pull. Rooms of professionals built the pull. Here is the leverage: teenagers hate being played more than they love any app. Hand them the names of the techniques and their own pride does the rest.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
Tonight, play spot the technique together on your own phone. Find one thing an app does to keep you there: a badge, a streak, an autoplay. Name it, out loud, both of you. That is it. Then send the lesson. At this age the child version leads, send it and they earn stars, and Zara turns naming techniques into detective sport.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Want a second opinion on what an app or a creator is doing? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Dark patterns have names*
Heading: If it feels hard to stop, something was designed to make it hard.
Body: The streak that dies if you skip a day. The autoplay that starts the next video before you chose it. The badge that stays red until you come back. The maybe you also missed screen when you try to leave. These are called dark patterns: design built to steer your behaviour toward what profits the app, dressed up as features for you. None of them are accidents, and none of them are illegal. They are just invisible until someone points at them. Consider them pointed at.

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *One evening, four techniques*
A phone evening plays out in the frame. 7.02pm: streak reminder, your 147 day streak ends at midnight! 7.15pm: a creator's video opens with do NOT skip this, you will regret it. 7.40pm: a post designed to enrage is at the top of the feed, comments already at war. 8.05pm: you try to close the app and a screen slides up: people you know posted while you were away. Question under the scene: four pushes in one evening. How many did you feel, and how many did you see?

**SLIDE (keywords)** — *Detective words*
dark pattern: design that steers you toward what profits the app · urgency trick: fake time pressure that stops you thinking, streaks, countdowns, last chance · engineered outrage: content boosted because anger holds attention longer than anything else · parasocial pressure: feeling like a creator is your friend when the friendship only runs one way

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (concept)** — *The friend who cannot hear you*
Heading: Parasocial pressure, the technique with a heartbeat.
Body: A creator talks straight down the lens every day. They know your username maybe, your jokes, your comments. You know their face, their room, their dog. It feels mutual. It is not: you are one of a million viewers, and they cannot hear you. Most creators are decent people. The technique is what the feeling gets used for: you lot are family, so smash the merch link. Do not skip, you owe me the watch time. The buy is dressed as loyalty. Enjoy creators. Just remember which way the friendship runs when their hand goes out.

**SLIDE (choice)** — *The drop*
Your favourite creator posts: my merch drop is TONIGHT, only 500 ever, you know I never let you down, family looks after family. You were not planning to spend anything this week. What is the detective read?
· They never let me down, and 500 is not many (tap to reveal: count the techniques in one sentence: urgency, scarcity, and family aimed at your wallet. Three in one line is not love, it is craft.)
· Name what it is doing: urgency plus parasocial pressure, then decide with your own head (tap to reveal: case cracked. You can still buy it! But now it is your decision, not the technique's. That is the whole difference. ⭐)
· Unfollow them, it is all fake (tap to reveal: nobody said the content is fake, the pressure is the part that is engineered. Keep what you enjoy, drop what plays you.)

**SLIDE (interactive: class-tally)** — *Which one has caught you?*
Anonymous tally, nobody's answer is shown with a name. Which technique has actually worked on you this month? Tap all that apply: kept a streak alive when I did not want to open the app · watched a whole video because the start said do not skip · got angry at a post built to make me angry · nearly bought something because a countdown was running · stayed because the leaving screen showed me one more thing. The class total builds live. Reaction line: look at the board. Every hand in this room, including whoever is teaching it. The techniques work on humans. That is why we name them instead of pretending we are immune.

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The outrage machine*
A post rockets across the feed: a clip cut to make someone look as bad as possible, caption asking how is nobody talking about this?? The comments are a bonfire, and the algorithm is feeding it oxygen because every furious reply is engagement, and engagement is money. Somewhere behind it, someone is watching the numbers climb. Question under the scene: your blood is up and your thumb is on the reply box. Who profits if you post?

**SLIDE (discussion)** — *Talk it out*
If the techniques work on everyone, is there any point fighting them? Talk about it. (Steer: naming is not immunity, it is a head start. You will still feel the pull, the difference is a two second gap between feeling it and obeying it. Every technique in this lesson needs you not to notice. Noticing is the whole fight, and you now do it on sight.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
A creator you have watched daily for two years says they need the follows on their new account, family supports family. They are basically a friend, so what is the truth here?
· They are basically a friend, two years is two years (reaction: this is the seeded one, and it is the technique itself talking. Two years of you watching them is not two years of them knowing you. The feeling is real. The friendship runs one way.)
· They are a stranger you know a lot about, and family is doing the work of a sales line (reaction: named like a detective. Support creators you rate, gladly, but as a choice, not a family obligation. ⭐)
· Creators are all liars (reaction: too far the other way. Most are decent. The technique is the target, not the person.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
The app says your 200 day streak dies at midnight. You are tired and you do not want to open it. What is actually at stake?
· Two hundred days of work (reaction: the streak is a counter, not a savings account. Nothing you made disappears. Only the number resets, and the number was never for you, it was for your return rate.)
· Nothing real: the streak is an urgency trick, and naming it hands the choice back to you (reaction: case cracked. Open it or sleep, but let it be you deciding. ⭐)
· Your friends will think less of you (reaction: nobody has ever admired anyone for a streak. The app is counting on you thinking they do.)
