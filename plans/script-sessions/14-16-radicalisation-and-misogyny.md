# Script session: Radicalisation and misogyny (14 to 16)

**Band:** 14 to 16 (parent briefing, near adult child version)
**Module:** Radicalisation and misogyny (module 18, KS4, DSL flagged)
**SENSITIVE: DiGi only, calm register.** Wry respect, zero lecture. No squad characters, minimal staging, nothing graphic, no jokes inside the hard content, warmth at the close. The spine throughout: recognise it, refuse it, report it, and you are not in trouble.
**Cast:** DiGi only, every beat
**Single action outcome:** I can recognise when content is grooming my beliefs, and I can ask who profits from me feeling this way.

---

## Track P — the parent briefing (about 70 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 10s)**
Your teenager says something at the dinner table about women, or about some group, that stops the room. It did not come from your house. It was delivered to them, in about two hundred small doses, by a feed that learned exactly what holds their attention.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 30s)**
Nobody gets recruited into an extreme worldview in a day. The pipeline is a drip feed: it opens with jokes and half truths, the algorithm serves each next step slightly stronger because outrage holds attention, and the community around the content offers something your teenager genuinely wants, which is belonging. That is why arguing the worldview head on so rarely works. And it is why the worst possible response is the one that comes most naturally: that is disgusting, hand over the phone, I am deleting all of it. Confiscation threats are the number one reason teenagers stop telling you what they are watching, and they hand the content its favourite proof, that the adults really are trying to silence it. Say this instead: that is interesting, where did that come from? Show me what this person is about. I actually want to understand it. Curiosity keeps the channel open. The channel is everything.

**P-BEAT 3 — THE HANDOFF (mission beat, 25s)**
The protection here is not a blocked list, it is a question your teenager learns to ask for themselves: who profits from me feeling this way? Rage retains audiences, and blame sells courses. Once they see the machinery, the spell thins on its own. So share the session with them. DiGi walks it through, no lecture. It names the pattern honestly, respects their intelligence, and hands them the question.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Worried about what a feed is feeding someone you love? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (concept)** — *How the pipeline works*
Heading: Nobody is handed a worldview on day one.
Body: Day one is a joke. Edgy, a bit forbidden, genuinely funny. Because a joke gets in the door without being examined, nobody fact checks a punchline. Then the algorithm does its only job: you watched that, so here is something slightly stronger, because stronger holds attention longer. Each step is small enough to feel like nothing. Months later, ideas that would have sounded absurd at the start now feel like common sense, and the viewer is certain they arrived there by thinking for themselves. That is the drip feed. The dose is never the danger. The direction is.

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The channel that turned*
An account that seems like a self improvement channel: gym clips, money talk, discipline, confidence. The early advice is genuinely useful, which is what makes the next part work. Slowly, between the useful clips, another message starts arriving: the reason your life is not where you want it is women, or some other them. The pattern is almost always the same three steps. Your pain is real, which is true. Here is exactly who to blame, which is false. And here is my course, my subscription, my merch, which is the till ringing. Question underneath: the useful advice was free. What was it actually buying?

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Belonging is the hook*
Heading: The pipeline sells brotherhood, not just blame.
Body: The creator talks to camera like a mate who finally gets it. The comment section feels like a locker room where you are already accepted. That feeling is real, and wanting it is not weakness, it is being human. The pipeline knows this, which is why lonely and left behind are its favourite audiences. But notice what this belonging costs: it only holds together as long as everyone keeps agreeing about the enemy. Real friendship survives disagreement. This kind evaporates the moment you question the script. That difference is worth everything.

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *The slide*
A creator you like starts drifting from fitness advice into contempt for women. The useful content is still in there. What is the honest read?
· The good advice proves the rest is probably right too (tap to reveal: the good advice is the delivery mechanism. Being right about press ups is not being right about half the human race. Judge each claim on its own feet.)
· Notice the direction of travel, keep the question running, and curate what you follow with your own head (tap to reveal: that is it. Nobody is telling you to unfollow fitness. You are being asked to notice when a channel starts farming your anger, and to choose on purpose. ⭐)
· Anyone who ever watched this content is already lost (tap to reveal: no. Watching something does not make anyone a bad person, and it does not put you in trouble. Recognising the machine is the skill, and you just did it.)

**SLIDE (discussion)** — *Talk it out*
Why do these pipelines aim hardest at people who feel lonely or behind in life? Talk about it. (Steer: because blame feels like relief and belonging feels like rescue, and both are being sold at the exact moment someone is most likely to buy. The honest answer to loneliness is people, offline and real, not a worldview with a checkout page. Saying I have been feeling behind out loud to someone real is the strong version of what the pipeline offers a fake version of.)

**SLIDE (concept)** — *The off ramps*
Heading: Every point on the pipeline has an exit.
Body: Talk to someone outside the bubble: one conversation with a person who is not in the comment section breaks the echo, which is why the content works so hard to make outsiders sound like enemies. Curate the feed on purpose: unfollow, mute, not interested. The algorithm obeys attention, so point yours deliberately and the pipeline starves. Use the trusted adult route: if content has its hooks in you or in a mate, saying so out loud is the strong move, and you are not in trouble for anything a feed served you. And if an account is pushing hate or pushing people towards harm, report it on the platform and tell an adult at school. Recognise it, refuse it, report it. Nobody is in trouble for telling.

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
It is just jokes. Jokes cannot change what somebody believes. What is true?
· True, humour is harmless by definition (reaction: this is the belief the pipeline is built on. A joke gets an idea in the door without it being examined, and two hundred jokes later the idea is furniture. The drip feed starts funny on purpose.)
· Jokes are the entry ramp, which is why the question that matters is who profits from where the jokes are heading (reaction: exactly. Laughing at an edgy joke does not make you a bad person. Noticing the direction of travel makes you a hard target. ⭐)
· Anyone who laughs at edgy jokes is already radicalised (reaction: no, and this mistake helps the pipeline too, because it makes ordinary people feel accused and pushes them towards the crowd that says come here, we get you. The skill is watching direction, not policing laughter.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
A mate is deep in this content and getting angrier, especially about women. What actually helps?
· Mock him publicly until he stops (reaction: humiliation is the recruiter's best friend. It proves the script that says they all hate us, and it pushes him further in.)
· Stay a real friend, talk with him outside the bubble, ask the who profits question together, and back him talking to an adult if it runs deep (reaction: this is the strong move. Real belonging is the thing the pipeline fakes, and you are offering the original. Neither of you is in trouble for any of it. ⭐)
· Cut him off completely and immediately (reaction: isolation is the pipeline's food supply. Keep the door open. The person outside the bubble he still talks to might matter more than you know.)
