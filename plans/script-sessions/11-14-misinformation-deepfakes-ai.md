# Script session: Misinformation, deepfakes and AI content (11 to 14)

**Band:** 11 to 14 (parent primer short, child version leads)
**Module:** Misinformation, deepfakes and AI content (module 12, KS3, THE REFERENCE LESSON: spec section 10.2 carries the scripted beats and this session adapts them, never competes with them)
**Cast:** Zara leads (Y7 to Y8 truth register, canonical still e29b139c; the KS3 reference beats are already rendered: 459b1662 intro, 66e88fe5 concept, 129f9d14 mission), DiGi opens and closes, DiGi Junior pause (library bc3337b7)
**Single action outcome:** I can run three checks before I believe or share something.
**The three checks (locked wording, never reworded):**
1. Who made this, and how do they know?
2. What do other places say?
3. How is it trying to make me feel?

---

## Track P — the parent primer (about 60 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 10s)**
Your 13 year old shows you a video of a footballer saying something outrageous. It has millions of views and it is all over the group chats. It looks completely real. You genuinely cannot tell either.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 20s)**
That is the honest starting point: at this age nobody can reliably tell by looking, including you. So the family skill is not spotting fakes by eye, it is running checks by habit. Your teenager is not gullible for believing it. The content is engineered to get past exactly the reaction they had, because big feelings share fast. The good news is they learn the checks quickly, and they love catching what adults miss.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
Next shocking thing either of you sees, say: run the checks on it, loser makes the tea. Who made this, and how do they know. What do other places say. How is it trying to make me feel. Then send the lesson. At this age the child version leads, send it and they earn stars, and Zara drills the three checks properly. Within a week they will be running them on your feed.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Seen something you cannot verify? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1 (learning cycle 1: content can be manufactured)

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Manufactured is a spectrum*
Heading: True, edited, or made from nothing.
Body: Every post sits somewhere on a line. Some are true and well sourced. Some started true and got cropped, cut or captioned into something else. And some were made from nothing: a photo of an event that never happened, a voice saying words nobody said. The tools that make the third kind are free and fast, and the results do not look fake. So looking harder is not the skill. Checking is the skill.

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *Three posts, you vote*
Three posts fill the frame, one at a time. Vote on each before the reveal: true, edited, or made from nothing.
1. A local news post: flood warning issued for the county this weekend, named weather service quoted, link to the service.
2. A photo of a celebrity holding a sign with a political slogan, shared by a fan account, no other source anywhere.
3. A clip of a world leader announcing something enormous, posted by an account with a name one letter off the real broadcaster.
Reveals: 1 is true, source checkable in one tap. 2 is edited, the original sign said something else entirely. 3 is made from nothing, the giveaway was never in the pixels, it was in the account name. Reaction line: nobody in this class got all three by eye alone. That is the whole point of what comes next.

### SLIDES after Beat 2 (check for understanding, then guided practice)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *Which check fires first?*
A video makes you furious the moment you see it. Which check does that feeling trigger?
· Check 1: who made this, and how do they know? (tap to reveal: you will get there, but something fired before you even thought about the source.)
· Check 3: how is it trying to make me feel? (tap to reveal: exactly. The fury arrived before any facts did, and that is the deepfake tell. Feelings first is their strategy, so noticing the feeling is your first move. ⭐)
· No check, feelings are not evidence either way (tap to reveal: half right. They are not evidence, but they are your best alarm. Detectives use the alarm.)

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *Guided practice one: the clip*
A video lands in the chat: a famous streamer, face and voice perfect, announcing they are quitting and blaming a rival by name. Run the checks together. Who made this: an account created last month, not the streamer's channel. What do other places say: the streamer's real channel posted normally an hour ago, no news site has it. How is it trying to make you feel: outraged on your streamer's behalf, ready to pile on the rival. Three checks, three fails, under a minute.

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *Guided practice two: the screenshot chain*
A screenshot of a news headline says a chemical leak closed a town nearby, forwarded through four chats, everyone adding be careful!! Nobody can find the article. That is check 2 failing in plain sight: if it were real, every local outlet would carry it. A screenshot of a headline is not a source. It is a picture of a claim.

**SLIDE (interactive: verdict-sort)** — *The verdict wall*
Four items slide in one at a time. Sort each onto the wall: BELIEVE, PAUSE, or DO NOT SHARE.
1. Flood warning post from the named weather service, checkable link. (believe)
2. Celebrity sign photo, single fan account, no original anywhere. (do not share)
3. A mate says a Year 11 got expelled for something wild, no details. (pause)
4. Voice note that sounds exactly like your head teacher announcing a day off. (do not share, and notice which check caught it)
Reaction lines: correct sorts get Zara's "case cracked", wrong sorts get "look again, which of the three checks fails first?"

### SLIDES after Beat 3 (apply, then the quiz)

**SLIDE (discussion)** — *Talk it out*
Your friend shared the streamer clip before anyone knew it was fake. Two hundred people saw it because of them. Are they stupid? Talk about it. (Steer: no. The clip was built by people whose whole job is beating smart people's eyes. Kind, clever people share fakes because the feeling gets there first. That is why the checks are a habit, not an intelligence test.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
A voice message that sounds exactly like your friend asks you to send money for an emergency. It is urgent and it is scary. What is the detective move?
· Send it, you know their voice anywhere (reaction: voices can now be made from nothing, and urgent plus scary is check 3 ringing at full volume. Contact your friend another way first.)
· Run the checks: contact your friend on a different channel, and notice the panic is the point (reaction: case cracked. Check 1 on the real person, check 3 on the fear. Under a minute. ⭐)
· Ignore it, it is probably fine (reaction: do not ignore it either. If it is real your friend needs you. Verify another way, that is the move.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
True or false: if a photo is AI made, it is always a lie.
· True. AI made means fake, fake means lie (reaction: this is the trap question. Plenty of AI made images are labelled, artistic or harmless. The problem is never the tool, it is a claim pretending to be evidence. That is why we check instead of panic.)
· False. The point is checking, not paranoia (reaction: exactly right. AI made art with a label is fine. An AI made photo passed off as news fails check 1 the moment you ask who made it. Checks, not fear. ⭐)
· False, because AI images are easy to spot (reaction: right answer, wrong reason. They are not easy to spot, and they will get harder. That is why the checks never rely on your eyes.)
