# Script session: Choosing apps for under 5s (0 to 5)

**Band:** 0 to 5 (parent only, the child never watches)
**Module:** gap. Nothing in the 21 modules covers this band; this session helps define the new 0 to 5 parent only strand named in plans/parent-video-research-2026-07.md.
**Cast:** DiGi only (golden star). No child track at this band.
**Single action outcome for the parent:** I can test an app myself before my child gets it, using one question: what does my child actually do in this?

---

## Track P — the parent session (60 to 90 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 12s)**
It is late and you are in the app store typing games for two year olds. Every single result says educational. You download the one with five stars, and by breakfast your two year old is tapping through more adverts than game.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 30s)**
Here is the pathway view. Educational on an app store is marketing, not a measurement. Anyone can write it, and almost everyone does. The good news is you do not need a review site to judge an app for an under five. You need one question: what does my child actually do in this? A good app at this age works like a good picture book. Your child makes something happen, the app responds, nothing interrupts to sell them things, and it gets even better with you sitting in. If the honest answer to the question is watch, tap whatever flashes, and sit through adverts, then it is not education. It is television with extra steps and worse manners.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
One move tonight. Before the next download ever reaches your child, play it yourself for five minutes first. Ask the question while you play: what will my child actually do in here? If you like the answer, keep it and hand it over like this: I found us a new game. Show me how it works. If you do not like the answer, delete it, and nobody misses a thing. Five minutes of you first beats five stars every time.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Found an app and not sure about it? Tell me the name and we will look at it together.
