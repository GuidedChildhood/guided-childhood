# Script session: Calm bodies, screens and feelings (5 to 7)

**Band:** 5 to 7 (parent first, then co watch together)
**Module:** Kind screens, calm bodies (`ks1-02-kind-screens-calm-bodies`)
**Cast:** Sofia + DiGi Junior, DiGi opens and closes
**Single action outcome:** When my body gives me the uh oh feeling, I stop and tell my grown up. And if my body says nothing but something still seems wrong, I can still tell.

---

## Track P — the parent session (60 to 90 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 12s)**
Half an hour after the tablet goes away, your six year old is bouncing off the walls, or teary over nothing, and cannot tell you why. They are not being difficult. They genuinely do not know what their body is doing.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 25s)**
Here is the pathway view. At this age children feel screens in their bodies before they can name anything: a wobbly tummy, a racing heart, fizzy legs, a scrunched up face. That body signal is gold. A child who can notice it and say my tummy feels funny has an early warning system for everything, from a scary video to one game too many. Your job is not to watch every minute of screen time. It is to teach them that the body talks, and that telling you what it says is always welcome. One honest caveat to teach alongside it: sometimes the body stays quiet even when something is wrong, and they can still come to you anyway.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
When screens finish tonight, borrow these exact words: let's check your body. Is your tummy wobbly or still? Is your heart racing or slow? No right answer, just noticing together. Thirty seconds, done. Then watch the kids version together, Sofia teaches it and they earn stars.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Big feelings after screens at your house? Tell me what happens and we will work it out.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (concept)** — *The uh oh feeling*
Heading: Your body has a special signal.
Body: When something is too much, or scary, or just not right, your body sends you a signal. A wobbly tummy. A heart that goes fast like a drum. Legs that feel fizzy. A face that goes scrunchy. We call it the uh oh feeling. It is not naughty and it is not silly. It is your body being your friend and saying: something needs a grown up.

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *After the game*
You have been playing a really exciting game. It stops, and your heart is still going fast and your legs feel fizzy and you feel a bit cross at everyone. Point to where your body feels it. Ask your grown up: where does YOUR body feel it when you have had too much phone?

**SLIDE (keywords)** — *Body whisperer words*
wobbly tummy: your tummy's way of saying uh oh · racing heart: a heart going fast like a drum · calm body: still tummy, slow heart, soft shoulders · tell: saying what your body said, out loud, to your grown up

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *Your tummy goes wobbly*
A video comes on and your tummy goes wobbly. What do you do?
· Keep watching, wobbly tummies go away (tap to reveal: the video might not be right for you. Your tummy noticed first!)
· Feel it, stop, tell your grown up (tap to reveal: yes! That is the body whisperer move. Your tummy will say thank you. ⭐)
· Feel embarrassed and hide (tap to reveal: never hide a wobbly tummy. Grown ups get them too, ask yours!)

**SLIDE (interactive: star-breath)** — *The calm down star*
DiGi Junior's star grows and shrinks on the screen. Breathe with it, you and your grown up together. In as the star grows, one two three. Out as it shrinks, one two three. Three times. Feel your heart slow down like a sleepy drum. This is how you help a fizzy body go calm, and you can do it anywhere, any time, no screen needed.

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The quiet body*
Here is a tricky one, whisperers. Sometimes your body stays quiet. No wobbly tummy, no racing heart. But something on the screen still seems... wrong. Maybe you cannot even say why. Guess what: you can STILL tell your grown up. You do not need a wobbly tummy for a ticket. Seeming wrong is enough.

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
Your heart is racing and your tummy is wobbly after a video. What is your body saying?
· Nothing, bodies just do that (reaction: your body never talks for no reason. It is your friend!)
· Uh oh, something needs a grown up (reaction: perfect listening, body whisperer! Feel it, stop, tell. ⭐)
· That I am being naughty (reaction: feelings are never naughty. Not one single feeling, ever.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
Your body feels fine, but something on the screen still seems wrong. Can you tell your grown up?
· No, I need a wobbly tummy first (reaction: no ticket needed! Seeming wrong is always enough.)
· Yes, I can always tell, even with a quiet body (reaction: that is the biggest secret of all, and you got it. Stars incoming! ⭐)
· Only if someone else tells first (reaction: you never have to wait for anyone. Your telling counts all by itself.)
