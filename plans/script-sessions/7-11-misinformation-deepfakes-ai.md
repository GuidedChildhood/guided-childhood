# Script session: Misinformation, deepfakes and AI content (7 to 11)

**Band:** 7 to 11 (parent first, child version via Star Lesson)
**Module:** Misinformation, deepfakes and AI content, KS2 register of module 12 (`ks3-12` carries the KS3 build; this session is the KS2 voicing of the same three checks)
**Cast:** Zara leads (truth finder, squad register with capes), DiGi opens and closes, DiGi Junior pause
**Single action outcome:** I can run three checks before I believe or share something.
**The three checks (locked wording, never reworded):**
1. Who made this, and how do they know?
2. What do other places say?
3. How is it trying to make me feel?

---

## Track P — the parent session (60 to 90 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 12s)**
Your nine year old runs in holding the tablet. Everyone at school is sharing it. A famous YouTuber has done something shocking and your child believes every word. You have about ten seconds before they share it too.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 25s)**
Here is what that moment actually is. Your child is not being gullible. Sharing feels like belonging at this age, and the posts that spread fastest are built to cause big feelings, because big feelings share fast. So the answer is not that is rubbish, put it down. That closes the door. The answer is to treat it like a detective case you solve together. Children this age love being the one who catches the fake. Give them the tool and they will use it on everything, including the stuff you worry about.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
Next time they show you something shocking, borrow these exact words: Interesting. Who made this, and how do they know? Then look together. That is check one of three, and one check tonight is enough. When you are ready, send the kids version. DiGi teaches all three checks as a detective mission, and your child earns stars for cracking the case.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Questions about a post your child saw? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The post that started it*
A post appears in the feed frame: "BREAKING! Your favourite gamer is giving away 1,000 free skins to EVERYONE who shares this TODAY ONLY!" 4,203 shares and climbing. Question under the post: your friend just sent you this. What is your gut doing right now?

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Anyone can make anything*
Heading: Posts are made by people.
Body: Every post, picture and video was made by someone. Some people make true things. Some people make mistakes. And some people make things up on purpose, because shares make them money or make them feel powerful. A computer can now make a photo of something that never happened. So a detective never asks does it LOOK real. A detective asks better questions.

**SLIDE (keywords)** — *Detective words*
source: the person or place a post comes from · evidence: how they know it is true · fake: made up on purpose · share: sending it on, which makes YOU part of the story

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *Run check one*
The gamer giveaway post is back. Check one: who made this, and how do they know?
· A brand new account with no name and no other posts (tap to reveal: hmm. No name, no history, no way they know. Check one says PAUSE.)
· The gamer's real channel, the one with the tick (tap to reveal: a real source you can check. Detectives still run check two.)
· It does not matter, free skins are free skins (tap to reveal: that is exactly what the maker wants you to think. Feelings first, questions never.)

**SLIDE (interactive: verdict-sort)** — *The verdict wall*
Three posts slide in one at a time. Sort each onto the wall: BELIEVE, PAUSE, or DO NOT SHARE.
1. "School dinner menu changes on Monday, says the school website." (believe)
2. "A dog in Spain learned to drive a bus!! No source, wild photo." (pause)
3. "Share this in ten seconds or your account gets deleted tonight." (do not share)
Reaction lines: correct sorts get Zara's "case cracked!", wrong sorts get a gentle "look again, what would check one say?"

**SLIDE (diagram)** — *The detective flow*
One arrow path: SEE IT → FEEL IT (notice the big feeling!) → CHECK IT (the three checks) → then choose: believe, pause, or do not share. The feeling comes BEFORE the checking for everyone, even grown ups. Detectives notice the feeling and check anyway.

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *The screenshot chain*
A message lands in the class group chat: a screenshot that says SCHOOL CLOSED FRIDAY, TEACHER STRIKE, with send this to everyone underneath. It looks like a real news page. Your best friend already sent it to twelve people. What now?

**SLIDE (discussion)** — *Talk it out*
Your friend shared it and it turned out to be fake. Is your friend a bad person? Talk about it: why do kind, clever people share fake things? (Steer: because the feeling got there first. That is why we check, not because people are silly.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
The screenshot says the school is closed and everyone is sharing it. What is the FIRST thing a detective does?
· Share it fast, better safe than sorry (reaction: the fake is counting on exactly this. Not this one.)
· Run check one: who made this, and how do they know? (reaction: case cracked! The school website or a teacher would know. A screenshot knows nothing. ⭐)
· Delete the group chat (reaction: you do not have to leave, you just have to check.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
A post gives you a HUGE rush, excited, angry, scared, all at once. What does that big feeling mean?
· The post must be true, feelings do not lie (reaction: feelings are real, but they are not evidence. Look again.)
· Nothing, ignore feelings (reaction: never ignore them. They are your first clue.)
· Big feeling equals big clue: pause and run the checks, because fakes aim for feelings (reaction: that is the whole secret, detective. Stars incoming. ⭐)
