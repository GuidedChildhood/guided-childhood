# Script session: Being kind and safe with others online (7 to 11)

**Band:** 7 to 11 (parent first, child version via Star Lesson)
**Module:** Being kind and safe with others online
**Cast:** Sofia leads (safety guardian, squad register with capes and shields), Oliver in support (voices the struggle and takes the mission beat), DiGi opens and closes, DiGi Junior pause
**Single action outcome:** If someone is unkind online, I do not join in, I tell a grown up, and I send something kind to the friend.

---

## Track P — the parent session (60 to 90 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 12s)**
Your child comes off the tablet quiet. Not tired quiet. Small quiet. You ask what is up and get nothing, I am fine. Something on that screen just made your child shrink, and they have decided not to tell you.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 25s)**
At this age, unkindness online lives in group chats and game lobbies, and children stay silent about it for one very logical reason: they are scared of what happens when they tell. The device gets taken away, which feels like being punished for being hurt. Or a grown up storms into school and makes everything bigger. So your job tonight is not to fix anything. It is to become safe to tell. A child who knows that reporting a problem never costs them the tablet will bring you the problem every time. A child who fears the consequence will carry it alone. And one more thing worth saying out loud to them: if someone is unkind to you, that is never, ever your fault.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
Tonight, unprompted, borrow these exact words: if anything online ever makes you feel horrible, you can tell me, you will not be in trouble, and you will not lose your tablet. We will sort it out together. That is the whole move. Say it once and mean it. When you are ready, send the kids version. Sofia teaches the upstander move and your child earns stars for learning it.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Worried about something your child saw or received? Ask me any time.

### SLIDES after Beat 1

**SLIDE (concept)** — *What cyberbullying is*
Heading: Unkind, on purpose, again and again.
Body: Cyberbullying is being unkind to the same person on purpose, over and over, on a screen. Mean messages. Leaving someone out of the chat on purpose. Sharing an embarrassing picture of them. A pile of laughing faces aimed at one person. And here is the truth that matters more than anything else on this slide, so read it twice: if it ever happens to you, it is NEVER your fault. Not once. Not ever.

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *Oliver froze*
Oliver appears with his football. "Can I tell you something? In my football game a boy missed an open goal, and the whole lobby piled on him. Loser. Uninstall. Worse words too. And I froze. I did not type anything mean. But I did not do anything kind either, and I have thought about it ever since." Do you know that frozen feeling? What could Oliver have done?

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Bystander or upstander*
Heading: Watching is a choice too.
Body: A bystander sees it happen and scrolls past. An upstander does the move. Here is the good news: you never have to fight anyone, and you never have to type back at the mean kids. The upstander move is quieter than that, and much stronger. Sofia is about to hand it to you.

### SLIDES after Beat 2

**SLIDE (choice)** — *The pile on*
Everyone in the class chat is sending laughing faces at Maya's drawing. Adding one more feels tiny. What is the guardian move?
· Send one laughing face so you fit in (tap to reveal: to Maya it is not one, it is fifteen. Every face makes the pile heavier, and yours would be in it.)
· Send nothing mean, then message Maya: I love your drawing, ignore them (tap to reveal: the pile just got lighter for the first time all night. That is the move. ⭐)
· Leave the chat quietly (tap to reveal: not joining in is a real start, well done. Maya still feels alone though. Upstanders add the kind bit.)

**SLIDE (interactive: class-tally)** — *The kindness tally*
Three columns on a wall: DID NOT JOIN IN, TOLD A GROWN UP, SENT SOMETHING KIND. As a class, or with your grown up at home, tap to tally every upstander step anyone in the room has ever done or seen someone do. Each tap adds a small glowing shield to the wall. Reaction line: look at that wall. Every shield up there made a real day better for a real kid.

**SLIDE (concept)** — *Telling is strength*
Heading: Reporting is not snitching.
Body: Snitching is trying to get someone INTO trouble. Telling is getting someone OUT of trouble, maybe you, maybe a friend. Know your grown ups by name: a parent, your teacher, a club coach. And one guardian trick: you can keep what happened on the screen to show them. You do not have to remember it in words, and you do not have to carry it alone for even one more day.

### SLIDES after Beat 3

**SLIDE (scenario)** — *If it happens to you*
A message lands and your tummy drops. It is aimed at you. Before anything else, two things to know. One: this is not your fault, and it would not be your fault even if you had posted the silliest video in the world. Two: you are not alone for one minute longer than it takes to find your grown up. Deep breath. What do you do next?

**SLIDE (discussion)** — *Talk it out*
Why do you think kids sometimes keep it secret when someone is unkind to them online? Talk about it. (Steer: they worry the device will be taken away, or that telling makes it bigger. In this squad, telling is always the strong move and never the trouble move. Your grown up wants the problem, not the silence.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 1*
A boy gets mean messages after posting a silly dance video. Your friend says: well, he should not have posted it. Is your friend right?
· Yes, he started it by posting the video (reaction: this one fools a lot of people, so listen close. Posting a dance is not an invitation to be hurt. Unkindness is always the choice of the person being unkind. It is never the fault of the person it lands on.)
· No. It is never the fault of the person being picked on (reaction: shields up. That is the whole truth, guardian. ⭐)
· It is half his fault for dancing (reaction: zero of it is his fault. Kindness has no small print.)

**SLIDE (choice)** — *QUIZ, question 2*
You see a pile on in a game chat. The full upstander move is...
· Type something even meaner back at the bullies to defend your friend (reaction: kind heart, wrong tool. Fighting feeds the pile. The quiet move is stronger.)
· Do not join in, tell a grown up, send the friend something kind (reaction: three small steps, one superpower. Stars incoming. ⭐)
· Do nothing, it is not your business (reaction: a guardian's business is people. One kind message costs you nothing and changes their whole night.)
