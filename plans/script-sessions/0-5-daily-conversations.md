# Script session: Daily conversations that normalise talking about screens (0 to 5)

**Band:** 0 to 5 (parent only, the child never watches)
**Module:** gap. Nothing in the 21 modules covers this band; this session helps define the new 0 to 5 parent only strand named in plans/parent-video-research-2026-07.md.
**Cast:** DiGi only (golden star). No child track at this band.
**Single action outcome for the parent:** I can narrate my own phone use out loud, so screens become a normal thing we talk about long before they become a hard thing.

---

## Track P — the parent session (60 to 90 seconds)

**P-BEAT 1 — THE MOMENT (dialogue beat, 12s)**
You are pushing the buggy and your phone buzzes. You check it, answer it, put it away. Your two year old watched every second of that, and neither of you said a word. In most homes the phone is the one thing everybody uses and nobody mentions.

**P-BEAT 2 — THE REFRAME (concept beat, 30s)**
Here is the pathway view. Your child is already learning about screens every single day, just by watching you. The only question is whether the learning comes with words. Think about what families talk about constantly: food, sleep, teeth, crossing the road. Those become topics children bring back to you for the rest of childhood. Screens usually get silence, and silence teaches a lesson too. It says this is not something we talk about here. You do not need a big conversation at this age. You need tiny ones, so often they become boring. The structural move is narration: say out loud what the screen is doing while you use it. A child who hears easy screen talk at two is a child who brings you a screen problem at ten.

**P-BEAT 3 — TRY THIS TONIGHT (mission beat, 20s)**
One move. The next time you pick up your phone in front of your child, narrate it in one sentence. Borrow this: I am checking the map so we know the way home. Or: I am sending Grandma a photo of your tower. Then close it out loud: done, phone away. That is the whole thing. One sentence, said out loud, once tonight. Do it most days and talking about screens becomes as normal in your house as talking about dinner. At this age the lesson is always yours, not theirs.

**P-BEAT 4 — DIGI TAG (5s, from the DiGi library)**
DiGi star wave: Not sure what to say out loud? Tell me what you use your phone for most and we will find your sentence together.
