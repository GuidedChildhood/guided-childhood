# Digital literacy landscape — the field, and where we win

**Written 9 Jul 2026 · Every serious provider mapped, the frameworks we align to, and the gaps Guided Childhood can own · No dashes**

Justin's ask: research all the top providers of digital literacy so we know the field and can be genuinely best in class. Here it is, distilled to what changes our decisions. Full profiles and sources are in the research handoff.

## 1. The field, in one line each

- **Common Sense Education** (with Harvard Project Zero). The world standard, free, in 70 percent of US schools, dilemma based not rules. US framed, lesson led, weak behaviour change, AI catching up.
- **Google Be Internet Awesome and Interland** (UK: Be Internet Legends with Parent Zone). The rare playful one, a real game, 10 million UK pupils. Primary only, and its own RCT found knowledge up, behaviour flat.
- **Childnet** (SWGfL group). Trusted UK charity, strong peer led Digital Leaders. A resource library, not one joined up premium journey.
- **Internet Matters and Digital Matters.** The UK's biggest parent reach, funded by the ISPs, best home link and device setup guides. Broad but shallow per topic, school product newer.
- **UKCIS Education for a Connected World and ProjectEVOLVE (SWGfL).** The de facto national framework and free toolkit, complete 3 to 18 coverage, assess then teach. Utilitarian, text heavy, low delight, weak parent link, no characters.
- **National Online Safety and The National College.** Compliance and CPD giant, the viral one page parent guides. Trains adults, does not deliver an engaging pupil curriculum.
- **NetSmartz (NCMEC)** and **ThinkUKnow (CEOP, National Crime Agency).** The safeguarding authorities, character led for the young (Jessie and Friends). Narrow to abuse prevention, fear framed, episodic.
- **BBC Own It.** The one real point of need nudge, a keyboard that intervenes as a child types. A tool, not a pathway, and scaled back.
- **Jigsaw PSHE.** A genuine 3 to 16 spiral with mindfulness, statutory RSHE. Online safety is a thread inside PSHE, not a specialist deep dive, slide led.
- **Nearpod.** Best in class interactive delivery and teacher analytics, mostly carrying Common Sense content. A delivery layer, not a curriculum owner.
- **Natterhub (UK).** The closest immersive analogue, a social media like world for ages 5 to 11 with a badge economy. Primary only, 2D, not character and video led.
- **News Literacy Project (Checkology) and MediaWise.** The strongest dedicated misinformation and AI content players. US framed, skew older.
- **iDEA Award and Barefoot Computing.** A digital Duke of Edinburgh badge scheme, and free primary computing training. Neither is a joined up 4 to 16 online safety curriculum.

## 2. The frameworks we map to (so schools get compliance for free)

- **UKCIS Education for a Connected World**, the eight strands (self image and identity, online relationships, online reputation, online bullying, managing online information, health wellbeing and lifestyle, privacy and security, copyright and ownership). The UK reference everyone maps to. Our curriculum matrix already uses these plus AI.
- **RSHE statutory guidance.** Compulsory. The revised guidance (July 2025) becomes compulsory 1 September 2026 and, crucially, explicitly names AI generated deepfakes, harmful influencers, image based abuse and online pornography. That is our timing.
- **Computing national curriculum** and **Keeping Children Safe in Education** are the other statutory homes for online safety.
- **UNESCO AI Competency Framework for Students** (Sept 2024), understand, apply, create, is the natural spine for a first class AI strand.

The move: map every lesson to the eight ECW strands and the RSHE duties as data, so a school clears its legal floor and can evidence it automatically. This is already the direction in the schools spec.

## 3. The gaps nobody owns (our openings)

1. **Knowledge to behaviour is the whole field's failure.** The best independent evidence (the Be Internet Awesome RCT, the University of New Hampshire evaluations) shows these schemes raise knowledge but barely change behaviour. Almost nobody designs for behaviour, or measures it.
2. **No true 4 to 16 pathway that is also delightful.** The immersive products stop at primary (Interland, Natterhub). The full age span products are not immersive (Jigsaw, ECW). Nobody owns both.
3. **AI is bolted on.** AI and deepfakes are afterthoughts on old citizenship frameworks, right as the 2025 RSHE update makes them compulsory.
4. **The home to school link is broken.** Curricula rarely reach the home; the parent players rarely connect to what the child learned this week. No shared thread from classroom to kitchen table.
5. **Teacher burden versus pupil delight is an either or.** The immersive options are heavy to prepare; the low prep options are adult training, not pupil learning.
6. **Fear framing over flourishing.** The safeguarding led players lead with abuse and fear, which limits engagement and behaviour.

## 4. Where Guided Childhood wins (the positioning)

1. **Design for behaviour change and measure it.** Habit loops, point of need nudges (the smart alerts layer, the daily practice, the quest games), and simple before and after behaviour measures per strand. Own the honest claim no incumbent holds: the one that actually changes what children do.
2. **One continuous, character led 4 to 16 world.** The full span as a single spiral where the cast grows with the child, mapped rigorously to ECW and RSHE so schools get compliance for free. The current cast is Sofia, Zara, Oliver and DiGi the star, with Vix and Brock. Nobody combines the full age span with genuine narrative delight.
3. **AI and misinformation as a first class strand.** A proper AI and deepfake strand mapped to the UNESCO framework and the 2025 RSHE named harms. Being current in 2026 beats a decade of incumbency here.
4. **A real home to school thread.** Each week the classroom lesson surfaces a matched parent moment: what your child learned, the one conversation, the one setting to check. We can own both ends, the reach and the thread.
5. **Low teacher burden, high pupil immersion.** Ready to play video plus interactive sessions from the database driven player, auto assessment and DSL evidence generated. Beat ProjectEVOLVE on effort and Nearpod on story.
6. **Lead with flourishing, not fear.** Calibrated pathways, never allow or deny, warm and direct. Confident kind capable citizens, not frightened ones. Matches both the evidence and the brand.
7. **Premium production as the moat.** Character led video, GSAP motion, Checker tokens and real craft. The whole field is out produced by the apps children use for fun, and public sector and charity content structurally cannot match this.
8. **Instrument for evidence and word of mouth.** A behaviour and coverage dashboard mapped to ECW and RSHE for schools, visible progress for parents. Rigorous evidence of behaviour change is the one credential missing across the entire market, and it is also the strongest retention asset.

## 5. What this confirms about the build order

- The **explainer video plus character slides plus task** structure (lesson-structure-plan) is exactly the immersion the field lacks. Keep going.
- The **AI and deepfake strand** should be deepened first, because it is both a legal requirement from Sept 2026 and the field's biggest open gap.
- The **home to school thread** (smart alerts already surfaces the parent side; the schools product surfaces the classroom side) is a genuine wedge, worth wiring explicitly.
- The **behaviour measures** are worth designing in now, per strand, so we can one day publish the evidence no competitor has.
