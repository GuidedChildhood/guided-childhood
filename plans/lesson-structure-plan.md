# Lesson structure — the best digital pathway, 4 to 16

**Written 9 Jul 2026 · The evidence based anatomy for every lesson · Builds on lesson-format.md, lessons-depth-plan.md and lesson-explainer-sample.md · No dashes**

Justin's ask: the best digital structure for a lesson from co watching at 4 to independence at 16, with a 5 minute explainer video before the slides we already have, and slides made more alive with characters. This plan sets it, grounded in the pedagogy research (Rosenshine, Mayer, Piaget, Vygotsky, Hirsh-Pasek, the EEF, and the character science). Sources are listed at the end.

## 1. The one idea that organises everything

The whole curriculum is **one spine, and a slowly removed scaffold**. Vygotsky's zone of proximal development says learning happens in the gap between what a child can do alone and what they can do with a guide. So the pathway is a staged handover: at 4 the parent is the scaffold, by 16 the young person is their own. Every design choice below is a dial on that one idea.

## 2. Five delivery tiers, by how children actually develop

The spine in section 3 never changes. What changes per tier is concreteness, segment length, how much the adult carries, and how much the child does alone.

- **Foundation, 4 to 7 (co watched).** Piaget preoperational: intuitive, perception led, cannot yet reason about the hidden or the future. This tier is **joint media engagement**, designed for two viewers, never solo. A visible parent prompt in every lesson. Segments 2 to 4 minutes. The task is a shared conversation or action, not a solo quiz. Design for the lap, not the login.
- **Builder, 8 to 10.** Concrete operational: logical about real things, understands rules and cause and effect, abstract systems still need a physical analogy. Teaching move: make the invisible concrete, give the example first, then the label. Segments 4 to 6 minutes.
- **Explorer, 11 to 13.** Threshold of abstract reasoning, and the start of peer driven social pressure. Teaching move: a portable strategy for the heated moment, not just a fact for the calm one. Rehearse the pause. Segments up to 5 minutes, scenario led.
- **Shaper, 13 to 15.** Abstract reasoning established, identity is the task. Understanding a manipulation is not the same as being immune to it. Teaching move: pull back the curtain, hand them the analyst's seat, teach the business model and let them judge it. Respect is the pedagogy.
- **Independent, 16 plus.** Mature reasoning, the limiter is habit not capacity. Teaching move: move from teaching to coaching, they plan, monitor and evaluate, and they can mentor the younger ones. The final scaffold to remove is us.

What changes across the tiers: the adult moves from co viewer to guide to background coach; content moves from concrete rule to example plus label to abstract system to self authored judgement; the task moves from shared action to guided practice to analysis to project and mentoring.

## 3. The lesson spine (one anatomy, flexed by age)

Seven beats, each earning its place from a named framework.

1. **Hook.** A real scene from the child's world that raises the lesson's question. Makes it meaningful first (the four pillars).
2. **Retrieval.** A quick low stakes pull from memory of the last lesson (Rosenshine daily review, the testing effect). Warms the schema so new material has somewhere to attach.
3. **Model, the explainer video.** The idea taught in small steps, narration and matching visuals together, segmented, stripped of decoration (Mayer, cognitive load, worked examples). This is the new 5 minute layer, section 4.
4. **Guided practice, the slides with characters.** The child applies the idea to a new case with prompts, choices and immediate feedback at a high success rate (Rosenshine guided practice). The character makes it social, the choices make it active.
5. **Check.** A short honest check with feedback that corrects gently and never just says wrong (Rosenshine checking for understanding). Retrieval, not a grade.
6. **Independent task.** The child applies it to their own life, sized to their age. Where learning becomes behaviour.
7. **Plenary and forward hook.** A recap in the child's own words (metacognition), one takeaway strategy named, a hook to next time, seeding the next spaced retrieval.

The dial per age is concreteness, segment length, and how much scaffold the adult versus the child provides. For Foundation, use the shorter form and a parent prompt at the close. The full age flex table is in the research and lesson-format.md.

## 4. The 5 minute explainer video, to the science

The explainer is the model beat. Its whole job is to teach one idea at low cognitive load. The rules that matter most, from Mayer:

- **Modality.** Words as narration while the eyes watch the visual, never as on screen sentences to read. The single biggest lever.
- **Redundancy, the killer mistake.** Never put the full narration on screen and read it word for word. On screen text is a few labels only.
- **Coherence.** Cut everything that does not teach the point. No decorative character, no competing music, no fun asides. Seductive detail lowers learning.
- **Signalling.** Cue the eye to what matters now, a highlight, an arrow, the character pointing.
- **Segmenting.** Short beats, one idea each, child paced where possible.
- **Pre training.** Name the key character and the one or two key words before the explanation.
- **Personalisation and voice.** Warm, conversational, second person, in a real human voice.
- **Contiguity and dual coding.** Say the words at the moment the matching picture is on screen. Every key idea arrives as word and image together.

**Length by age**: Foundation 2 to 3 minutes with a parent pause point, Builder 3 to 4, Explorer and Shaper up to 5, Independent 5 or a little more. The number to hold is one idea per video.

**The reusable script template** (seven beats: warm open and question, pre training, the idea in small steps, wrong then right, name the takeaway strategy, your turn is coming, warm close and forward hook) is written out with timings and per beat illustration notes in the research, and a full worked example is in **lesson-explainer-sample.md**. Each beat names what its illustration must show, so the Higgsfield pipeline consumes the script directly, one visual idea per beat, narration and image always matched.

## 5. Characters, our cast, teaching not decorating

The current cast is **Sofia** (the protector), **Zara** (the curious one who sees through tricks), **Oliver** (the one who thinks ahead), **DiGi** (the star, the guide), with **Vix** and **Brock** as secondary. The evidence on pedagogical agents (the persona effect, Mayer embodiment and personalisation, parasocial bonds, the Sesame Street lesson) gives clear rules:

1. **Recurring, not disposable.** A small fixed cast the child meets again and again, so a real bond forms. Consistency is the asset.
2. **The character carries the teaching.** It does the explaining, points at what matters, models the strategy. It is the worked example, not a spectator in the corner.
3. **Socially responsive, always.** Gesture, look at the thing discussed, react to the child's choices. A frozen mascot adds nothing.
4. **Warm, conversational, second person, real voice.**
5. **One or two on screen at a time.** Introduce any new character in the pre training beat.
6. **Emotionally consistent and trustworthy.** The bond that drives learning is built on the character being reliably kind and the same each time.
7. **Charm serves the point.** Personality yes, antics that do not teach no.
8. **Fade the hand holding as the child ages.** Warm friend beside the parent at Foundation, peer or coach voice by 16.

The one test for any character moment: is the character teaching the idea right now, or just present. Teaching, keep it. Present, cut it. This is the brief for the character beats in the animation system plan, and it maps DiGi as the star to the guide role, matched by stage.

## 6. What this means for the build

- **Add the explainer layer.** A lesson gains an explainer video slot (a field for the video, plus the beat by beat script and its illustration prompts as data). The slide player plays the explainer first, then the deck. This is the change that turns the existing slides into full lessons.
- **Make the slides alive.** The guided practice slides gain character beats per the rules in section 5 and the animation system plan, socially responsive, teaching, faded by age.
- **The age dial.** One lesson template, five renderings, the dial set by stage on segment length, concreteness and scaffold, exactly as section 2 and 3 describe. Same write once, sell twice as the schools spec.
- **This feeds the fuller school curriculum.** Once the home lesson format is proven and JP is happy with the content, the same spine and explainers become the fuller school lessons and curriculum, which is the strongest part of the guided pathway.
