# Pathway Page Research Briefing

Prepared for the Guided Childhood Pathway redesign. Two research buckets, then 12 concrete recommendations for our page.

---

## Bucket A: Rival and adjacent products

### Monitoring and control apps

**Bark** — Onboarding is the benchmark: add child, connect devices, pick categories, done in 5 to 15 minutes. Dashboard nothing more than two clicks deep. Alerts arrive with context, not raw logs. Framing is "we do not read everything, we surface what matters."
- Borrow: the two clicks to anything rule, context wrapped events instead of raw feeds.
- Wrong: it is a monitoring feed, not a journey. No sense of a parent progressing or a child growing up. Never graduates.

**Qustodio** — Most intuitive of the deep control tools, widest device set, good per child schedules.
- Borrow: explicit multi device coverage as a visible grid, per child rules not one global setting.
- Wrong: setup is per device drudgery with no through line.

**Aura** — Wins on simplicity, one clean daily limit, whole family wellness not spying.
- Borrow: clean simple default first, depth on request (progressive disclosure).
- Wrong: too shallow for older kids, no education layer.

**Canopy** — Strong filtering but notorious install friction. Cautionary tale: if the first milestone is painful, the journey dies at step one.

**Gabb and Pinwheel** — Closest to us philosophically. Gabb's "Tech in Steps" is the best idea in the set: technology introduced in stages aligned to development, a gradual journey not a switch. Pinwheel: device modes by time of day, a curated app set that grows with the child.
- Borrow: the developmental staircase (Gabb), the right setup changes as the child ages (Pinwheel). Exactly our 4 to 16 arc.
- Wrong: both locked to their own hardware. We are device agnostic, our advantage.

**Aro** — Most relationship first in the category. Refuses to block, builds the habit of putting the phone down, rewards presence with gentle nudges. Anti surveillance.
- Borrow: reward the positive (presence, a battle solved) not punish the negative. Gentle reminders, not alarms.
- Wrong: no education content, a single habit not a curriculum.

**MMGuardian, Bright Canary, Cyber Dive, Bosco, Jiminy** — Variations on AI monitoring. They compete on how much they can see. None frame the parent as a learner or the child as growing toward independence. That flank is open to us.

### Non-profit and platform tools

**Internet Matters (UK)** — Best structural idea in the UK space: a layered model, network first (broadband level across all devices), then device controls, then in app safety on the specific apps and games. Age tailored, and they say controls should loosen over time toward self regulation.
- Borrow directly: network then device then app layering as the structure of our first milestone, and controls loosen as they grow baked into the age arc.
- Wrong: a static library of guides. No memory, no progress, no personalisation. We wrap their content model inside an actual journey.

**Common Sense Media** — Trusted age based reviews, strong credibility, weak as a journey (a lookup tool).

**Google Family Link and Apple Screen Time** — The tools our parents already have and hate. Apple: limits silently vanish, requests never arrive, iPad setup painful, Apple only. Google: does not work on iPhone, so mixed device homes are stuck, framed purely as taking time away, creates conflict.
- The opportunity: nobody helps parents across a mixed device household (kid on Android, parent on iPhone, plus smart TV, tablet, console). That fragmentation is the gap our first milestone fills. We are the connective layer they wish existed.

**Category wide gap:** every one treats screen time as something to subtract. None frames it as a childhood you are building. None graduates the child toward independence. None combines setup, education and real moments in one place. That combination is our whole thesis.

## Bucket B: Journey and habit-app patterns

**Duolingo path** — Replaced a confusing menu with a single winding path, one node at a time, because of choice paralysis. Nodes unlock in order, progress always forward, review reframed as forward motion.
- Steal: single path no choice paralysis, one clear next node, revisited work as forward progress.
- Avoid: streak guilt and punishing loss mechanics. For a parenting product, guilt is toxic to the relationship we sell.

**Finch** — Compassionate gamification. Care for a bird that grows as you complete real self care. Never pressures or punishes a missed day, encourages a return.
- Steal: growth as care metaphor, no punishment for lapses, warm re entry after absence. The exact register for parents who feel judged about screens.

**Headspace** — Onboarding quiz personalises a path and doubled course starts. Hard lesson: front door design boosted starts but did not increase actual meditation days. Real habit forms across the whole experience, not just onboarding.
- Steal: light personalisation quiz. Warning: a beautiful first milestone is not enough, the ongoing moments loop creates the habit.

**Onboarding checklist research** — Checklists with a visible progress bar hit about 67% completion versus 35% average. Keep it short, allow skipping, fill empty states with help, progressive disclosure, personalise to reduce time to value.
- Steal: the first milestone is a checklist with a real progress meter, personalised to the child's age and actual devices, skippable, helpful empty states.

**What makes a journey motivating not a nag list:** one obvious next step, visible progress toward a named destination, wins framed as growth not compliance, warm re entry after a lapse. A nag list is open tasks with no arc and a scolding tone.

## Recommendations for our Pathway page

1. **One spine, three strands, one destination.** A single vertical path from age 4 to 16. Named emotional destination like "a young person who can handle a phone without you." The three strands (device setup, lessons, moments) woven as node types on one spine, not three tabs. Kills choice paralysis.

2. **Make the 4 to 16 arc a visible climb with our own stage names.** Gabb's tech in steps in our voice. Life stages (First Screens, Own Tablet, First Phone, Social Years, Nearly Independent) with a soft glow on the current one. Path ahead visible but faded. Top node: "The goal is not control. It is a kid who does not need it."

3. **First milestone as a device coverage board, not a list.** Every device a tile: phone, tablet, smart TV, YouTube, console. Three states: not set, set, needs a look again. A ring around the board fills as tiles complete. Bark's two clicks plus the checklist progress meter.

4. **Structure setup in Internet Matters layers.** Network, then device, then apps. One broadband step protects everything and gives an instant early win before the fiddly per device work. Faster time to value.

5. **Own the mixed device household as our wedge.** "Kid on Android, you on iPhone, TV in the lounge, console upstairs." Copy: "One place for every screen in your house. Even the ones that do not talk to each other."

6. **Recommend, never dictate.** Each tile offers an age appropriate recommended setup applied with guidance, framed as "what most families do at this age." Keeps DiGi calibrated, never allow or deny.

7. **Gate lessons behind moments with a key metaphor, not a lock.** The moment is the key that unlocks the next lesson. "You just handled phones at dinner. That unlocked the next lesson: why the dinner table matters." The moment earns the lesson, the lesson is the reward.

8. **Always keep one strand open so nobody is ever blocked.** No live moment, the lesson track stays open on its own cadence, and vice versa. Never a hard stop.

9. **Log moments as growth, not incidents.** A logged battle lights a node. Solving it is a bloom, "your family got better at this," never "problem closed."

10. **Handle recurrence with warmth, never a broken streak.** A solved moment that returns is "this one came back, which is normal." Show history: "third time, and each time it lasted longer before returning." Offer revisit the lesson or adjust the device setting. Copy: "Back again? That is how habits work. Here is what helped last time."

11. **Tie the strands together with cross links.** A recurring moment suggests the device setting that eases it and the lesson that explains it. A completed lesson nudges the device tile it relates to. No competitor does this. Turns a front door win into an ongoing habit.

12. **Reward positive behaviour, and let the pathway graduate.** Celebrate presence and solved battles, not screen minutes. Let the journey visibly end near 16, controls loosen, the path resolves into "they can handle this now." Final node: "You are nearly done here. That was always the plan."

### Tone guardrails
- Education and relationship first. Never monitor, track, catch, block. Prefer set up, work through, get better at, handle.
- No punishment mechanics. No broken streaks, no guilt, no red alarm feeds.
- One clear next step visible at all times, everything else faded not gone.
- No dashes anywhere in copy.

### Data we already have to build on
- device_setup_progress + device_guides: the device ticks for the coverage board.
- concerns ledger: the moments, with status and recurrence already modelled.
- lessons + lesson_completions: the ordered track.
The redesign is mostly assembly and visualisation, not new schema.
