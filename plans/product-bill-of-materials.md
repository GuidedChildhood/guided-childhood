# What we need to produce — the complete bill of materials
**7 Jul 2026 · The comprehensiveness bar set by Jigsaw's portal, White Rose's workbooks and PD structure, and the school readiness research. Status: ✓ built · ◐ partial · ○ to build.**

## What the exemplars taught us

**Jigsaw's portal** (JP screenshots, 7 Jul): comprehensive but messy and informal. It carries: a Knowledge Hub, RSHE 2025 compliance messaging with mapping documents, webinars per audience (primary, secondary, governors) with downloadable slides, policy review guidance, FAQs, governors checklist, Puzzle Maps (unit overview tables: pieces, learning intentions, resources), Calm Me scripts, practitioner prep sheets, pupil Knowledge Organisers, vocabulary lists, home learning, class learning journals. Their end of unit workbook ties every question to numbered "I can" assessment criteria (NCFE accreditable), uses a scenario picture with probing questions, a Three Two One reflection, and an evidence portfolio page.

**White Rose** (workbook sample + site): one pupil workbook per block with a name and class cover, contents page, one skill per spread, numbered questions in filled circles, margin icons for talk and draw tasks, character speech bubbles posing questions, and clean progression. The site organises everything by phase and by ROLE (teachers, school leaders, teaching assistants), sells printed workbooks alongside digital, and runs professional development and a curriculum review service.

**The lesson:** comprehensiveness wins trust, but Jigsaw proves comprehensiveness without structure reads as clutter. Our portal stays clean by organising everything into seven layers, each reachable in one click from the dashboard.
