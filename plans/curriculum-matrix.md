# The curriculum matrix — 9 strands by 5 stages

**Source of truth:** migration 018 seeds every topic below as a stub in both audiences (parent/home educator and teacher). A topic goes live when its slides are written (Days 4 and 5) and its classroom video is rendered (Day 6).

Benchmarks: the eight UKCIS Education for a Connected World strands plus an AI safety strand. Statutory RSHE and Computing tags are added per lesson as slides are written.

## Self image and identity
How children see themselves is shaped by what screens reflect back. This strand keeps the real self in charge of the online one.

- **Foundation (4 to 7)**: Me on a screen and me in real life. Knowing the difference between a picture of a thing and the thing itself.
- **Builder (8 to 10)**: Your first avatar. Choosing what we show about ourselves in games and apps, and what we keep back.
- **Explorer (11 to 13)**: Filters are not faces. Seeing how edited images work so they stop setting the standard for real ones.
- **Shaper (13 to 15)**: Who are you online?. Running different accounts while staying one honest person.
- **Independent (16 plus)**: Your identity is yours to write. Presenting yourself for adult life, from group chats to first job applications.

## Online relationships
Connection is the strongest protective factor in all the research. This strand teaches warmth, boundaries and judgement with people through screens.

- **Foundation (4 to 7)**: Kind words on screens. Speaking to people through a screen the way you would face to face.
- **Builder (8 to 10)**: Friends you know and friends you do not. Telling the difference between a real friend online and a stranger who plays one.
- **Explorer (11 to 13)**: Group chats without the drama. Muting, leaving and standing up for someone kindly.
- **Shaper (13 to 15)**: Relationships, pressure and phones. Recognising pressure in private messages and knowing consent applies online.
- **Independent (16 plus)**: Meeting people from the internet. Moving an online connection into real life safely, like an adult.

## Online reputation
What goes online stays reachable. This strand builds the habit of thinking in years, not minutes.

- **Foundation (4 to 7)**: The internet remembers. Understanding that what goes on a screen can stay there.
- **Builder (8 to 10)**: Think before you post. The five second check before anything is sent or shared.
- **Explorer (11 to 13)**: Your digital footprint is already real. Searching yourself and understanding what is already out there.
- **Shaper (13 to 15)**: Reputation and the long game. Reading your own posts the way a sixth form, a coach or an employer will.
- **Independent (16 plus)**: Owning your online record. Auditing, cleaning up and deliberately building the record you want found.

## Online bullying
Every child will see unkindness online. This strand makes sure they know what to do, whether it lands on them or someone else.

- **Foundation (4 to 7)**: When screens make you sad. Telling a grown up every time something on a screen feels bad.
- **Builder (8 to 10)**: Mean messages. What to do, what not to do, and who to tell when messages turn unkind.
- **Explorer (11 to 13)**: Bystander or upstander. Breaking a group chat pile on instead of feeding it.
- **Shaper (13 to 15)**: Screenshots, shame and standing firm. Surviving public embarrassment online and helping someone else survive theirs.
- **Independent (16 plus)**: Calling it out safely. Supporting a target without becoming one, and knowing when to report instead.

## Managing online information
Feeds are built to hold attention, not to tell the truth. This strand teaches how information really reaches us and how to check it.

- **Foundation (4 to 7)**: Real or pretend?. Spotting the difference between stories, adverts and true things on a screen.
- **Builder (8 to 10)**: Adverts are everywhere. Noticing when something on a screen is trying to sell you something.
- **Explorer (11 to 13)**: How the algorithm decides what your child sees. LIVE, exemplar lesson with video.
- **Shaper (13 to 15)**: Deepfakes and doctored truth. Verifying before believing or sharing, because seeing is no longer proof.
- **Independent (16 plus)**: Your news diet. Building an information diet you chose on purpose instead of one chosen for you.

## Health, wellbeing and lifestyle
Sleep, mood and movement are where screens bite first. This strand builds routines that protect all three without a daily fight.

- **Foundation (4 to 7)**: Screens, sleep and growing bodies. Why screens live outside the bedroom from the very start.
- **Builder (8 to 10)**: The cool down lap. Ending screen time without a meltdown, every time.
- **Explorer (11 to 13)**: Mood and the scroll. Noticing what the feed does to how you feel, and acting on it.
- **Shaper (13 to 15)**: Sleep is not optional. Keeping nine hours a night when the phone wants them all.
- **Independent (16 plus)**: Designing your own digital life. Choosing habits on purpose before university and work choose them for you.

## Privacy and security
Personal information is valuable and children give it away without knowing. This strand builds the privacy shield early and keeps it growing.

- **Foundation (4 to 7)**: My privacy shield. Knowing what we never tell a screen: name, school, address, passwords.
- **Builder (8 to 10)**: Passwords are secrets. Making strong passwords and understanding why they are never shared.
- **Explorer (11 to 13)**: Location, cameras and microphones. Knowing what your phone shares without asking and turning it off.
- **Shaper (13 to 15)**: Scams aimed at teenagers. Recognising sextortion, fake offers and pressure tactics, and what to do next.
- **Independent (16 plus)**: Your data, your accounts, your money. Securing an adult digital life before it holds anything worth stealing.

## Copyright and ownership
Everything online was made by someone. This strand teaches respect for other peoples work and pride in your own.

- **Foundation (4 to 7)**: Someone made that. Understanding that pictures, songs and games belong to the people who made them.
- **Builder (8 to 10)**: Copying and creating. Using other peoples work fairly and starting to make your own.
- **Explorer (11 to 13)**: Remix culture. Memes, edits and giving credit where credit is due.
- **Shaper (13 to 15)**: Your work has value. Protecting what you make and post, because it is worth something.
- **Independent (16 plus)**: Creating and getting paid. Ownership, licensing and earning from what you make online.

## AI safety and literacy
AI will be the water this generation swims in. This strand teaches what it is, what it is not, and how to use it to grow rather than shrink.

- **Foundation (4 to 7)**: Some voices are not people. Knowing that assistants and characters are tools, not friends.
- **Builder (8 to 10)**: What is a robot brain?. Understanding that AI learns from examples, and gets things wrong.
- **Explorer (11 to 13)**: AI companions and real friends. Feeling the pull of an AI that always agrees, and keeping it in its place.
- **Shaper (13 to 15)**: Using AI to learn, not to skip learning. Homework, honesty and using AI to get stronger instead of weaker.
- **Independent (16 plus)**: Building with AI. Moving from user to maker: prompting, judgement and the changing world of work.
