# Age 9: The Puppy You Accidentally Train

## A short story

Maya got a puppy called Biscuit.

One day Biscuit barked at the door. Maya laughed and gave him a treat.

The next day, Biscuit barked at the door again. And again. And again.

Maya was confused. Why did Biscuit keep barking?

Then she worked it out. She had trained him. Every bark got a laugh and a treat. So Biscuit did it more.

She never meant to. But the treats taught him.

Your feed is a bit like Biscuit. You train it without meaning to.

## What is really happening

When you open an app, it shows you a feed: a long list of videos, one after another.

That feed is not random. It is chosen for you. The app made it just for you, and a different one for your friend.

So how does it choose? It watches what you do. The things it watches are called **signals**.

The biggest signal is simple: **how long you watch.**

You do not have to like, comment or follow. Just watching a video to the end is a treat for the app. It thinks, "More of this, please."

That is the trick with Biscuit too. You think a treat is only a treat if you mean it. But the puppy takes every treat the same way.

So if you stop on a video you did not even mean to watch, the app counts it. You just handed out a treat.

This is called a **feedback loop**. What you do changes what you see next. Then what you see next changes what you do. Round and round.

## Try this

**1. Spot your treats.** For one day, notice every time you stop and watch something to the end. That was a treat. Did you mean to give it?

**2. Train on purpose.** Pick something good you actually want more of, like football skills or art. Watch a few of those right through. Search for them. Watch your feed change over a few days. You can train Biscuit on purpose, not just by accident.

**3. The two minute pause.** When you feel stuck scrolling, stop and ask, "Am I enjoying this, or am I just barking at the door?" Then choose.

## You are in charge

Here is the thing about training a puppy. You are the trainer.

Biscuit only learned to bark because someone kept rewarding it. That means you can teach better habits too.

You can give your treats on purpose. Watch what helps you. Skip what makes you feel flat. Search for the good stuff. Stop when you have had enough.

The app learns from you. So the smartest move is to teach it well. You hold the treats.
