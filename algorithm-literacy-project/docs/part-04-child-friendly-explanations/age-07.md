# Age 7: The Robot Waiter

## A little story

Imagine a cafe with a robot waiter.

You sit down. The robot brings you a snack.

You smile and eat it all up. Yum.

So the robot brings you another one like it.

You frown at a different snack. You push it away.

So the robot stops bringing that one.

The robot is watching you. It wants to bring snacks you like.

Your apps are a bit like that robot waiter.

## What this means

When you open an app, it shows you videos and pictures.

It did not pick them by accident. It picked them for you.

How does it know what you like?

It watches you. It does not have eyes. But it can tell when you keep watching.

If you watch a video for a long time, the app thinks, "Oh good, they like this one."

So it brings you more like it.

If you swipe it away fast, the app thinks, "Not that one."

So it brings less of that.

You are training the robot. Even when you do not mean to.

## Try this

**1. The smile test.** Watch your face next time you use a screen. Are you smiling? Or are you just stuck there? Snacks you like and snacks you cannot stop eating are not the same.

**2. Say it out loud.** When a new video starts, say, "The app picked this one for me." It feels strange. But it is true.

**3. The on purpose test.** Pick one thing you love. Maybe dogs. Watch three dog videos all the way. Then look. Did more dogs come? You taught the robot.

## You are in charge

Here is the best part.

The robot waiter works for you.

If you keep watching things that make you feel good, it brings more of those.

If you swipe away the ones that make you feel yucky, it learns.

You can put your tablet down whenever you want. The robot has to stop.

You are the boss of the robot. Not the other way round.
