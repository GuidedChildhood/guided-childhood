Device Readiness Checklist

Stage 1 · Ages 4 to 7 Foundation · First device: shared screen only
Before they have their own device

Build the habits now. No solo device yet. Screens are something your family does together.

Set up now
Say this week
Watch for
Normal at this age: Excitement about screen time, preferring screens to other activities sometimes, some upset when a device is taken away. These are not warning signs on their own.
Stage 2 · Ages 8 to 10 Builder · First device: restricted phone or tablet
Their first device. Your most important rules.

This is when bedroom access and sleep become the critical variables. Set the rules before they have a personal phone, not after.

Set up now
Say this week
Watch for
Normal at this age: Wanting more screen time than allowed, being frustrated by rules, preferring YouTube to books. These are typical, not signals.
Stage 3 · Ages 11 to 13 Explorer · First device: guided smartphone, no social media yet Critical window
The highest-risk window. The most important conversations.

Cambridge MRC research identifies 11 to 13 as specifically elevated risk. Identity is forming through comparison. The algorithm amplifies vulnerability. Your presence is the strongest protective factor.

Set up now
Say this week
Watch for
Normal at this age: Wanting more privacy, caring a lot about what peers think, spending significant time online. The concern is pattern and intensity, not the behaviour itself.
Stage 4 · Ages 13 to 15 Shaper · First device: monitored social media, guided access
Keep the door open. Before anything goes wrong.

A teenager who fears losing their phone is a teenager who hides problems. The goal now is trust, not control.

Set up now
Say this week
Watch for
Normal at this age: Valuing peer approval over parent approval, wanting significant privacy, being on their phone a lot. The concern is disconnection from reality or from you, not from devices.
Stage 5 · Ages 16 and above Independent · Full access, trust-based
Hand over the wheel. Keep the door open.

The goal at this stage is not compliance. It is genuine digital literacy. A young person who can articulate what technology gives them and what it costs them is ready for what comes next.

Set up now
Say this week
Watch for
Normal at this age: Full independence online, not wanting parental oversight of their accounts. The concern is complete disconnection from trusted adults, not independence itself.
