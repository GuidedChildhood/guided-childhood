Your four-year-old wants the iPad.

And you give it to them. Of course you do. It keeps them calm. It buys you ten minutes.

Here's what the research actually says you should be doing — and it's not what most parents expect:

Co-view. Stay in the room. Watch with them.

That's Stage 1 of the Guided Digital Pathway. Ages 4–7. Shared screen only. No feeds. No solo browsing.

It's not about banning screens. It's about being the guide before the algorithm becomes one.

The most common question at this stage:
"I can't get my four-year-old off the iPad. What am I doing wrong?"

Nothing. You're just missing the framework. Children at this age need an explicit off-screen routine — the exit is part of the habit.
