Most parents do their best with screens.

They set limits. They worry. They Google "how much screen time is too much."

And then their child turns 16 and they hand them a phone with full internet access and hope for the best.

That gap between "no, not yet" and "here you go" — that's the cliff edge.

Nobody built the pathway between them.

Until now.

This week I'm sharing the five-stage framework behind Guided Childhood — the research-backed system that takes children from their first screen at age 4 to full digital independence at 16.

One stage a day. If you're a parent, a teacher, or both — this is for you.
