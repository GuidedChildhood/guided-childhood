Dr Amy Orben at Cambridge has spent years studying how social media affects children.

Her finding: there is a developmental sensitivity window.

For girls: ages 11–13.
For boys: ages 14–15.

This is the stage where social comparison, algorithm exposure, and mood disruption do the most damage.

This is also the stage most schools handle the worst.

Not because teachers don't care. Because nobody gave them a structured approach.

Stage 3 of the Guided Digital Pathway — Explorer — is built specifically for ages 11–13. Guided smartphone. No social media. Scripts for when mood drops after screen time. And lesson plans designed for the Year 6–8 classroom.

The most common parent question at this stage:
"Her mood drops every time she puts her phone down. I'm worried."

You're right to be. But worry without a framework doesn't help her.
