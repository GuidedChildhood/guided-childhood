Every school in the UK delivers some form of digital safety education.

Most of it isn't working.

Not because teachers aren't trying. Because one-off assemblies, fragmented PSHE sessions, and reactive safeguarding responses aren't a curriculum. They're gap-filling.

The Online Safety Act 2023 is law. Statutory RSE requires digital safety content. Ofcom's Media Literacy Framework sets clear expectations. The DfE's AI in Education Guidance (June 2025) adds another layer of accountability.

The accountability framework exists. The structured, evidence-based curriculum often doesn't.

This week I'm sharing the five-stage Guided Digital Pathway — the framework behind Guided Childhood — built for both schools and families. One stage a day.

For PSHE leads, DSLs, class teachers, and school leaders: this is worth following.
