The research is consistent on one point: school-based digital literacy education is significantly more effective when reinforced at home.

The problem? Most schools send information home once a term, in a newsletter, that most parents don't read.

Every Guided Childhood school lesson includes a parent note — not a newsletter, but a specific, brief action a family can take that week, directly aligned to what was taught in class.

For schools this means:
— Parental engagement built into the curriculum, not bolted on
— Consistent messaging between home and school
— Parents who feel equipped rather than blamed

The platform also includes parent evening packs — separate primary and secondary versions — with a facilitator guide for staff.

Digital safety education works when the school and home are saying the same thing in the same week.
