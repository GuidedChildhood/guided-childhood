Effective digital literacy education shares five characteristics. We call it TRUST.

T — Timing: Developmentally sequenced content. A Year 1 child needs something categorically different from what a Year 9 student needs. The curriculum must reflect that.

R — Relationships: Warm, connected relationships — with parents and trusted adults — are the strongest protective factor in the evidence base. Schools play a direct role in reinforcing this.

U — Upstream: Rules and restrictions treat symptoms. Understanding platform mechanics, algorithm design, and persuasive technology treats causes. Upstream thinking is genuine media literacy.

S — Sleep: No devices in bedrooms at night. The research on sleep disruption and adolescent mental health is unambiguous. This belongs in every school's digital behaviour policy.

T — Transparency: Children who understand why limits exist comply more consistently and build self-regulation more effectively than those who are simply monitored.

Every Guided Childhood lesson — from EYFS to Sixth Form — is structured around TRUST.
