# How each platform's algorithm differs — verified mechanics
Verified 3 July 2026. Documented vs inferred flags included. Practical lever per platform.

## TikTok
- Leaked internal doc (NYT Dec 2021, authenticity confirmed): optimises retention and time spent.
- Monolith engineering paper (arXiv 2209.07663): model retrains on user behaviour within minutes. This is why a feed reset plus changed watching reshapes TikTok within a session or two, fastest of any platform.
- WSJ bot experiment 2021: in the WSJ's test, interests pinned in under 2 hours, some in about 40 minutes, from lingering alone. Phrase as "in the WSJ's test."
- Controls: For You feed reset button; under 18 60 minute default prompt (dismissible, not a hard stop); parent forced STEM feed via Family Pairing.
- Family lever: the feed reset works fast here; watch behaviour is the steering wheel.

## Instagram / Facebook
- Mosseri official: multiple separate ranking systems per surface. Stories ranks on relationship closeness; Reels top signals are watch time, likes per reach, sends per reach (DM shares strongest for reach). Multiplier claims are secondary reporting; use the ranked list only.
- Teen Accounts (Sept 2024): private by default, sleep mode 10pm to 7am; independent red team testing (Fairplay/Molly Rose, 47 features) found 64% ineffective, 17% working as promised. Never present as solved.
- History: angry emoji weighted 5x a like 2018 to 2020; zeroing it cut misinformation. Historical only.
- Family lever: relationships drive the feed; who you follow and message matters more than watch time.

## YouTube
- Covington 2016 (platform authored, peer reviewed): ranks on expected watch time, not clicks. Goodrow 2021: now "valued watch time" via 1 to 5 star satisfaction surveys.
- Controls: in supervised accounts a parent can disable autoplay and the child cannot re enable it; take a break and bedtime reminders default on for teens; Shorts daily limit to zero from March 2026.
- Family lever: autoplay off attacks the exact mechanic the ranker optimises.

## Snapchat
- Snap research (WSDM 2022) and support docs: friend Stories rank on communication frequency and recency, a friendship graph, not a content graph. Only Spotlight is TikTok style.
- Snapstreaks: reciprocity within every 24 hours; peer reviewed association with problematic use and FOMO; teens describe obligation and loss aversion. Snap publishes contrary research; phrase as association.
- Family lever: the pressure is social obligation, not content; conversations about streaks beat content filters.

## X
- Open sourced weights April 2023: reply engaged by author 75.0 vs like 0.5; report minus 369. Historical snapshot; ranker since replaced (Jan 2026).

## Regulator differentiation
- Ofcom 2025: harmful content encountered by secondary age children in 4 weeks: TikTok 53%, YouTube 36%, Instagram 34%, Facebook 31%; 73% of 11 to 17s overall. Children's codes require recommender filtering from 25 July 2025.
- EU DSA: TikTok addictive design preliminary finding (Feb 2026); Meta under 13 access proceedings. Always say preliminary.
