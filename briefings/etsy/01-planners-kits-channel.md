Multi agent research sweep, 20 July 2026. Companion: plans/etsy-launch-plan.md

# School Planners on Etsy: What Wins and What to Copy

Verification note: Etsy, eRank and Alura blocked direct fetches during research, so figures come from 2025 to 2026 search index snapshots. Sales counts are shop level (Etsy hides per listing sales); favourites and reviews are listing level where stated.

## The benchmark shops

**HappyDownloads (teacher digital planners, on Etsy since 2014)** is the category ceiling: 124,887 sales, 9,313 reviews at 4.9 stars, 17,895 admirers, 164 listings, Alura estimated ~GBP 1.01M lifetime revenue (estimate, unverified methodology). Built around ONE flagship listing (Etsy listing 757487097, 4,650+ favourites) that has been re dated every year since ~2019, so its reviews and search rank compound. Inside: 60+ templates (lesson plans, gradebook, attendance, IEP, seating chart, parent comms log, behaviour log), fully hyperlinked tabs, dated plus undated years, 50,000 digital stickers and 500 covers, works in GoodNotes, Notability, Noteshelf, Samsung Notes, OneNote (separate OneNote listing 770914393). Observed around $12.99.

**FutureADHD (Grace Koelma, AU)** proves niche beats generic: essentially ONE ADHD digital planner in ~6 colourways, each colourway a separate listing to own more search slots (rainbow listing 1282376971, dark rainbow 1620103130, sunset 1259249004, monochrome 1331622566). 60,463 sales, 14,228 admirers, 6,000 to 7,000 reviews, priced $16.72 to $17.21, roughly 2 to 3x generic planners, purely on identity positioning: "made by an ADHDer", "guilt free", lifetime updates, private Facebook community, therapist endorsements. Spun off a book deal and podcast. The ADHD student intersection (e.g. listing 1784983115) has no dominant shop yet, it is comparatively open.

**ThrivingPlanners** is the volume and discount end: 10,571 shop reviews (implying six figure sales at typical review rates), flagship at $6.27 (25% off $8.36) and $6.45 (40% off $10.74), 1,563 to 1,689 reviews at 4.5 on the flagship. Its real lesson is presentation: all 10 photo slots used as a mini sales page with feature callout arrows, compatibility badges, layout close ups and a hyperlink walkthrough video.

**AllieMarieDigital (Allie Hymas, Utah)**: 26.6k sales in ~3 years, 2.5k reviews at 4.9. Personal brand student planner (listing 4338617667): semester overviews, 8 class sections per semester, dated dailies for the whole year, 3 weekly layouts, 7 tab colourways, plus YouTube and linktr.ee funnel. Related riser: PrintMagicStudioX ("All in One Digital Planner 2025 2026 2027", 6,077 favourites; shop 34,290 sales).

**Other named players**: AishelleArte (5.4k sales, Star Seller) sells one teacher binder PDF in 7+ themes as separate listings (799 and 543 favourites on the two biggest). AnalidaDesigns (25,289 sales, ~400 listings) does single sheet homework printables in 8 sizes at $2 to $6. The budget flank is Google Sheets assignment trackers, not PDFs: HeyMorning's tracker (listing 1845305118) showed "11 people bought this in the last 24 hours" with 485 favourites; rivals carry 547 favourites and 632 reviews at 4.6, sold on auto sorting by due date demos. Homeschool is the softest sub niche for a new entrant (HelloRainbowCreative, 3.4k sales, is representative; multi child support is THE differentiator). Primary age kids planners barely exist as digital planners; the money there is print at home ($2 to $5 first day of school signs and homework planners as July to August traffic magnets).

## Pricing and economics

Winning band is $6 to $17 with permanent strike through discounting; never below $5 for a single planner. Premium niches (ADHD, teacher) sustain $13 to $20. Bundles at ~1.5x lift AOV. Long tail niched titles ("ADHD productivity planner printable PDF") price ~22% higher AND convert ~18% better than generic ones. Etsy fees on a digital planner are ~10 to 12% plus $0.45 (a $10 sale keeps roughly $8.60), with 12 to 15% more only on Offsite Ads orders.

## Seasonality (US first, UK second)

US "back to school" searches rise from May and peak July to August (Google Trends "etsy digital planner" hit its 100 peak in August 2025; Etsy ran homepage back to school promos by 3 August 2025). UK demand is structurally smaller per eRank (six week break, uniforms, no wardrobe change) and lands late August to early September. UK specific winners: September to August dated academic planners, A4 first, personalised teacher planners (Blue Marble Design's A4 2026 to 27 planner) and teacher gifts (~53,800 US searches for "teacher gifts" at the November peak). Calendar: dated listings live by mid June, US push 1 July to 15 August, UK push 15 August to 10 September, January refresh, and re date the same listings every May, never delete.

## Anatomy of a winning listing (the copy sheet)

1. Three date states in one listing: current academic year, next year, undated. Academic dating, with a Sept to Aug UK variant.
2. Hyperlinked navigation: home page, side tabs, month to week to day drill down. The number one thing shown in listing videos.
3. Student core: semester overviews, 6 to 8 subject hubs, timetable, assignment tracker, exam and revision planner, grade tracker, project planner, dated dailies, 2 to 3 weekly layouts. Teacher version swaps in lesson plans, gradebook, attendance, seating chart, parent comms.
4. Life pages as padding value: habit tracker, budget, meal plan, reading log, journal, 5 to 7 paper types. Plus a sticker and cover bonus.
5. Format spread: GoodNotes file plus hyperlinked PDF plus US Letter and A4 print versions; state "iPad, Android, Windows" explicitly.
6. Colourway splitting: same master file in 5 to 7 themes as separate listings.
7. Title formula, front loaded: "2026 2027 Student Digital Planner Goodnotes, Academic Planner, College Planner, iPad Planner, Notability, Undated School Planner".
8. All 10 photos plus a 5 to 15 second screen recording tapping through hyperlinks (the ThrivingPlanners method).
9. Strike through pricing: list $10 to $17, permanent 25 to 50% sale to land at $6 to $13.
10. Re date, never relist. Reviews compound on one listing ID.
11. Cold start maths: ~7 to 10% of buyers review (HappyDownloads runs 7.5%), so plan for 150 to 300 sales via cheap tracker and sign listings before a $13 planner looks safe to buy (15 to 25 visible reviews).

Flagged as unverified: Alura's revenue estimate, ThrivingPlanners' total sales, exact live prices (all sale price snapshots), UK month level keyword ranks.

# Build at Home Kits: The Market and the Winning Formula

Verification note: same proxy limits as above; Etsy sales counters seen in snippets are shop level figures that change daily.

## Proof of scale, category by category

- **Felt quiet books (the purest buy materials, build with child kit)**: littledear's "5 Felt Quiet Book PDF Sewing Patterns" (listing 628489924) shows 68,422 sales at 5.0 stars. PDF pattern plus instructions plus SVG cut files for Cricut; the parent buys felt, thread and velcro.
- **Chore and reward systems**: OvCreativeStudio, 71.9k sales, 4.9 stars, 6.8k reviews in ~4 years, engine is an editable chore chart (listing 1489437519). The killer review: daily rewards worked for the 7 year old, weekly cash for the 12 year old. Parents buy a system, then build it (print, laminate, magnets, sticker jar). This spans the full 5 to 14 age range Guided Childhood serves.
- **Busy books and learning binders (highest volume, lowest build effort)**: JustKidsDesign, 11.4k sales, 906 reviews at 4.9 in ~2.5 years, busy book listing 1626205219 has 6,892 favourites. A Toddler Busy Book Mega Bundle (400+ pages) shows 5,690 sales at 5.0. Build is print, laminate, cut, velcro dots, binder.
- **Pretend play kits**: a doctor pretend play printable set (listing 774787694) shows 8,990 sales at 5.0. The verbatim review that explains the whole category: parent laminated the pages and "paired with a stethoscope, otoscope, and play band aids", i.e. the PDF turns $10 of props into a toy. theCharlotteKirsten's 45+ page doctor bundle leads with "designed by a Child's Play Specialist" (250 favourites).
- **Printable board games**: Printable Blank Board Game Templates DIY (listing 1661138262), 4.9 stars, ~1,900 reviews, implying roughly 13k to 38k sold at the usual 5 to 15% review to sale ratio (inference).
- **Escape rooms (the premium event end)**: EscapeRoomGeeks segments by age: Kiddo 5 to 8, Tween 9 to 13, Teen 12 to 16, Adult 16+. PrintableEscapeRoom.com prices at $12.99 to $13.99 per game; Lock Paper Scissors and PaperEscapeCo similar. Setup 5 to 15 minutes, gameplay 45 to 90 minutes.
- **Science packs (underexploited)**: Digital Science Experiments Cards, 20 activities ages 3 to 7 (listing 1767092641) with "a complete materials list using common household items from the grocery or dollar store". Best in class cards (listing 4317918264) add per activity time, mess level and supervision flags. No big verified sales counts surfaced here, so it is soft ground for a new entrant.
- **Sensory and mud kitchen**: Summer Mud Kitchen Recipe Cards (listing 4333810773), 4.9 stars, 412 reviews, ages 2+. Recurring promise: "ready for play in less than 5 minutes". Sellers lead with "designed by an early years teacher".
- **Papercraft**: Numberblocks paper toys (listing 778265342), 4.9 stars, 293 reviews, but IP risk (BBC property, do not copy that part). aliceapple's dollhouse paper toy has 1,623 favourites. Ages 5+, 15 to 20 minutes to complete.
- **Boredom jars and screen free kits**: 100 activity and 140 idea jars, a 30 Day Screen Free Summer Learning Kit (listing 4317261398), and the Summer Camp at Home planner (listing 1448822722) whose materials list is "crayons, markers, paper plates, popsicle sticks, scissors and glue".

Third party estimates (Alicia Rafiei blog, unverified): daily routine card sets earning ~$9,000/month and preschool activity packs $1,000+/month at top shops.

## Prices, ages, economics

- Price band: $3 to $15 for most kids printables; escape rooms $12.99 to $13.99; mega bundles $10 to $20. Ladder: $4 to $7 single kit, $10 to $15 themed kit, $15 to $25 mega bundle, plus seasonal editions (Halloween, Christmas, Summer) that resell the same buyers.
- Fees: ~$0.88 on a $5 download (17.6% take), ~12.5% at $10. Bundles at $10 to $20 are structurally more profitable than $3 to $5 singles.
- Ages: 2 to 5 is the volume king (all the 5k to 70k sales counts sit there), but for Guided Childhood's 5 to 14 audience the relevant lanes are papercraft 5+, science 3 to 7, escape rooms 5 to 16 with tweens 9 to 13 the party sweet spot, and mixed age family products (boredom jars, board games, chore systems) that widen the buyer pool.

## The materials list mechanic

1. Generic, cheap, ubiquitous. The dominant pattern is a one page list framed as household, grocery store or dollar store items, with cheapness marketed as a feature.
2. No affiliate links in the PDF. Amazon Associates ToS bans Special Links in PDFs and offline content, and Etsy is not an Amazon approved site. Top listings all use generic lists. Etsy's own affiliate policy requires conspicuous disclosure anyway.
3. Cutting machine crossover: felt patterns bundle SVGs so a Cricut does the cutting.
4. Prep effort labelling: best sellers state materials, prep time in minutes, mess level and supervision level per activity.

## Why parents pay for instructions they could google

Time and curation (a purchase replaces an evening of Pinterest triage), speed to calm ("ready for play in less than 5 minutes", often bought in a desperate moment), cohesive design that feels like a real product once laminated, credentialed authority ("early years teacher", "Child's Play Specialist", exactly the slot Guided Childhood's expert voice fills), format insurance (A4 plus US Letter, unlimited reprints), and social proof (400 to 6,800 reviews at 4.8 to 4.9 de risk a $7 purchase).

## What a winning kit contains (synthesis)

1. One named outcome, not "50 worksheets": "run a spy escape room party", "build your child's calm down kit".
2. Cover page plus one page materials list of cheap household items with quantities, plus a nice to have column. No affiliate links.
3. Step by step photo instructions, prep time in minutes, mess level, supervision level.
4. A4 AND US Letter, ink friendly black and white option, SVGs for cut heavy kits.
5. Durability layer: laminate, cardstock and velcro instructions so the printout becomes a reusable toy.
6. Tight age banding on the listing (5 to 8, 9 to 13): the escape room shops prove parents shop by age first.
7. A credibility line plus a skills developed paragraph (fine motor, problem solving) for gift buyers.
8. Price ladder and seasonal editions to resell existing buyers.
9. Fast relief promise up front: "5 minute prep", "screen free", "instant download".
10. Review flywheel basics: both paper sizes, fast replies, a print troubleshooting page.

# The Guided Childhood Etsy Channel: One Shop, Two Jobs

The shop earns on its own AND feeds the platform. Every product is profitable at the point of sale (73 to 84% gross margin), and every buyer is a warm, already paying parent who can be moved, compliantly, onto the Guided Childhood list and toward the starter pack. Think of it as paid acquisition in reverse: instead of spending to acquire an email, each email arrives with ~£2 to £10 of margin attached.

## 1. The economics you are actually playing with (UK seller)

Fee stack: $15 one time setup fee ($15 to $29 by location, new shops since 15 Sept 2024), £0.16 listing fee per listing per 4 months that also auto renews on every sale, 6.5% transaction, UK payment processing 4% + £0.20 flat, UK Regulatory Operating Fee 0.48% (raised from 0.32% on 22 June 2026), plus 20% VAT on all Etsy fees unless you register a VAT number with Etsy. Buyer side VAT on digital items is added, collected and remitted by Etsy; you price ex VAT.

Real margins (calculated at the verified rates):

| Price | Total fees | Take rate | You keep |
|---|---|---|---|
| £3.00 | £0.82 | 27.4% | £2.18 |
| £5.00 | £1.08 | 21.6% | £3.92 |
| £8.00 | £1.47 | 18.4% | £6.53 |
| £12.00 | £1.99 | 16.6% | £10.01 |
| £15.00 | £2.38 | 15.9% | £12.62 |

Fee traps: opt out of Offsite Ads on day one (15% per attributed order, pushing total take to ~40% on a £5 item; becomes mandatory for life at 12% once you cross $10,000 in a 365 day window, and you can only opt out while under it). No Etsy Ads on sub £8 listings (CPC ~$0.25 to $0.50 is price blind; if testing, bundles only, $5/day cap, kill after ~100 clicks with no conversion). No Etsy Plus at launch. Nothing priced below £4 except deliberate entry offers.

## 2. The catalogue: 30 listings at launch, built from the two winning lanes

Consensus for digital shops is 20 to 50 listings at launch, not 3 to 5 (one documented seller added ~10 listings/week to ~350 listings and hit ~5,000 sales in year one). Each age band, colourway and editable variant is its own listing, its own keyword surface and its own honeymoon boost (new listings get roughly 24 to 72 hours to ~14 days of boosted visibility, then live or die on conversion).

Launch grid for Guided Childhood:
- **8 routine and screen time charts** (£4 to £5 entry, the review velocity engine): morning and bedtime routines by age band (5 to 7, 8 to 11, 12 to 14), ADHD variant, after school checklist. Demand proof: an editable weekly chore chart listing (1660446583) has 762 reviews at 4.9; screen time contracts sell at ~$2.85 to $4.74 (listings 1623223496, 1846164928, 1848913374, 1836722705); PrintableDandelion, a teacher designed kids printables shop, has 47,000+ sales since 2022.
- **6 school planners** (£8 to £12 core): student digital planner in 2 to 3 colourways, dated 2026 to 27 plus undated plus a UK Sept to Aug variant, hyperlinked, GoodNotes plus PDF plus A4 and US Letter, built to the anatomy in the school planners section. It is 20 July: the US back to school wave is live NOW, these go up first.
- **6 parent guides** (£4 to £6): the guides already written, repackaged per Etsy conventions. Policy guardrails: "helps families build healthier screen habits" is fine; "reduces your child's anxiety" is a prohibited medical claim regardless of disclaimers. Everything must be genuinely yours (June 2025 Creativity Standards ban PLR and marketplace templates and require AI disclosure on AI assisted work). Re read the new Prohibited Items Policy before it takes effect 11 August 2026.
- **6 build at home kits** (£4 to £12): screen free boredom jar, family escape room by age band (9 to 13 sweet spot), science experiment cards with household materials lists, calm down kit. Each with the materials list mechanic: one page of cheap household items, no affiliate links, prep time, mess level, supervision level, A4 plus Letter, laminate instructions, credibility line.
- **4 bundles** (£15 to £25, the revenue core): e.g. a "Family Screen Reset Kit" (guide plus charts plus family media agreement plus activity kit), priced 20 to 30% below the sum of singles.

Positioning gap the research found: the niche is dominated by generic Canva template shops; almost nobody sells an expert voiced parent guide plus tools bundle with a real digital wellbeing brand behind it. That credibility, delivered through Etsy's proven formats, is the differentiator.

## 3. Per listing execution

Titles ~70 characters, structured, not stuffed: "Kids Screen Time Chart | Editable Daily Routine Printable | Ages 5-10". All 13 tags covering different phrasings than the title ("adhd routine chart", "family media agreement", "tech contract for teens"). Every attribute filled (blank attributes silently exclude you from filtered search). All 10 image slots: lifestyle mockup first (chart on a real fridge), contents flat lay, an "Instant Download, no physical item ships" overlay, sizing guide, how it works, bundle contents; plus a 5 to 15 second page flip video. Minimum 2000px, Guided Childhood palette and Nunito throughout, warm not corporate.

Pricing behaviour: launch week sale via Etsy's sale tool, then plan price rises. Documented test: a planner raised from $8 to $12 after 50 sales gained 35% revenue with only a 15% conversion drop. Underpricing is the common error; top planner shops report $8,000 to $15,000/month at $8 to $35 price points.

## 4. Reviews and the cold start

Buyers of digital items can review for 100 days from first download. Allowed: one polite post purchase message, a genuinely free bonus for ALL buyers, a warm one line review ask on the PDF's last page, replying to every review. Banned: any incentive tied to reviews or sentiment, asking twice, review swaps (Etsy enforcement plus the FTC rule with penalties up to $53,088 per violation). Roughly 20 to 25% of buyers review at best with a good flow, 7 to 10% is typical at the top of the planner niche, so plan for 150 to 300 sales through the £4 to £5 entry listings before the £12+ products carry the 15 to 25 reviews that make them look safe.

## 5. The funnel: how the shop feeds the platform (all inside Etsy's rules)

The proven model: Gold City Ventures used cheap Etsy printables plus content to build an email list that produced a $70,000 launch weekend for a $247 course that has grossed millions (students report $3.38M+ combined Etsy sales). Big Life Journal, the closest parenting analog, built a 200,000+ list of parents and teachers on free printables and a weekly Friday freebie email, then sold journals to the list. Sarah Titus opened her own store with 35,868 subscribers banked and did $52,060 in month one. Rachel Jimenez went from a 2018 side hustle to ~$160,000 in 2021 and $10,000+/month.

The rules line is transaction location, not links. Prohibited: moving a purchase off Etsy, QR codes directing buyers to another venue to buy, "cheaper on our site" anywhere, marketing via Etsy Messages (sellers get muted for coupon pushes), and adding any buyer to an email list without consent (also a GDPR problem; never use bulk email export tools). Explicitly permitted: inviting signups on your public shop page and during the order process. The standard practice everyone uses: an opt in link to a FREE bonus inside the delivered PDF (no written Etsy rule addresses in file links; keep the destination a free resource page, never a checkout).

The mechanics, wired to Guided Childhood:
1. **Last page of every PDF**: one named free bonus gated by email at a trackable landing page (/etsy-bonus). The bonus extends the purchase: screen time guide buyers get the family screen agreement template; chart buyers get a free DiGi mini lesson, the strongest possible bridge to the platform.
2. **The printable as a recurring billboard**: charts live on fridges for months; a discreet footer URL to a free page earns repeat impressions.
3. **Auto "Message to Buyers"** on every digital order: download help plus one soft line ("your guide includes a free bonus on the last page"). Nothing else.
4. **Shop announcement and About section**: "Join parents getting a free guide every month at guidedchildhood" (the explicitly permitted placement).
5. **Welcome sequence**: 3 emails over 7 days (deliver bonus and introduce the brand, one practical win, starter pack offer), then a Big Life Journal style weekly freebie cadence with offers interleaved. Every CTA routes to /starter-pack, per house rule 9.
6. **Share & Save**: once the newsletter and socials push traffic TO Etsy listings, Etsy rebates 4% of the order (transaction fee effectively 2.5%), and external traffic that converts is itself a ranking signal.
7. Also free upside: Etsy listings rank in Google, Etsy has ~95M active buyers, and agentic ChatGPT driven traffic to Etsy grew ~15x YoY in Q4 2025.

## 6. Realistic conversion numbers (planning figures, middle step is proxy based)

- Visit to buyer: digital listings average 6 to 7% (top shops 12 to 20%); plan on 2 to 4% for the first 90 days as a new shop.
- Buyer to email: no published Etsy specific benchmark exists; nearest proxies (QR post scan form fills at 10 to 25%) suggest a zero friction in PDF link with a genuinely wanted bonus lands at 10 to 25% of buyers. Plan on 10 to 15%.
- Email to paid member: median email to paid is 0.62% (beehiiv 2026); 2 to 5% is the realistic goal for a warm niche list of proven buyers over 6 to 12 months.
- Composite per 1,000 Etsy buyers (who have already banked you roughly £3,500 to £4,500 net): ~100 to 250 subscribers, ~2 to 12 paid members in the first months, trending to 10 to 30 over a year. Net buyer to member: ~0.3 to 1% initially, 1 to 3% over 12 months.
- Implication for the 50 seat founder rate: filling it from Etsy alone would need ~5,000 to 15,000 buyers. So Etsy is the compounding, self funding list builder, review engine and SEO surface; faster channels (newsletter, LinkedIn, schools) fill the founder cohort.

## 7. Launch sequence

1. This week: open the shop, budget the setup fee, opt out of Offsite Ads immediately, complete every profile section.
2. Build the 30 listing grid before opening; school planners and back to school items first (the US wave peaks now through mid August; UK wave 15 August to 10 September).
3. Price the ladder (£4 to £5 / £8 to £12 / £15 to £25), launch week sale, plan the $8 to $12 style rise after ~50 sales per item.
4. Week 1: push the existing newsletter and socials to 3 hero listings during their honeymoon boost; reply to every message within hours.
5. Wire the funnel: bonus page in every PDF, /etsy-bonus landing page, auto buyer message, welcome sequence to /starter-pack.
6. Cadence: 5 to 10 new listings/week for 8 to 10 weeks; watch Etsy Search Analytics and double down on converting keywords; re date planner listings every May, never delete.
7. Before 11 August: re read the new Prohibited Items Policy. If VAT registration happens later, give Etsy the number to stop the 20% VAT on fees.
