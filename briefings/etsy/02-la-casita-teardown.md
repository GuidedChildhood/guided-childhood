# Where the English market gap is biggest (ranked)

1. **A to Z word wheel [Pasapalabra]: THE gap.** Essentially zero print-and-play presence in English. TpT "pasapalabra" results are Spanish-class resources; English versions are only free digital quizzes on Educaplay/Wordwall; Twinkl's nearest is a KS2 PowerPoint. Needs a new name ("A to Z Showdown") since the TV anchor does not exist in the UK.
2. **Word fishing: large gap.** Top TpT rival "Fishing For Words" has only **12 ratings at 4.74 stars**. A handmade Phase 2 magnetic fishing game sells on Etsy UK (31 laminated fish, Sassoon Primary font), proving UK parents pay for exactly this, but nobody does it as a polished digital download with kawaii art and reel demos.
3. **Ice cream shop money game, UK £ edition.** US dollar dramatic play is crowded (5 to 6 active Etsy listings) but a KS1 coin-counting game with pound coins is thin; only one 75-page kit found includes both $ and £.
4. **Christmas Alphabet Wheel** (seasonal skin of #1): nothing comparable found.
5. **Road Safety Wheel for Brake's Road Safety Week (November)**: only worksheets and PowerPoints exist; schools expect it free, so treat as a viral lead magnet.
6. **UK end-of-term game packs (July + December)**: the US "end of year" TpT category is saturated but is a May/June category; Etsy UK "end of term" is near empty.
7. **Secret word decoding**: freebie-level competition only (Twinkl free Phase 2 version; a TpT bundle with 1,200+ cards); use as funnel entry, not hero product.
8. **Phase-aligned word mapping mats**: UK-only angle (US science-of-reading market saturated).
9. **Tricky words / Phase 2 to 5 game bundle**: demand proven, supply mostly handmade or low design.
10. **The model itself**: no English account found running hands-on reels + 24-hour flash freebies + own shop at their 400K scale (flagged: absence of evidence, not a census).

**Do NOT transfer:** syllable mechanics (Spanish reads syllabically; English is phoneme first, and the residual niche has Etsy Star Sellers with 48 to 60 card sets); class rules posters (saturated); uppercase/lowercase matching standalone (commoditised); routine sequencing cards (mature mega-niche); Sant Jordi-style bookmarks as paid (World Book Day bookmarks are free everywhere, including licensed Bluey/Gruffalo sheets from The Works); the Pasapalabra name or TV trade dress (IP risk).

## The repeatable format recipes (English, ready to run)

1. **The Overhead Table Demo (the default unit, ~80% of output).** 15 to 30 seconds, camera straight down, a child's hands playing the laminated game start to finish, velcro and counter sounds, no faces, no talking, text overlay naming the skill ("Phase 3 tricky words, age 5+"). One identical caption CTA every time.
2. **Free for 24 Hours.** Normally paid printable, "FREE FOR 24 HOURS" as first frame and first caption line, delivered only through your own checkout so every download captures an email. Run 1 to 2 times a month; announce first on the broadcast channel so superfans light up the algorithm in hour one.
3. **The Seasonal Reskin.** Never invent a new mechanic for a season; reskin a proven one. Their word wheel became Christmas, Road Safety and Back to School editions; syllables became summer, autumn and Christmas tree editions. One asset library, four or more posting moments a year.
4. **The Frustration Hook.** Open on the pain: "If your child gets frustrated learning to read..." then show the game making it easy. Same line in the bio so profile visits convert.
5. **TV-Show Borrow.** Clone a beloved national game format (their Pasapalabra is Spain's biggest word quiz). UK equivalent: a Countdown-style letters round.
6. **The Freebie Wedge Pair.** Keep a permanent free version (Buy Ice Creams, FREE, 5 stars) next to paid siblings (Syllable Ice Creams, Frozen Letters, Super Ice Creams and Sums). The free one goes viral, the family upsells.
7. **Founder With Props.** 5 to 10 seconds, founder to camera holding the seasonal printable: "This week only..." Used for launches and urgency, humanising the brand between hands-only demos.
8. **Behind the Scenes.** "Here's how they're made": laminating, cutting, velcro-ing. Craft ASMR that doubles as an assembly tutorial and buying-confidence builder.
9. **The Spoken-URL CTA.** One identical typed-not-tapped CTA on every post for years: "Get it at [domain].com, Resources section, [category]." Consistency turns the URL into a jingle; no dependence on link-in-bio taps.
10. **Teacher Hashtag Stack, Parent Bio.** Hashtags target teachers (their #maestra #profes #claustrodeig; UK equivalents #edutwitter #teacher5oclockclub #earlyyears) because teachers are the sharers; the bio speaks to parents because parents are the buyers. Same reel serves both.
11. **Micro-Price Everything.** Every digital item cheap enough to buy within 60 seconds of the reel, priced-not-free so the catalogue trains buying behaviour. Volume and list growth over per-unit margin.
12. **Checkout-Gated Freebies.** Never a raw PDF link. Free goes through cart, details, order, email delivery. Every viral spike becomes owned audience, which is what makes recipe 2 compound instead of being a giveaway.
13. **(Bonus) The Sponsored Supplies Haul.** Once the audience exists, monetise their other purchases with an affiliate deal and referral code (their Temu deal, code duy3252). Keep it adjacent to your products so it reads as service. Note for the UK: Temu carries brand risk with UK parents; Amazon UK affiliate is the safer equivalent.

## The funnel behind the formats
Reel shows the game being played → caption CTA sends people to type the URL (broadcast channel pushes 24-hour drops to superfans as push notifications) → free product forces full checkout → email captured → email list resells the 2 to 3.50 EUR catalogue forever → upsell into physical boxes (Infant Box, Assembly Box, peninsula-wide delivery) → affiliate income on top. No published revenue anywhere; a bottom-up model (flagged as estimate) puts printables at roughly 2,500 to 8,000 EUR a month, with affiliate income potentially matching it in campaign months.

# Guided Childhood Adoption Plan

## The strategic read first
La Casita monetises a top 1% audience with bottom-quartile pricing: no membership, no subscription, singles at 2 to 4 EUR. The English market has already climbed the ladder they never did: Busy Toddler (2.4M followers) sells a $29.90 to $39.90 flagship, The Measured Mom runs $14.97/month or $149/year, the Plato Pack is $39/month or $299/year, and Twinkl turned the category into a **£78.6M/year subscription business (FY to 30 April 2024, +18% YoY, £45.3M of it UK)**. Guided Childhood already has the membership. So we adopt their **acquisition machine** (reels, freebies, forced checkout, seasonal reskins) but invert their **monetisation**: printables are funnel fuel for the platform, not the business. The whitespace against Twinkl is not coverage, it is character IP and game-first style, which is exactly what DiGi and the digi-squad are.

One warning their data hands us for free: their Etsy shop has 5 lifetime sales since 2021 while their own store carries the business. Etsy is a discovery and search channel, never the home. Every Etsy listing exists to move buyers onto our own checkout and email list.

## The first 8 to 10 products to build

1. **DiGi's A to Z Showdown** (the Pasapalabra mechanic, renamed). Printable alphabet wheel, one clue per letter, hosted by DiGi. The single biggest verified gap: zero English print-and-play competitor. KS1/KS2 vocabulary editions. Paid, £4.
2. **Word Fishing with Teo** (or the UK animals). Print, laminate, paperclip, magnet wand. **Phase 2/3 tricky words** editions labelled by phase and GPCs covered (parents still search "Phase 2/3/4/5" even though the DfE retired Letters and Sounds in 2021; never use the Little Wandle or Read Write Inc trademarks). School-style font (the Etsy UK evidence points to Sassoon Primary). Top existing rival has 12 ratings. Paid, £3.50 to £4.
3. **Alma's Ice Cream Shop, £ coins edition.** Coin-counting game mapped to Year 1 (recognise coins and notes) and Year 2 (combinations of coins to make amounts), 1p to £2. The dollar dramatic-play market is crowded; the KS1 pound-coin game barely exists. Paid, £4. A free cut-down version mirrors their 5-star free Buy Ice Creams as the permanent freebie wedge.
4. **Secret Word with DiGi Junior.** Decode-the-picture-letters reveal game. Competition is freebie level only, so this is our permanent lead magnet: free forever, checkout required.
5. **Christmas A to Z Showdown.** First seasonal skin of product 1, December. Nothing comparable found in English. Paid.
6. **UK End of Term Game Pack** (July and December editions). Near-empty Etsy UK category. Bundle of 5 to 8 games at £15 to £25, in line with the observed $18 to 30 bundle norm.
7. **The Online Safety Wheel.** Our translation of their Road Safety Pasapalabra, but on home turf: an A to Z of digital childhood (apps, passwords, group chats, screen time words) timed to **Safer Internet Day in February**, with a road safety edition for **Brake's Road Safety Week in November**. Free lead magnets (schools expect safety content free), each one a direct on-ramp to the platform and the parent guides. This is the product only Guided Childhood can make.
8. **Phase-aligned Word Mapping Mats.** UK-only angle; the US version is saturated. Secondary paid product, £3.
9. **World Book Day bookmark sheet (digi-squad characters).** March. Zero commercial gap (The Works gives away Bluey and Gruffalo sheets free) so ours is free, checkout-gated, timed to World Book Day week.
10. **Tricky Words Phase 2 to 5 bundle.** Rolls products 2 and 8 plus new singles into the £15 to £25 flagship bundle once 4 to 5 singles exist.

Pricing across the range: £3 to £5 singles, £15 to £25 bundles, everything included free inside the platform membership. Founder rate stays capped at 50, enforced in code.

## Mapping the free 24 hours mechanic to Justin
- Their mechanic: a normally paid product goes to 0.00 for 24 hours, announced by reel plus broadcast channel, and even free items must complete checkout, so every download is a captured email. Verified running from October 2023 to May 2025 because it works; modelled at 300 to 900 emails per drop from a 30K-view reel.
- Our version: **"Free until tomorrow" drops, once or twice a month, delivered only through our own checkout** (Stripe handles 0.00 orders; docs/01 covers the flow). Never a raw PDF link, never through Etsy (Etsy cannot list at £0 and never gives us the email). Announce on Instagram broadcast channel first, reel second. Every drop's thank-you page and delivery email routes to /starter-pack, per the non-negotiable that every CTA on /join routes there.
- Their founder-face format: Aida is "mum whose kids it worked on", not a credentialed teacher, and it is load bearing. Justin's equivalent is "dad who built the calibrated pathway for his own kids", which fits the existing voice rules. Format split copied directly: ~80% overhead hands-on-table demos (kids' hands, laminated DiGi characters, no faces, no talking, skill named in overlay), founder-with-props pieces of 5 to 10 seconds for launches and 24-hour drops, plus behind-the-scenes laminating content. One spoken-URL CTA repeated identically on every post until it is a jingle. Their cadence was 22 posts a month for 7 years; we start at a sustainable 3 to 5 reels a week with the overhead demo as the default unit, following the UK school calendar: September back to school, November Road Safety Week, December Christmas skins and end of term, February Safer Internet Day, March World Book Day, July end of term.

## How it feeds Etsy and the platform funnel
- **Etsy's job is search discovery only.** List the paid singles and bundles there for buyers searching "phase 2 games" and "end of term games", price at the £3 to £5 / £15 to £25 tiers, and put a printed insert page in every download inviting buyers to claim a free game on our own site (which requires checkout, which captures the email). Expect Etsy to stay small; that is fine and matches the evidence.
- **The platform is the ladder's top.** Free checkout-gated printable → email list → 24-hour drops train buying → £3 to £5 singles → £15 to £25 bundle → membership where every printable is included and DiGi lessons live. The printables are Twinkl-proof because the moat is the characters, and each physical game doubles as a screen-free activity, which is the brand promise itself.
- **Parent guides slot in as the upsell** on every printable thank-you page, and the schools channel mirrors their "Physical Materials for educational centres" page later (their model validated laminated physical kits, like their Infant Box and Assembly Box, as the higher-ticket tier).
- **What we deliberately do not copy:** their 2 EUR pricing ceiling, syllable products (wrong pedagogy for English phonics), class rules posters, routine cards and letter matching as paid products (all saturated), the Pasapalabra name or TV trade dress, and Temu-style affiliate deals (brand risk with UK parents; Amazon UK affiliate only, and only for laminators, wallets and magnet wands adjacent to our own games).

Build order for the first six weeks: products 4 and 3-free (the two permanent lead magnets) plus product 1 and 2 as the first paid pair, then the seasonal calendar dictates the rest. Plan to be written to /plans/ before building, per house rules.
