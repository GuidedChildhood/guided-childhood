# Etsy Research Pack 3: The Verified Top Ten

Multi agent sweep (8 lenses, 75 raw findings), synthesis to 14 candidates, live verification of every candidate (14 confirmed, 0 dropped), final ranking. 20 July 2026.

NOTE: any legacy character names in agent output (Teo, Olga, Alma) map to the CURRENT squad per digi-squad/README.md: Oliver, Zara, Sofia, DiGi golden star, UK animals.

## Rank 1: All in one digital planners for GoodNotes and iPad (dated plus undated)

- **Category:** Planners and organization
- **Why it sells:** Largest digital niche on Etsy. Dated versions force an annual January repurchase, and hyperlinked GoodNotes bundles with sticker packs and tutorial videos create premium perceived value sold on a perpetual 50 to 65 percent off anchor.
- **Verified demand:** Strongest verified evidence in the set: AmethystGarnet confirmed live at about 134,544 sales, 5.0 stars, with the exact claimed 22,388 review listing verified, plus Plannerscollective live at about 141.3k sales and 12,066 reviews. Multiple live Bestseller badges confirmed on category listings.
- **Price range:** $9 to $15 typical sale price, anchored from $25 to $28 list
- **Competition:** Very high; winners differentiate with multi year bundles, thousands of hyperlinks, included stickers and tutorials
- **GC fit (score 5/10):** A Guided Childhood Family Digital Rhythm Planner: a dated annual planner for parents that builds screen routines, device handover milestones and family media agreements into the weekly spread, with DiGi squad art. Slots as a standalone Etsy product for audience acquisition rather than core curriculum; the dated format gives an annual repurchase and a natural Sunday newsletter tie in each January.

## Rank 2: Seasonal and occasion printables (Christmas/Elf kits, shower games, party bundles)

- **Category:** Seasonal and party printables
- **Why it sells:** Instant download owns the last minute December panic window, and one core template recycles across every season. The play is 50 to 200 small rotating listings, not one hero product, with editable Canva versions at 2 to 3x static pricing.
- **Verified demand:** Strong evidence quality: CNBC independently confirmed Rachel Jimenez's $77,000 in 2023 from Elf and Christmas printables. MintyPaperieShop verified live at 5.0 stars with 16,341 shop reviews and a Bestseller badged game template with 13,234 reviews at $4.99. eRank confirms Q4 keyword dominance.
- **Price range:** $3 to $12 for kits and single sheets ($2.99 to $4.99 singles common); editable bundles list $15 to $40, typically selling at $10 to $25
- **Competition:** Moderate per listing because demand fragments across hundreds of seasonal keywords; listing 6 to 8 weeks pre peak is the edge
- **GC fit (score 6/10):** Screen free seasonal kits in the digi squad style: a Screen Free Summer Holidays challenge kit, a 12 Days of Offline December advent, half term boredom buster packs. Slots as the rotating engine of a standalone Etsy shop and gives the Sunday newsletter a seasonal product to point at six times a year; the summer and Christmas kits double as starter pack bonuses.

## Rank 3: Printable wall art (nursery signature styles plus gallery wall mega bundles)

- **Category:** Home decor printables
- **Why it sells:** Highest margin subcategory since buyers print at home. Two proven plays: a distinctive signature illustration style that owns Pinterest traffic, or enormous 10,000 plus piece bundles sold cheap on value framing.
- **Verified demand:** TheCrownPrints verified live at 139K+ sales, 5.0 rating, one person shop with a signature nursery style. PrintableSky corroborated at 112K+ sales and about $1.3M lifetime. DepArtPrints mega bundle verified with exactly the claimed 7,002 favorites. Category consistently top 3 in Etsy printables with 400,000+ monthly searches.
- **Price range:** $4 to $18 single prints (most common $4.99 to $12); mega bundles $10 to $30 after heavy discounts
- **Competition:** Extremely crowded race to the bottom on singles; a signature style, personalization or mega bundle scale is required to win
- **GC fit (score 4/10):** Kids bedroom and classroom posters in the digi squad illustration style: calm tech habit prints (devices sleep outside bedrooms, the pause before you post), affirmation art featuring Teo, Olga, Alma and DiGi Junior. Low strategic priority as a standalone play, but the classroom poster set slots naturally into the school pack and the bedroom prints pad out Etsy order values as add ons.

## Rank 4: Kids daily routine cards and visual schedules (morning/bedtime rhythm charts)

- **Category:** Family and parenting printables
- **Why it sells:** Illustrated mix and match cards solve morning and bedtime battles. Montessori and daily rhythm framing performs, and a documented SEN sub market exists via occupational therapists recommending visual schedules for pre readers and neurodivergent kids.
- **Verified demand:** LittleFolkPrintables verified live at 125.4k shop sales, about 9.6k reviews at 4.9 stars, Star Seller, with the flagship 204 card set confirmed at exactly the claimed 7,957 favorites. Three dedicated Etsy market pages confirmed plus several adjacent ones, with bestselling routine card items surfaced on each.
- **Price range:** $3 to $13 per set (sale prices $4.85 to $6.49, list $9.99 to $12.84); editable Canva versions at the top of the range
- **Competition:** Moderate with one dominant shop, but SEN positioning and editability remain open gaps
- **GC fit (score 9/10):** Morning and bedtime routine cards where screens are a calibrated step in the sequence rather than banned: devices down card 30 minutes before bed, one show then shoes on. Illustrated with the digi squad, narrated by DiGi. Slots three ways: a 12 card starter set as the lead magnet, the full deck as a starter pack add on, and an SEN visual schedule variant inside the school pack. One of the clearest matches to the calibrated pathways message.

## Rank 5: Calm down corner kits and SEL regulation tools (feelings charts, coping cards, breathing cards)

- **Category:** SEL and emotional regulation printables
- **Why it sells:** Dual market multiplies volume: parents at home plus teachers, counselors and therapists spending SEL budgets. Poster kits, feelings thermometers, coping decks and breathing cards cluster into bundles that are very cheap to produce.
- **Verified demand:** MissJacobsClassroom verified live at 104.8k sales, 4.9 stars from about 9.4k reviews, Star Seller, selling calm down corner kits in 8 themes. Dedicated Etsy market pages confirmed (calm_down_corner, calming_cards, mindfulness_breathing_cards). PeacefulThoughtsbyBC's claimed 40 poster set confirmed to exist.
- **Price range:** $3 to $15 per kit or deck (comparable off Etsy sets about $17); classroom bundles $20+
- **Competition:** Dense but still absorbing new entrants; feelings cards sell inside calm corner and SEL bundles rather than standalone
- **GC fit (score 9/10):** The Mental Health Checker made physical: a Guided Childhood calm corner kit with feelings check in cards, finger tracing breathing cards led by DiGi, and coping pathways for big feelings after screen time ends. Slots as the emotional core of the school pack (schools already buy exactly this from SEL budgets) and as a home edition on Etsy that funnels buyers toward the Checker and starter pack.

## Rank 6: ADHD and neurodivergent planners (digital and printable)

- **Category:** Wellbeing planners
- **Why it sells:** Pain point niching beats generic planners: lived experience authenticity, science based framing, dark mode variants and built in habit trackers. The yearly refresh turns one asset into an annual repurchase; repeatedly named the top planner sub niche for 2026.
- **Verified demand:** FutureADHD confirmed live with 6,000+ reviews and high sales volume flags, plus a solidly confirmed Penguin Random House book deal built from the planner. ExclusiveDesignLab verified as a Star Seller at 102,411 sales. Digicarft ADHD planner observed live at $7.20 (60 percent off $17.99). The 100,000 copies figure is self reported.
- **Price range:** $7 to $15 realized; list prices $10 to $18 with near permanent 50 to 60 percent discounts
- **Competition:** Growing fast but less saturated than generic planners; credibility framing is the moat
- **GC fit (score 6/10):** A focus friendly homework, gaming and downtime planner for neurodivergent kids and their parents, leaning on Guided Childhood's research voice rather than a lived experience claim it cannot make. Executive function scaffolding around transitions off screens is the differentiator nobody else owns. Slots as a standalone Etsy line reaching the SEN parent audience, cross sold with the SEN routine card variant; not core curriculum.

## Rank 7: Budget and finance spreadsheets (Google Sheets, budget by paycheck)

- **Category:** Spreadsheet templates
- **Why it sells:** Functional editable tools command 3 to 7x printable prices. Budget by paycheck and 50/30/20 angles dominate with guaranteed January and October dated product demand waves.
- **Verified demand:** SimplyOrganizedPro verified live at 99,864 sales since 2022 with 8,696 reviews. Side Hustle Nation's $250k in under 2 years case confirmed (Emily McDermott, 29,000+ sales). Five dedicated Etsy market pages confirmed including 2026 dated ones, with Bestseller badges observed in snippets. Two of the four cited revenue anecdotes unverified.
- **Price range:** $3 to $15 typical realized (list $15 to $26 at 50 to 60 percent off); premium dashboards and bundles $30 to $70
- **Competition:** High on generic budget PDFs; the spreadsheet format with paycheck cycle features and bundling is where the six figure jumps happen
- **GC fit (score 4/10):** Weak direct fit for money budgeting, but the format transfers: a Family Screen Time Budget spreadsheet where the household allocates, tracks and banks screen hours like a currency, plus a pocket money and screen bucks hybrid tracker. Slots as a single experimental Etsy listing to test whether the audience wants tools in spreadsheet form; not worth a product line unless it outperforms.

## Rank 8: Editable kids chore and reward charts

- **Category:** Family and parenting printables
- **Why it sells:** 82 percent of parents use chore charts. Editable Canva versions with picture based tasks for pre readers dramatically outsell static PDFs, and the emerging bestseller mechanic bundles screen time rewards (screen bucks earned via chores) directly into the chart.
- **Verified demand:** OvCreativeStudio verified live: Star Seller, 71.9k shop sales, 4.9 stars from 6.8k reviews, with the claimed listing 1489437519 confirmed at 2,708 favorites (note: 71.9k is a shop total, not one listing, so the $250k+ single listing extrapolation is unsupported). Digicarft verified at 9,500 reviews. Dedicated market pages and dozens of bestseller flagged siblings confirmed.
- **Price range:** $2.60 to $6 per chart ($4.49 sale from $5.99 common); $10 to $15 bundles unverified
- **Competition:** Static singles are commoditised; editable, multi kid, picture based and screen time reward variants are the differentiators
- **GC fit (score 9/10):** The screen bucks mechanic that is emerging as the category's bestseller variant is Guided Childhood's home turf. A GC Earn Your Screen Time chart: editable, picture based for pre readers, chores and offline wins converting into calibrated screen tokens, DiGi as the referee. Slots as the hero free lead magnet (the single best list builder in this research) with the editable multi kid deluxe version as a paid Etsy listing and starter pack add on.

## Rank 9: Therapy worksheets and CBT/DBT printables for kids and teens (clinician market)

- **Category:** Therapy and mental health printables
- **Why it sells:** Buyers are professionals purchasing repeatedly for sessions: hundreds of cheap singles laddering to bundles and whole shop offers. Made by a licensed therapist credibility markers appear on nearly every top listing.
- **Verified demand:** MentalHealthCenter verified live at 45.8k sales, 4.9 stars, with the claimed 131 DBT listings confirmed and the 220+ worksheet bundle confirmed as two live listings. PlannerMomCompany adjacent signal confirmed at 76,429 sales. Dedicated market pages confirmed (therapy_worksheets, cbt_worksheets_kids, mental_health_whole_shop).
- **Price range:** $3 to $6 singles; bundles observed $10 to $16; large bundles plausibly $10 to $25; whole shop tiers higher
- **Competition:** One dominant incumbent; kid friendly DBT skills and Spanish versions remain less saturated
- **GC fit (score 8/10):** Digital life CBT worksheets for the school counselor and ELSA market: thought records about online comparison, worry ladders for group chat anxiety, urge surfing cards for gaming, all mapped to the Mental Health Checker's language. Slots primarily into the school pack (counselors are repeat professional buyers, mirroring the schools channel GC already serves) with a clinician facing Etsy line that builds professional credibility for the platform.

## Rank 10: Screen time contracts, trackers and digital detox family kits

- **Category:** Screen time and digital wellbeing printables
- **Why it sells:** Rising parental concern with no dominant incumbent: fresh 2025 to 2026 listings, bestseller strips and reviews citing real behavior change, but no 100k sale shop yet. Token mechanics and expert drafted authority positioning are the emerging winning formats; teen phone contracts monetize the first phone handover moment.
- **Verified demand:** Softest sales volume in the top 10 but everything claimed verified: all three listing IDs confirmed real and on category, at least 10 dedicated Etsy market pages indexed (which Etsy only generates for terms with real search volume), a confirmed 2025 to 2026 wave of 7 and 30 day detox kits, and review sentiment matching the behavior change claim. Placed 10th on demand, but it is the whitespace pick: verified niche, no incumbent.
- **Price range:** $1.75 to $4.74 observed for contracts and charts (typical about $3.63); detox kits plausibly $5 to $12 but unverified
- **Competition:** Lowest saturation in the entire set; the clearest whitespace and no dominant shop to displace
- **GC fit (score 10/10):** This is not an adjacent category, it is the brand. The Guided Childhood Family Phone Agreement (expert drafted, calibrated pathways, never a ban), a first phone handover kit for the age 10 to 12 moment, screen bucks tokens, and a 7 day family reset challenge. Slots everywhere: the contract as a lead magnet, the handover kit as the starter pack core, the reset challenge as a newsletter activation, and the whole line as the anchor identity of the Etsy shop. GC can plausibly become the incumbent this niche currently lacks.

## Cross cutting notes

Cross cutting patterns from the verified data. PRICING: almost nothing sells at list price; the universal architecture is a $15 to $28 anchor with a near permanent 50 to 65 percent discount, landing realized prices of $3 to $15, with charm pricing ($4.49, $4.99, $7.99) throughout. Claimed price ranges in source research consistently ran high because they quoted list, not transacted, prices. BUNDLING: every top shop runs a value ladder: cheap singles ($2 to $6) that win search, mid bundles ($10 to $25) that win margin, and whole shop or mega bundles ($25 to $75) that win order value; bundles reliably outearn singles per transaction, and volume framing (204 cards, 600 pages, 10,000 posters) is a purchase driver in itself. EDITABILITY: editable Canva versions outsell static PDFs at 2 to 3x the price across chore charts, routine cards, invitations and contracts; static PDF only products are being commoditised, partly by PLR flooding. SEASONALITY: Q4 is the engine (December planner and Christmas surges, last minute instant download panic buying), with secondary waves in January (new year, dated refreshes, detox), back to school, and May (Mental Health Awareness Month, which is squarely GC territory); dated products convert one asset into an annual subscription in disguise. CREDIBILITY: authority framing (made by a licensed therapist, made by an ADHDer, certified teacher) appears on nearly every top listing in the wellbeing niches; Justin's research voice and expert positioning is a genuine moat GC should print on every listing. WHAT TOP SELLERS DO: catalog depth (100 to 500 listings) plus one signature visual style plus Pinterest traffic, then relentless bundling. STRATEGIC READ FOR GC: the ranking is by demand, but the portfolio logic is to enter through rank 10 (screen time, the verified whitespace with no incumbent, where GC is the natural category owner), make rank 8 (screen bucks chore chart) the free lead magnet, build ranks 4 and 5 (routine cards, calm corner kits) as the paid Etsy and school pack spine, and treat ranks 1 to 3 as later volume plays once the shop has reviews. FOUR CANDIDATES CUT (none resurrected from the dropped list, which was empty): SVG cut files and wedding suites (near zero brand fit, and the wedding niche's specific evidence claims failed live verification), toddler busy books (weakest verified volume at 11.4k shop sales plus PLR commoditisation), and kids educational worksheets (real category, but both headline proof points failed verification: the $1M seller earned primarily on Teachers Pay Teachers, not Etsy, and the 9.8k review listing count could not be confirmed; it also collides with GC's existing curriculum rather than complementing it).
