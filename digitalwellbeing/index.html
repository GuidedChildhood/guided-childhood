Common questions

Frequently asked.

Is this diagnosing my child?

No. The Digital Health Report identifies observed patterns — not clinical diagnoses or labels. When the report flags something like "self-harm language detected in search history," it is surfacing a pattern that warrants attention — not making a clinical judgement. It is designed to help you understand what may be happening earlier, so you can respond calmly and with the right support. If a critical flag appears, we always recommend speaking with a qualified professional.

What makes this different from parental controls?

Parental controls limit access. This helps you understand behaviour. Restricting screen time tells you nothing about what your child is searching for, what content the algorithm is reinforcing, or whether patterns are quietly shifting. This tool gives you insight — not just restriction.

Why do I need to upload their data?

Because patterns show up over time. A single session tells you very little. History reveals what's being watched repeatedly, when habits are occurring, what's replacing other activities, and how things are trending. The data is what makes the insight meaningful.

Which platforms does it support?

YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, and Facebook. All platforms allow you to download a data export directly from their settings. No logins, no ongoing access, and no third-party integrations are required.

Is my child's data private?

Yes. Raw files are deleted immediately after processing. Only essential structured data is used to generate the report. Your child's data is never stored long-term, never shared, and never sold. Privacy is foundational to how this tool is built.

Do I need to be technical to use this?

Not at all. The process takes less than 2 minutes. You answer 6 short questions, upload the data export from the relevant platforms, and receive a clear, parent-friendly report. No technical knowledge required.
