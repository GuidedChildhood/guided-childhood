The Opportunity

13 million children under 16 in the UK. Every one of them will grow up navigating social media, AI tools, group chats, and constant screen pressure — and almost no parent has a structured framework to guide them through it.

Governments are legislating. Schools are overwhelmed. Parents are anxious. The market is not short of restriction tools — screen-time blockers, content filters, monitoring apps. What does not exist is a structured, developmental pathway that teaches children how to navigate technology safely, stage by stage, from first device to digital independence.

The Product

Guided Childhood is a subscription platform built around five developmental stages — Foundation (4–7), Builder (8–10), Explorer (11–13), Navigator (13–15), and Independent (16+).

The platform includes DiGi, an AI advisor personalised to each child's stage; Scripts, word-for-word conversation guides for parents; a Progress Tracker; and a Schools module with full curriculum-aligned lesson packs.

DiGi never bans or restricts. It always provides a calibrated, developmental pathway — because the goal is not to limit children, it is to prepare them.
