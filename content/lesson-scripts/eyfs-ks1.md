# Guided Childhood Lesson Scripts, EYFS and KS1

Three short explainer scripts for our youngest learners, made to be watched on a grown up's lap, together. Each one ends with a Parent Pause, a simple question the grown up asks out loud.

---

**Lesson**: Screens and kindness, real and not real
**Stage**: EYFS, Reception, ages 4 to 5
**Host character**: Sofia, the protector, emblem a shield. Warm and steady. With DiGi Junior, a tiny star.
**Learning outcome**: I can ask a grown up if something on a screen is real.
**Runtime**: about 2 to 3 minutes, 5 beats
**Voice**: Gentle, sing song, unhurried. Lots of warmth. Smile in every line. Leave little pauses for the child to look.

### Beat 1 · Hello from Sofia (0:00 to 0:30)
**Narration.** Hello. I am Sofia. This is my little friend DiGi Junior. Say hello. Today we sit close together, you and your grown up. We are going to look at screens. Little screens and big screens. Are you ready? Let us go.
**On screen.** Sofia holds a soft shield and waves. A tiny yellow star, DiGi Junior, bounces beside her. A child and a grown up sit close on a cosy sofa.
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A kind cartoon guide called Sofia with a soft round shield waving hello, a tiny smiling yellow star beside her, a small child and a grown up cuddled on a cosy sofa. Soft colours, gentle and inviting, 16 by 9.

### Beat 2 · Screens can be lovely (0:30 to 1:00)
**Narration.** Screens can be lovely. We can see a puppy. We can see a song. We can see grandma waving hello. When a screen is kind and fun, that feels warm and good. DiGi Junior loves a happy screen.
**On screen.** A tablet shows a happy puppy, then a waving grandma. DiGi Junior spins with joy. Warm hearts float up.
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A tablet on a child's lap showing a cheerful puppy and a smiling grandma waving, a tiny yellow star spinning happily with little floating hearts. Cosy and bright, gentle colours, 16 by 9.

### Beat 3 · Real, or pretend (1:00 to 1:35)
**Narration.** Now here is a big new idea. Some things on a screen are real. A real puppy you could stroke. And some things are pretend, like a cartoon dragon. A dragon is fun. But a dragon is not real. Sofia will show you.
**On screen.** Split picture. On one side a real fluffy puppy. On the other a friendly purple cartoon dragon. Sofia points to each. DiGi Junior tilts, thinking.
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene split gently in two. On the left a real soft fluffy puppy, on the right a friendly round cartoon dragon. A kind guide called Sofia pointing to each side, a tiny yellow star looking curious. Soft storybook colours, 16 by 9.

### Beat 4 · The magic question (1:35 to 2:10)
**Narration.** Sometimes it is hard to know. Real, or pretend? And that is fine. Here is the magic thing you can do. You ask your grown up. Just say, is this real? Your grown up always knows. Asking is clever. Asking is brave.
**On screen.** The child looks up at the grown up and points at the screen. The grown up smiles and nods. DiGi Junior gives a proud little sparkle. One label word appears, "ask".
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A small child looking up and pointing at a screen, a grown up smiling warmly and nodding, a tiny yellow star sparkling proudly nearby. Tender and reassuring, soft colours, 16 by 9.

### Beat 5 · Parent Pause (2:10 to 2:45)
**Narration.** Now it is your turn, together. Grown up, point at the last thing on the screen and ask your little one this. Do you think that was real, or pretend? Have a lovely chat about it. Well done, both of you. Sofia is proud. See you soon.
**On screen.** The child and grown up talk face to face, foreheads almost touching. Sofia holds up her shield with a warm smile. DiGi Junior waves goodbye. Label words, "Parent Pause".
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A grown up and a small child chatting happily face to face, close and cosy, a kind cartoon guide called Sofia holding a soft shield and a tiny yellow star waving goodbye. Warm golden light, gentle and loving, 16 by 9.

**Lesson**: Kind screens, calm bodies
**Stage**: KS1, Years 1 to 2, ages 5 to 7
**Host character**: Sofia, the protector, emblem a shield. Warm, steady, reassuring.
**Learning outcome**: I can name how I feel after screen time and tell a grown up.
**Runtime**: about 3 minutes, 6 beats
**Voice**: Warm and calm, a little slower. Kind and steady, like a trusted grown up. Gentle energy, never rushed.

### Beat 1 · Sofia says hello (0:00 to 0:25)
**Narration.** Hello again. I am Sofia. I help you feel safe and calm. Today we are going to think about our bodies and our feelings after screen time. Sit close to your grown up. This one is a lovely one to do together.
**On screen.** Sofia waves with her shield. A child and a grown up settle side by side. Soft light, calm room.
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A kind cartoon guide called Sofia with a soft round shield waving, a child and a grown up settling side by side in a calm cosy room. Soft warm colours, gentle, 16 by 9.

### Beat 2 · Screens give us feelings (0:25 to 1:00)
**Narration.** When we watch a screen, it gives us feelings inside. Sometimes we feel happy and giggly. Sometimes we feel excited and buzzy. And sometimes, after a long, long watch, we feel a bit grumpy or tired. All of those feelings are okay. They are just messages from your body.
**On screen.** A child watches a screen. Three little thought clouds pop up, one happy face, one excited zigzag, one tired droopy face. Sofia watches kindly.
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A child watching a tablet with three gentle thought clouds above them, a happy face, an excited zigzag, and a sleepy droopy face. A kind guide called Sofia watching warmly nearby. Soft storybook colours, 16 by 9.

### Beat 3 · Listen to your body (1:00 to 1:40)
**Narration.** Sofia has a trick. When the screen goes off, do a little body check. Are your eyes tired? Is your tummy hungry? Is your body wriggly and needs a run? Your body is always talking to you. We just have to stop and listen.
**On screen.** The child stands up and stretches. Little glowing arrows point to eyes, tummy, wriggly legs. DiGi could peek in, but Sofia leads. Label word, "body check".
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A child standing up and stretching after screen time, gentle glowing arrows pointing to their eyes, tummy and legs, a kind cartoon guide called Sofia smiling beside them. Warm and calm, soft colours, 16 by 9.

### Beat 4 · Naming the feeling (1:40 to 2:15)
**Narration.** Now here is the clever part. You can put your feeling into a word. You might say, I feel happy. Or, I feel a bit tired. Or, I feel wriggly. When you name it, the feeling gets easier. Sofia calls this naming to tame it.
**On screen.** The child speaks, and a warm word bubble appears with a simple feeling face inside. Sofia gives a proud nod. Label word, "name it".
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A child speaking with a soft round word bubble showing a simple friendly feeling face, a kind guide called Sofia nodding proudly. Tender and warm, gentle colours, 16 by 9.

### Beat 5 · Tell a grown up (2:15 to 2:45)
**Narration.** And the very best thing you can do is tell your grown up. Just say how you feel. Your grown up will help you. Maybe you need a cuddle, or some water, or a run in the garden. Telling a grown up is a strong and clever thing to do.
**On screen.** The child turns to the grown up and talks. The grown up leans in warmly. Outside the window, a sunny garden waits.
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A child turning to talk to a grown up who leans in warmly, a sunny green garden visible through a window behind them. Cosy and reassuring, soft warm light, 16 by 9.

### Beat 6 · Parent Pause (2:45 to 3:15)
**Narration.** Time for your turn, together. Grown up, ask your little one this, right now. How does your body feel at this very moment? Give them a quiet second to think, and really listen. Beautiful work, both of you. Sofia is very proud. See you next time.
**On screen.** The child and grown up sit face to face, calm and close. Sofia holds up her shield with a warm smile. Label words, "Parent Pause".
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A grown up and a child sitting face to face, calm and close, sharing a quiet moment, a kind cartoon guide called Sofia holding a soft shield with a warm smile. Golden gentle light, loving, 16 by 9.

**Lesson**: Real, pretend, or made by a computer
**Stage**: KS1, Years 1 to 2, ages 5 to 7
**Host character**: Zara, the curious one who sees through tricks, emblem a magnifying glass. Playful, gently conspiratorial. With DiGi Junior, a tiny star.
**Learning outcome**: I can spot that a picture might not be real.
**Runtime**: about 3 minutes, 6 beats
**Voice**: Playful and bright, a friendly detective sharing a secret. Warm, a little cheeky, lots of wonder. Never spooky.

### Beat 1 · Zara the picture detective (0:00 to 0:25)
**Narration.** Psst. Come closer. I am Zara. This is my magnifying glass, and this is my little friend DiGi Junior. We are picture detectives. Today we learn a secret. Some pictures are not what they seem. Ready to investigate? Let us go.
**On screen.** Zara peeks round the corner of the frame holding a big magnifying glass. DiGi Junior, a tiny star, bounces beside her. A child and grown up lean in, curious.
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A playful cartoon guide called Zara peeking round the frame holding a big magnifying glass, a tiny smiling yellow star beside her, a child and a grown up leaning in curiously. Bright, gently mysterious, soft colours, 16 by 9.

### Beat 2 · Three kinds of picture (0:25 to 1:05)
**Narration.** Here is the secret. Pictures come in three kinds. Some are real, like a photo of your cat. Some are pretend, like a drawing of a unicorn. And some are made by a computer, and those can trick your eyes. A computer can make a cat with wings that looks almost real.
**On screen.** Three framed pictures in a row. A real cat photo, a hand drawn unicorn, and a shiny computer made cat with wings. Zara points at each with her magnifying glass.
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. Three picture frames in a row, a real photo of a cat, a hand drawn unicorn, and a slightly shiny computer made winged cat, a playful guide called Zara pointing at each with a magnifying glass. Soft storybook colours, 16 by 9.

### Beat 3 · The computer can pretend (1:05 to 1:40)
**Narration.** A computer is very clever. It can make a picture of something that never happened. A dog driving a bus. A cake as big as a house. It can look so real. But it is not real. It is just made up, by a machine.
**On screen.** A silly computer made picture of a dog driving a bus. DiGi Junior gasps with big eyes. Zara raises one eyebrow.
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A funny picture of a dog driving a red bus shown on a screen, a tiny yellow star gasping with big surprised eyes, a playful guide called Zara raising one eyebrow. Bright and silly, soft colours, 16 by 9.

### Beat 4 · Detective clues (1:40 to 2:15)
**Narration.** So how do we spot a trick? We look for funny clues. Too many fingers. Wonky wobbly bits. Something that could never really happen. When you see a funny clue, your detective brain goes, hmm, that might not be real.
**On screen.** Zara's magnifying glass zooms in on a hand with too many fingers, then a wobbly wonky wheel. A little glowing "hmm" bubble appears. Label word, "clues".
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A magnifying glass zooming in on a cartoon hand with too many fingers and a wobbly wheel, a small glowing thought bubble with a curious face, a playful guide called Zara investigating. Soft, gently playful colours, 16 by 9.

### Beat 5 · Ask a grown up (2:15 to 2:45)
**Narration.** And here is the best detective trick of all. When you are not sure, ask your grown up. Say, is this real, or did a computer make it? Your grown up is the head detective. Asking together is how detectives always win.
**On screen.** The child points at a picture and looks up at the grown up. The grown up nods with a smile. Zara gives a thumbs up. DiGi Junior sparkles.
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A child pointing at a picture and looking up at a grown up who nods and smiles, a playful guide called Zara giving a thumbs up and a tiny yellow star sparkling. Warm and reassuring, soft colours, 16 by 9.

### Beat 6 · Parent Pause (2:45 to 3:15)
**Narration.** Detectives, it is your turn now, together. Grown up, find a picture nearby and ask your little one this. Do you think a computer could have made this? Talk about the clues you can see. Brilliant detective work, both of you. Zara is proud. Case closed. See you next time.
**On screen.** The child and grown up look at a picture together, heads close, pointing and chatting. Zara tips an imaginary detective hat. DiGi Junior waves. Label words, "Parent Pause".
**Illustration prompt.** A warm hand drawn childrens book scene. A grown up and a child looking closely at a picture together, heads close, pointing and chatting, a playful guide called Zara tipping an imaginary detective hat and a tiny yellow star waving goodbye. Warm golden light, joyful, 16 by 9.
