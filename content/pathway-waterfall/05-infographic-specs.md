# INFOGRAPHIC SPECS

*Cornerstone visuals for the waterfall, built in the style of the reference image: hand drawn boxes and arrows on a warm off white ground, a simple wrong story up top, the fuller evidence picture below, sources in small mono type at the foot. These get saved and reshared on every platform. Briefs are written so a designer or Canva can build them, and so they can be handed to an image tool.*
*June 2026. Justin. guidedchildhood.com*

---

## STYLE GUIDE FOR ALL FIVE

Match the reference image exactly in feel.

- Ground: warm cream, the colour of paper, not white.
- Drawing: hand drawn rounded rectangles, thin ink outline, light sketch shadow. Hand lettered headings, looser than a font.
- Two zones, top and bottom, split by a dashed horizontal rule. Top is labelled the common story. Bottom is labelled the evidence.
- Arrows are hand drawn. Feedback loops curve back with a dashed return arrow and a small label like and repeat.
- One small icon per node, line drawn, no fill, in the brand green. A brain, a phone, a heart, a moon.
- Sources sit at the bottom in small IBM Plex Mono, two or three citations, exactly like the reference.
- Brand footer: small, Guided Childhood, guidedchildhood.com. No logos shouting.
- No dashes in any label. Spell ranges with the word to. Age 4 to 16.
- Colours from the README token set: cream ground, green ink for accents, coral for the warning node, gold for the resolution node.

Keep all five visually identical in system so they read as one set. Consistency over novelty.

## INFOGRAPHIC 4 · Where the harm actually lives

*Reframes time into sleep and type of use. High save value.*

Heading (hand lettered): It is not how much. It is when and what.

The common story (top zone):
A dial drawn like a clock, labelled screen time, with a worried face. Caption: count the hours.

The evidence (bottom zone):
Two boxes side by side.
Left box, coral edge, labelled when: a small moon icon, phone in a dark bedroom, 11pm. Caption: late night use wrecks sleep, the strongest lever on mood.
Right box, labelled what: two icons, a speech bubble for active use, an infinite scroll spiral for passive use. Caption: texting a friend is active and mostly fine. Scrolling on autopilot is passive, and that is where mood drops live.
A gold resolution box below both: charger in the hallway. Autoplay off. Two changes, tonight.

Footer line: Once you account for sleep, the link to poor mental health almost disappears.

Sources (mono, small): Swedish longitudinal study, n=4,810 · Odgers and Jensen, 2020.
