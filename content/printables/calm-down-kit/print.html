Ten minutes before the first big feeling

What a calm corner is, and what it is not

A calm corner is a cosy spot you choose together, in a calm moment, before it is ever needed. A beanbag by the bookshelf. A cushion under the stairs. The carpet corner of your classroom. What makes it work is not the spot. It is the deal: this is where feelings come to get smaller, and your child chooses to go there. It is never a punishment spot. The moment a child is sent there in disgrace, it becomes the naughty step in a nicer jumper, and it stops working.

The golden rule

The corner is where feelings get smaller. It is not where children get sent.

Introduce it in a calm moment

Set it up on an ordinary afternoon when everyone is fine. Let your child choose the cushion and place the cards. Practise the star breathing together once, for fun, like a game. Then, when a big feeling arrives, the corner is already a friend. You are not teaching anything new in the storm. You are pointing at something familiar.

In the moment, keep it tiny

Get low, slow your voice, and offer, never order. Try "shall we visit your corner?" If the answer is no, that is allowed. Sit nearby and breathe slowly yourself. Children borrow calm from the adult in the room long before they can make their own. The script card on page eight gives you the exact words.

The ten minute setup, with things you already own

1. Pick the spot together. Low traffic, a bit tucked away, never behind a closed door.
2. Add one cushion, one soft toy, and a book or two your child already loves.
3. Put the calm corner sign up at your child's eye height, not yours.
4. Cut out the feelings cards and the calm choice cards, and keep this kit in a folder in the corner.
5. Stick the star breathing poster where a small finger can reach the star.

Teachers: this works as a whole class corner too. One corner, one chair, the same cards. Laminate everything and it will survive the year. This kit helps children practise calming down and builds a calm corner habit. It is a parenting and teaching tool, not a treatment for anything.

What to say when the feeling is big

In the storm, children cannot take in much. Say less than you think you need to, slower than feels natural, and let the corner do the rest.

In the moment

You are safe.
I am here.
Let's make the feeling smaller together.

Then offer, never order: "Shall we visit your corner? Do you want the star or the cards?" Get low. Slow your own breathing. If they say no, stay close and wait. Your calm is the loudest thing in the room.

Save for later

That was a big feeling.
You got through it.
What helped?

Never talk about the behaviour until everyone is calm, including you. Teaching lands after the storm, not during it. Later, keep it short and warm, then let it go. The corner stays a safe place, whatever happened before it.

Why this works

A child mid storm is all feeling and no listening. Your first job is not to fix or to teach. It is to be the safe, steady grown up while the feeling passes through. The words on these cards do exactly that job and nothing else. Everything else can wait ten minutes, and it will go better because it waited.
