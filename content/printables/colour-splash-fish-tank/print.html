Two minutes before you play

How to play, and a note for grown ups

BEEP BOOP! Hello little one. I am DiGi. Print the fish and pop them out. Then find the bowl that is the same colour as each fish. Red fish to the red bowl, blue fish to the blue bowl. Say the colour out loud with me as you go. Ready? Let us splash!
Setting it up

Print the pages and cut out the fish along the dashed lines. Big scissors for grown up hands. Laminate the fish and the bowls if you want them to last, then play again and again. Nothing else to buy.

How to play

Spread the six bowls out. Give your child one fish at a time. Name the colour together, then find the bowl that matches. When every fish is home, the tank is full and the colours are sorted.

A note for grown ups

Colour is one of the very first sorting skills. Before children can count or read, they group the world by what looks the same. Matching a red fish to a red bowl is real early maths, quietly at work. Play alongside your child rather than testing them. Name each colour out loud, go slow, and let them place the fish themselves. If a fish lands in the wrong bowl, that is part of learning. Just say the colour again warmly and try together. Celebrate the match, not the speed.

For the colour in edition

DiGi's colouring tips

BEEP BOOP! In this edition the fish and the bowls are ready for you to colour. Take your time and make each one bright. Here is how I like to do mine.
Colours to try

Colour a red fish for the red bowl, a blue fish for the blue bowl, then green, yellow, orange and pink. Match each fish to a bowl the same colour. That is the whole game, and you make the colours yourself.

Press lightly first

Start soft. Lay down a gentle layer of colour, then go over it again to make it richer. Light to strong always looks better than pressing hard, and it never tears the paper.

Big shapes first

Colour the big round bowl first, then the fish, then the little pebbles and bubbles. Big to small keeps your page tidy and your hand relaxed.

Stay in the lines, gently

Follow the bold black outlines and slow down at the edges. If you wander over a line, no worry at all. Your page, your rules.

One more thing

Colour a fish and its bowl in the very same colour so they are easy to match. When every fish has a home, your tank is full and bright. Brilliant work, little one.
