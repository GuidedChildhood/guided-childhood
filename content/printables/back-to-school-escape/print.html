How to play, and the grown up guide

Detective, listen up. This is your mission. The classroom is locked with a five digit padlock. Each puzzle you solve hands you one digit. Solve all five, write the code, and you crack it open. Work in order, trust the clues, and no peeking at the answer key. Ready? Case open.
Setting it up

Print the pages and go. You can laminate them and use a dry wipe pen so the mission plays again and again. There is nothing to hide and nothing to cut out. Just work through the puzzles in order, one to five.

How the code works

Every puzzle gives one number, the digit in the gold box at the bottom of the page. Write each digit in order onto the padlock on the finale page. Five digits, and the classroom door swings open.

Grown up tips

Read it together. For younger detectives, read each puzzle aloud and let them do the counting and the thinking. For older ones, hand it over and time them. Praise the effort, not just the answer. If they get stuck, point back to Zara's clue rather than giving the digit away. The joy is in the crack, not the copy.

For the colour in edition

Zara's colouring tips

A good detective notices the little things, and colouring is the same. Take your time, look closely, and make each page yours. Here is how I do mine.
Colours from my world

My colour is gold. Try warm gold and honey for the coins, the padlock and my magnifying glass. School reds and blues look brilliant on the pencils, the PE kit and the water bottle. Green is lovely for the glue sticks.

Press lightly first

Start soft. Press lightly and lay down a gentle layer of colour, then go over it again to build it up richer. Light to strong always looks better than pressing hard straight away, and it never tears the paper.

Big shapes first

Colour the big shapes first, like the school bag or the awning, then move to the small details like the pencil tips and the coins. Big to small keeps your page tidy and your hand relaxed.

Stay inside the lines, gently

Follow the bold black outlines and slow down at the edges. If you wander over a line, no worry at all. Real detectives make mistakes and carry on. Your page, your rules.

One more thing

Colour the puzzles in as you solve them, or solve first and colour after. Either way works. When every page is bright and the code is cracked, you have a mission you made your own. Brilliant work, detective.
