Ten minutes before the shop opens

Set up the shop, then let the till do the teaching

Setup · About ten minutes

1. Print everything. Coins, cards and the shopkeeper kit come out best on card. A laminate on the menu and the coins keeps the shop open all summer.
2. Cut out the coins, the cones, scoops and toppings, and the twelve order cards.
3. Build the counter: menu propped against a cereal box, coins sorted into the till, teddies in the queue with a small pile of coins each.
4. Badge on, sign flipped to OPEN, and you join the queue.

Game 1 · Year 1 maths

Pay the price

The customer orders one thing and pays with exactly the coins on the price. A single scoop is 20p, so a 20p coin crosses the counter. Say every coin's name out loud as it lands. That is the whole game, and it is Year 1 coin recognition doing its job.

Game 2 · Year 2 maths

Make it another way

Now the till has no 20p coins left! Every order is paid with two or three coins that make the amount. A 45p double scoop becomes 20p, 20p and 5p. One amount, many ways: that is the Year 2 skill of combining coins.

Game 3 · Year 2 stretch

The change challenge

Customers get fancy and pay with 50p or £1. The shopkeeper counts the change back out loud, counting up from the price. A 20p scoop paid with 50p: "twenty... thirty, forty, fifty. Your change is 30p!"

The golden tip

Let your child be the shopkeeper, not the customer. The one behind the till does the reaching, sorting, counting and handing back, and the handling is where the learning lives. Your job is to be the fussy customer who orders wrong, pays with the wrong coin and needs correcting. Being put right by a six year old is the whole point.

My rule: the shopkeeper touches every coin. If a grown up starts counting for you, they go to the back of the queue.

Curriculum note for the grown ups: Game 1 practises Year 1 maths, recognising coins and their values. Games 2 and 3 practise Year 2 maths, making amounts from combinations of coins and giving change.

Year 1 maths · Recognise coins

Play money: coppers and small silvers

Cut round the dashed circles. These play coins print close to the size of the real thing, so the shop works just as well with real pocket change when you have it. Coppers print golden, silvers print blue, pounds print deep gold, which keeps sorting easy for small hands.

Shopkeeper short of change? Print this page twice. Card stock and a laminate make coins that survive the whole summer holiday, and a real coin taped beside its play twin makes a lovely matching game for day one.

Year 2 maths · Make amounts and give change

Play money: big silvers and pounds

These are the change makers for Game 3. A 50p or a £1 coin pays for almost everything on the menu, and counting the change back out loud is the Year 2 skill teachers most want practised at home. The £2 coin exists for one glorious order: the family tub.

A note on our coins. These are stylised play coins in Pebble's shop colours, made for little fingers and coin value practice. They are not pictures of real money, which is exactly why every one says PLAY MONEY on its face.

Year 1, Year 2 and change challenges

Order and challenge cards

Shuffle the cards face down. The customer draws one, reads it in their silliest voice, and the shopkeeper does the maths. Answers hide at the bottom of each card: fold them under, or trust the person behind the till.

Year 1

"One single scoop, please!"

Which one coin pays for it?

Answer: the 20p coin
Year 1

"A flake for my scoop, please!"

It costs 15p. Pay with two coins.

Answer: 10p + 5p
Year 1

"A single scoop with sprinkles!"

What does it cost altogether?

Answer: 20p + 5p = 25p
Year 1

"A single scoop with strawberry sauce, please!"

What does it cost altogether?

Answer: 20p + 10p = 30p
Year 2

"A double scoop, please!"

Pay 45p with exactly three coins.

Answer: 20p + 20p + 5p
Year 2

"One ice lolly, please!"

Answer: 50p + 5p
Year 2

"A triple scoop, please!"

Answer: 45p + 15p = 60p, paid 50p + 10p
