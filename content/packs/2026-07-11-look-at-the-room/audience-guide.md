## Verification notes (READ BEFORE POSTING)

### Solid, use freely
- UK ACEs: ~47% of adults report at least one, ~1 in 10 report four or more (Bellis 2014, BMC Medicine; Public Health Wales). Graded risk (Hughes 2017, Lancet Public Health).
- One always-available trusted adult more than halves later mental-illness risk in high-ACE UK adults (Bellis 2017, BMC Psychiatry; PHW ACE and Resilience survey).
- One stable caring adult as the core resilience finding; tolerable vs toxic stress buffered by a responsive adult (Harvard Center on the Developing Child; Shonkoff).
- Caregiver mental health among the strongest, most consistent predictors of child mental health (Goodman 2011 meta-analysis, effects small to moderate, d around 0.4 to 0.5).
- Treat the mother's depression, the child improves (STAR*D-Child; Weissman 2006 JAMA; Pilowsky/Wickramaratne 2011). This is the strongest causal, hopeful finding in the pack.
- Co-regulation, the adult as the child's first regulator (Rosanbalm and Murray, ACF/UNC; Kopp).
- Still-face, rupture and repair beats perfection (Tronick et al. 1978).
- Perinatal mental illness cost, ~72% falls on the child (Bauer, Knapp et al., LSE).
- Technoference, parental device use reduces responsive interaction (technoference scoping review).
- Intergenerational transmission as a breakable pattern (Kaufman and Zigler: ~30% continuity, so ~70% discontinuity).

### State honestly, do not overclaim
- "Caregiver mental health is the strongest predictor": say ONE OF the strongest, not cleanly "by far". Do not rank it above poverty, maltreatment or genetics, which are entangled with it.
- ACE scores predict populations, not individuals. Never a diagnosis, a label, or a child's destiny (Baldwin/Anda 2021; Anda 2020). This is why Post 1 insists on "patterns, not a score".
- Effects are modest and probabilistic. Most children of a struggling parent are fine.

### CUT ENTIRELY — do not put these in any post (they would sink credibility)
- Epigenetic or biological inheritance of trauma "passed down the bloodline". Human evidence weak and disputed (Horsthemke 2018; critiques of Yehuda). The Substack names this only to say we are NOT relying on it.
- Polyvagal theory mechanisms, "ventral/dorsal vagal states", "dysregulated nervous system" as established science. Heavily critiqued (Grossman et al. 2026). Use plain "co-regulation" and "settle/steady" language instead.
- "One wrong sentence scars a child for life." Single-event determinism, not supported. Post 4 actively corrects it.
- "A whole generation of traumatised parents" as a population fact. Fine as a gentle framing ("many of us parent with our own childhood unresolved"), never as a statistic.

### Could not fully verify
- A few precise sub-figures (e.g. exact cell values in the Bellis 2017 trusted-adult study) were corroborated at the headline level ("more than halved") across Public Health Wales sources but not re-read from the primary PDF. Safe as stated; do not attach a false decimal precision.
