## Reddit · r/ScienceBasedParenting (evidence first, links inline, neutral tone)

**Title:** "Social Media Dependency Disorder" is not a recognised diagnosis. Here is what the evidence actually supports.

A framework listing five concepts for kids and social media has been circulating. One is presented as a clinical diagnosis, "Social Media Dependency Disorder." It is not in any diagnostic manual, so I pulled the actual literature on each underlying idea. Sharing in case it is useful.

**On diagnosis.** The only screen or internet use behavioural addiction recognised in the WHO ICD-11 is Gaming Disorder (2019, code 6C51). Social media addiction is in neither ICD-11 nor DSM-5-TR (DSM lists Internet Gaming Disorder only as a condition for further study).

**On prevalence and measurement.** The accepted construct is "problematic use." WHO HBSC 2024 put it at roughly 11% of adolescents, up from 7% in 2018 (girls 13%, boys 9%). The validated Social Media Disorder Scale (van den Eijnden et al., 2016) uses nine DSM based items, threshold five of nine, all loss of control and impairment criteria, not hours of use. Billieux et al. (2015) is the standard caution that confirmatory scale building can overpathologise ordinary behaviour.

**On reduction.** A 2023 RCT (Thai et al., Psychology of Popular Media, n=220, young people with emotional distress) found capping use at roughly one hour a day for three weeks improved appearance and weight esteem versus unrestricted controls. RCPCH (2019) declines to set a universal screen time threshold and recommends negotiated, displacement aware limits.

**The honest counterweight.** Reduction is not universally protective. Mattick et al. (Lancet Public Health, 2018, n=1,927, six year cohort) found parental supply of alcohol increased later harm, i.e. for still deferrable behaviours, delay outperforms a harm reduction approach.

**On response, if an image is shared.** NCA guidance: do not pay, stop contact, do not delete (evidence), screenshot, tell a trusted adult, report; the child is the victim. Report Remove (IWF and Childline) hashes and blocks images for under 18s.

Happy to add DOIs in the comments. Main takeaway: the ideas are mostly sound, the branding was not, and precision matters because mislabelling ordinary use as addiction has a cost.

## Facebook · parent group (warm, shorter, one idea, ends on a question)

Quick one that made me feel better this week.

That scary phrase going round, social media dependency disorder, is not actually a real diagnosis. It is not in any medical manual. The only recognised screen related condition is gaming disorder.

There is a real thing doctors look at, they call it problematic use, about 1 in 9 teens. But here is the bit that matters. They measure it by whether a child can stop when they mean to, whether it is causing rows, whether it is eating into sleep and friendships. Not by hours on the phone.

So the better question is not how long were they on it. It is can they put it down, and is everything else in their life still happening.

Kinder question. Usually a more reassuring answer too.

How do you tell the difference in your house, hours or something else?
