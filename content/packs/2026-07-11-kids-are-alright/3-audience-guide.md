## Corrections made to the original drafts (READ THIS)

**The Kids Are Alright:**
- DROPPED "each generation tests a little smarter." The Flynn effect stalled and reversed across the developed world after the mid 1990s (Bratsberg and Rogeberg, PNAS 2018). Keeping it would have handed critics an easy kill.
- CORRECTED "a little more empathetic." Empathy fell ~40% among US students 1979 to 2009 (Konrath 2011), then recovered from ~2009 (Konrath 2025). Stated as a fall and a recovery, never a smooth rise.
- ANCHORED "most educated generation" to real numbers (record HE participation, record low dropout) rather than the slogan.
- KEPT and stated honestly the mental health exception, with the UK nuance that youth suicide here is not clearly rising and the self harm admissions fall is partly a recording change.

**The Pac-Man panic:**
- CORRECTED the arcade story to the real UK episode: George Foulkes MP, "Control of Space Invaders" Bill, 20 May 1981, lost 114 to 94. Dropped the US "quarters/burglaries" framing (UK arcades used 10p coins).
- CORRECTED Koop: he did say it, 9 Nov 1982, but retracted within days as personal opinion with no science. Dropped "rewire attention spans" (a modern gloss; his concern was violence).
- STRENGTHENED the comics point with the fact that Wertham's evidence was later shown fabricated (Tilley 2013).
- Odgers: written as "every generation has its Pac-Man panic" (her framing). The "family flashes a Pac-Man photo" anecdote is UNVERIFIED and must NOT be presented as a quote.

## Verification notes — the numbers

**UK (lead with these, your audience):**
- Under-18 conception rate down 66%, 42.0 (2007) to 14.2 (2022) per 1,000 (ONS). Small 2022 uptick from the pandemic trough, still below pre-pandemic; keep that caveat visible.
- First-time entrants to youth justice down ~93%, ~110,800 (2006/07) to ~8,100 (2024/25) (Youth Justice Board / MoJ). Quote exact figures from the YJB bulletin table at publication.
- Teen smoking: 11% of pupils have ever smoked in 2023, lowest ever, vs 22% current smokers in 1996 (NHS England, Smoking Drinking and Drug Use). Drinking (37% ever) and drugs (13% ever, down from 18% in 2021) also down.
- HE participation risen for every 15-year-old cohort since 2001/02 (DfE).

**US (corroboration):**
- Youth violent victimisation down 85%, 184.8 to 27.4 per 1,000 (12-17), 1993 to 2022 (BJS/NCVS). Cleanest stat in the set.
- Teen birth rate down ~79% from its 1991 peak (61.8 to 12.7 per 1,000), 2024 record low (CDC/NCHS).
- 2024 record abstinence: 67% of 12th graders and 80% of 10th graders used no alcohol, cannabis or nicotine in the past month (Monitoring the Future). Caveat: nicotine pouches rose; do not say every substance is down.
- Dropout rate 5.3% record low; 40% of 25-29 hold a degree (NCES).

**The exception:**
- UK CYP probable mental disorder: ~1 in 9 (2017) to ~1 in 5 (2023); 17-19s roughly doubled to ~1 in 4 (2017-2022) (NHS England).
- US: teen girls persistent sadness 57% (2021), 53% (2023); youth suicide (10-24) up 62% 2007-2021 (CDC).
- UK nuance to keep: youth suicide not clearly rising here; self harm admission fall partly a recording change.

**The labelling point:** prevalence inflation hypothesis (Foulkes and Andrews 2023) plus nocebo/iatrogenic-intervention evidence. Defensible as "may", never as an established law, and never used to dismiss real suffering.

**Sourcing caveat:** ONS, NHS Digital, GOV.UK, BJS and NIDA pages block automated fetch, so exact latest-table values (the ~8,100 FTEs, the exact 2023 smoking figure, the 184.8/27.4 victimisation figures) were cross-checked across official summaries but should be copied from the primary bulletin table before final publication.
