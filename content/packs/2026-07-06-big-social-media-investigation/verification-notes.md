## 1. UC Davis feed experiment, CONFIRMED (with two precision rules)
- Citation: Hilbert, M., Cingel, D. P., Zhang, J., Vigil, S. L., Shawcroft, J., Xue, H., Thakur, A., and Shafiq, Z. (2025). #BigTech@Minors: social media algorithms have actionable knowledge about child users and at risk teens. Telematics and Informatics, 103, 102341. UC Davis.
- Use the word "almost seven times", not "about" or "seven times". Source phrase: "almost seven times more child directed content".
- RULE: the almost sevenfold figure is CHILD DIRECTED content (age targeting), NOT harmful content. Keep separate everywhere. The posts and newsletter do this explicitly.
- Confirmed: within a single session; three platforms (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok); 396 sock puppet accounts; the phrase "actionable knowledge about child users and at risk teens"; conclusion that personalisation is "not for their protection".
- DO NOT claim preregistered or open data. Unverified.
- Primary: sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0736585325001030 ; ssrn abstract 4674573.

## 2. School phone ban study, CONFIRMED (with attribution and sample caveats)
- Citation: Allcott, Baron, Dee, Duckworth, Gentzkow, Jacob (2026). The Effects of School Phone Bans: National Evidence from Lockable Pouches. NBER Working Paper No. 35132. NOT yet peer reviewed.
- Attribute as six authors across Stanford, Duke, Michigan and Penn, not "a Stanford study". Public face is Thomas Dee (Stanford GSE).
- "40,000+ schools" is the comparison universe (~40,542). The treated pouch adopting schools that drive the estimate are ~4,600. The posts say "more than forty thousand schools compared", which is accurate; do not imply 40,000 schools banned phones.
- Confirmed: 61% to 13% in class phone use over three years (teacher reported); test score effect "close to zero" on average (small high school maths positive, slight middle school negative); no measurable improvement in perceived online bullying; wellbeing dipped year one, above baseline by year three.
- Design: staggered difference in differences, quasi experimental, Yondr pouch schools only, self report heavy. Present as strong evidence, not proof. The posts and newsletter state the working paper and natural experiment caveats openly.
- SHAKY on direct fetch: primary PDF not opened here. Eyeball nber.org/papers/w35132 or tom-dee.github.io/files/w35132.pdf before posting.

## 3. Ofcom, May 2026, CONFIRMED figures, one CORRECTION
- Two documents, both 21 May 2026: "Children's Online Experiences" research report (the survey figures) and the Project Mercury "call for action" enforcement update (the platform commitments). Do not conflate with the separate "Children and Parents: Media Use and Attitudes" report.
- Confirmed: 73% of 11 to 17 year olds saw harmful content in a four week period (survey, recall based); 35%, "just over a third", via scrolling their personalised feed; Ofcom phrase "content feeds are children's primary pathway to harm"; Snap, Meta, Roblox committed (mainly anti grooming and contact safety); TikTok and YouTube did not commit to feed/recommender changes; Ofcom "deeply concerned by the overall response from industry to our challenge to fix the problem".
- CORRECTION applied in copy: Ofcom is EXPLORING new inspection powers (skilled persons reports, remote viewing notices), NOT wielding a live "real time" algorithm inspection power. The posts say "exploring". Strengthened Protection of Children Codes / additional safety measures statement due Autumn 2026: confirmed.
- SHAKY on direct fetch: ofcom.org.uk was blocked here. Eyeball the two 21 May 2026 Ofcom URLs before posting.
