# Post 5 — Founder video script (60 seconds, face to camera)

**Series:** In Proportion. **Slot:** Week 2, Sunday. **Speaker:** Justin, direct to camera.
**Why video, not carousel:** the strongest line here can be misheard as blaming parents. A face and a warm voice carry the nuance that text flattens. Say the blame line looking straight down the lens.

**Delivery:** warm, slow, unhurried. This is not a rant. It is one parent telling another something true and slightly uncomfortable. Full stops are pauses. No dashes in the captions. UK English.

**Length:** about 165 words, roughly 60 seconds at an unhurried pace. Trim the money section first if you run long.

---

## The script

**[0:00 to 0:06] Hook. Straight to camera, still.**
The strongest predictor of your child's mental health is not their phone. It is not their school.

**[0:06 to 0:12] Small pause. Let it land.**
It is the mental health of the adult raising them. And almost nobody is legislating for that.

**[0:12 to 0:26] Softer. This is the line that matters. Look right down the lens.**
That comes from Candice Odgers, one of the calmest researchers in this field. And I want to hold it gently, because it sounds like blame and it is the opposite of blame. A parent under financial strain, or grief, or their own anxiety with a two year wait for help, is not failing their child. They are carrying something heavy with almost no support.

**[0:26 to 0:33] Beat. Then a small shift in tone, wry.**
Now look at where the money goes.

**[0:33 to 0:44] A touch faster, this is the fact that stings.**
Six platforms made close to eleven billion dollars in one year from advertising to children. Your child is not the customer. Your child is the product on the shelf.

**[0:44 to 0:55] Slow back down. Land the proportion.**
None of this means phones do nothing. But if we spend the whole decade fighting the thing in a child's hand, we will miss the things doing the real damage.

**[0:55 to 1:00] Warm, direct. The soft close.**
Look after the adult in the house too. Then build the real world back up, one brick at a time. Follow along for the rest.

## Pinned first comment (post with the video)
The proportionality point is not that social media does not matter. It is that the size of the response should match the size of the evidence. Right now the most visible cause gets almost all the attention and the strongest predictors get a fraction. Sources: Odgers, Nature 2024, on caregiver mental health, and the Harvard study in PLOS ONE, 2023, on the eleven billion.

## Production notes
- Shoot in a real home setting, not an office. Kitchen or living room. Warm light.
- One continuous take if possible. The honesty reads better without cuts.
- No music under the blame section. Let it be quiet.
- Keep the eleven billion caption on screen a beat longer than feels comfortable.
- Do not use the potatoes or 0.4 percent line here. That one needs its guardrail and does not belong in a short video.
