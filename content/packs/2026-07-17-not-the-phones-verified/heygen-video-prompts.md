- Path B, produced version: create_video_agent (chat mode) with the full prompt below, which adds motion graphic overlays for the data beats. Use this for the punchier social cut.

## Global settings (all six)
- Aspect: 9 by 16 portrait as primary for Reels, TikTok and Shorts. Duplicate at 16 by 9 for LinkedIn native.
- Duration: about 60 seconds, Auto to honour script length. No per scene timestamps.
- Avatar: the selected presenter (Justin). Delivery: warm, calm, unhurried, a parent who did the homework, never a guru.
- Voice: Justin's cloned voice, or a warm UK male preset at a slightly slow rate.
- Captions: burn in for sound off. UK English. No dashes anywhere in on screen text.

## Shared STYLE BLOCK (paste at the end of every prompt)
In Proportion brand. Warm, calm, human, evidence led, the opposite of panic. Colours: cream #F7F3EE background, deep green #2E7D5A and soft green #AFDCA2 for affirmation, coral #D4600A for one point of emphasis per scene, gold #F2C94C for highlights, near black #1C1C1C for text. Never blue, never purple, never Inter. Fonts: Nunito in heavy weights for all display and headlines, IBM Plex Mono for small eyebrows and source credits. Motion is restrained: numbers COUNT UP, cards SLIDE in gently staggered 0.3s, source lines type on in mono, soft dissolves between scenes, no screen shake, no hype. Lower thirds sit on a cream bar with a thin green rule. Intro and outro cards show the IN PROPORTION wordmark in Nunito with a mono eyebrow above.

## Shared MUSIC
Warm, sparse, unhurried. Soft piano or acoustic bed, low in the mix, no drop, no build to hype. Drops to near silent under the most sensitive line in each script.

## VIDEO 1 — The clock is not the enemy

FORMAT: 60 second evidence explainer, calm and confident, one idea.
TONE: authoritative but warm. Reference: a trusted GP explaining a result, not a TED stage.
AVATAR: the selected presenter, conversational, direct to camera.

SCENE 1 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre frame, still, direct to camera.]
VO: "The strongest thing I can tell you about screens this year is this. A study followed four thousand two hundred children for four years, and how long they spent on a phone told you almost nothing about their risk. How they used it told you almost everything."
Lower third types on: "4,285 children. 4 years." Mono source line under it: "Xiao et al, JAMA 2025".

SCENE 2 A-ROLL plus OVERLAY
[Presenter left 35 percent. Content right 65 percent.]
VO: "Total screen time was not linked to suicidal behaviour. But a rising, compulsive pattern of use roughly doubled the risk."
RIGHT: two cards SLIDE in staggered. Card one, cream: "Total screen time: not linked." Card two, coral rule: "Addictive pattern: risk x2." "Risk ratio 2.14" COUNTS UP beneath card two.

SCENE 3 FULL SCREEN B-ROLL
[NO AVATAR, motion graphic only, hold 10 seconds.]
VO: "Think about two children. Same three hours, same app. One is making things and messaging friends and is fine. The other is in a spiral and is in real danger. Same number on the report."
Two soft green panels SLIDE in side by side, each with a simple child icon and "3 hours". A quiet coral underline DRAWS under the second panel only.

SCENE 4 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre, warmer.]
VO: "So the thing we all obsess over, the clock, is the wrong measure. The clock is not the enemy. The loop is. A rule watches the clock. A parent watches the child."
Headline SLIDES up and holds: "The clock is not the enemy. The loop is."

CRITICAL ON SCREEN TEXT (display literally):
- "4,285 children. 4 years."
- "Total screen time: not linked."
- "Addictive pattern: risk x2."
- "Risk ratio 2.14"
- "The clock is not the enemy. The loop is."
- "Xiao et al, JAMA 2025"

## VIDEO 2 — The fire was lit in 1971

FORMAT: 60 second reframe, calm, a long view.
TONE: measured, a touch of wonder at the numbers.
AVATAR: the selected presenter, reflective.

SCENE 1 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre, direct.]
VO: "Here is a pair of numbers that quietly dismantles the story everyone is telling. In 1971, about eighty percent of seven and eight year olds in England walked to school on their own. By 1990, it was under ten percent. The iPhone did not exist until 2007."
Three mono stamps type on in sequence, held together: "1971: 80%", "1990: under 10%", "iPhone: 2007".

SCENE 2 FULL SCREEN B-ROLL
[NO AVATAR, hold 10 seconds.]
VO: "So whatever we did to childhood, we started long before a screen could be the cause. We took the walk to school, the unsupervised afternoon, the freedom to get a bit lost, and we removed almost all of it."
A single line DRAWS left to right across a cream field, sloping gently down, with small year markers. A soft green dot travels the line.

SCENE 3 A-ROLL plus OVERLAY
[Presenter left 35 percent.]
VO: "This does not prove phones do nothing. A generation that already lost its freedom got handed a device built to fill every empty minute. The phone did not empty the streets. It moved into streets that were already empty."
RIGHT: headline SLIDES in: "The phone is not the fire. The fire was lit in 1971."

SCENE 4 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre, warm close.]
VO: "So you do not win this by taking the phone away and leaving the empty room. You win it by making the room worth being in again. One freedom handed back. One hour that belongs to them."

CRITICAL ON SCREEN TEXT (display literally):
- "1971: 80%"
- "1990: under 10%"
- "iPhone: 2007"
- "The phone is not the fire. The fire was lit in 1971."
- "Hillman, One False Move, 1990"

## VIDEO 3 — What happens the day after the ban

FORMAT: 60 second commentary, practical, forward looking. Never argues for or against the ban.
TONE: steady, prepared, a colleague you trust.
AVATAR: the selected presenter, straight talking.

SCENE 1 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre.]
VO: "Australia removed four point seven million children's accounts under its under sixteen ban. Then researchers followed the children, and more than eighty five percent were still using the apps."
Two figures COUNT UP in a lower third: "4.7 million accounts removed" then "85% still using".

SCENE 2 A-ROLL plus OVERLAY
[Presenter left 35 percent.]
VO: "I am not here to argue about the ban. It is coming here in Spring 2027, that is settled. The useful question is the one nobody is asking. What happens the day after."
RIGHT: a calendar card SLIDES in, "Spring 2027" in gold. Below it, mono: "The question: what happens next."

SCENE 3 FULL SCREEN B-ROLL
[NO AVATAR, hold 10 seconds.]
VO: "We have banned scary media before. The comics code in the fifties. The game ratings in the nineties. Both calmed the adults and changed the shelf. Neither reached the child."
A simple timeline DRAWS across cream: three markers SLIDE in, "1950s comics", "1990s games", "2027 social media", each with a small shelf icon.

SCENE 4 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre, warm.]
VO: "A ban a child walks around in an afternoon is not a plan. It is a headline. The plan is the boring part. Teaching a child, from primary school up, how these systems actually work, so that by sixteen they step through the gate ready."

CRITICAL ON SCREEN TEXT (display literally):
- "4.7 million accounts removed"
- "85% still using"
- "Spring 2027"
- "eSafety Commissioner; University of Newcastle, 2026"

## VIDEO 4 — The man who deleted his own best statistic

FORMAT: 60 second trust piece about intellectual honesty.
TONE: admiring, plain, a little wry.
AVATAR: the selected presenter, candid.

SCENE 1 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre.]
VO: "A psychologist built part of his new book on a striking statistic. A journalist checked it. He asked his publisher to delete it. That is the most encouraging thing I read all week."

SCENE 2 A-ROLL plus OVERLAY
[Presenter left 35 percent.]
VO: "His figure was that school stress in teenagers had leapt to eighty three percent by 2013. Then a journalist checked it. The eighty three percent was real, from a 2013 survey of thirteen to seventeen year olds. But the earlier number he set against it came from a different survey, that asked a different question, of a wider age group that included eight year olds."
RIGHT: card one SLIDES in, "83% (2013, ages 13 to 17)". Card two SLIDES under it in coral, "Earlier figure: different question, different ages". A soft strike line DRAWS through card two.

SCENE 3 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre, warmer.]
VO: "And to his credit, when this was put to him, Gray asked his publisher to pull the figure. That is the rule worth keeping. You report the finding that hurts your case as loudly as the one that flatters it."
Headline types on: "Say the number that hurts your case too."

SCENE 4 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre, quiet close.]
VO: "None of this means the harm is imaginary. It means the confident numbers have to survive checking, in both directions."

CRITICAL ON SCREEN TEXT (display literally):
- "83% (2013, ages 13 to 17)"
- "Earlier figure: different question, different ages"
- "Say the number that hurts your case too."
- "APA Stress in America; The Atlantic, 2026"

## VIDEO 5 — The strongest predictor nobody legislates for (founder video)

FORMAT: 60 second founder piece, emotional, first person. This is the single most important one to shoot as a real face to camera take.
TONE: warm, slow, unhurried. One parent telling another something true and a little uncomfortable.
AVATAR: the selected presenter (Justin), looking straight down the lens, especially on the blame line.

SCENE 1 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre, still, no overlay, let the face carry it.]
VO: "The strongest predictor of your child's mental health is not their phone. It is not their school. It is the mental health of the adult raising them. And almost nobody is legislating for that."
Mono source line, small and low: "Candice Odgers, Nature 2024".

SCENE 2 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre, softer, no music under this.]
VO: "I want to hold it gently, because it sounds like blame and it is the opposite of blame. A parent under financial strain, or grief, or their own anxiety with a two year wait for help, is not failing their child. They are carrying something heavy with almost no support."
No overlay. Caption only. Keep the frame on the face.

SCENE 3 A-ROLL plus OVERLAY
[Presenter left 35 percent, a small tonal shift, wry.]
VO: "Now look at where the money goes. Six platforms made close to eleven billion dollars in one year from advertising to children. Your child is not the customer. Your child is the product on the shelf."
RIGHT: "11 billion dollars from children in one year" COUNTS UP in coral. Under it, holds: "Your child is the product."

SCENE 4 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre, warm close.]
VO: "None of this means phones do nothing. But if we spend the whole decade fighting the thing in a child's hand, we will miss the things doing the real damage. So look after the adult in the house too. Then build the real world back up, one brick at a time."

CRITICAL ON SCREEN TEXT (display literally):
- "The strongest predictor is not the phone."
- "11 billion dollars from children in one year"
- "Your child is the product."
- "Odgers, Nature 2024; PLOS ONE, 2023"

## VIDEO 6 — It is not the phones (the short manifesto, pinned or ad)

FORMAT: 60 second manifesto, the whole argument compressed. Good as a pinned profile video or a paid ad. Carries the one CTA.
TONE: calm conviction, landing somewhere hopeful.
AVATAR: the selected presenter, warm and certain.

SCENE 1 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre.]
VO: "Two famous psychologists, two bestselling books, one crisis, and they cannot agree on the villain. One says it is the phones. The other says it is school. After all of it, the honest scientific verdict is still mixed and inconclusive."
Lower third types on: "Verdict: mixed and inconclusive". Mono under it: "National Academies, 2024".

SCENE 2 A-ROLL plus OVERLAY
[Presenter left 35 percent.]
VO: "So here is where I have landed, as a parent who read the studies. It is not the phones. And it is not only the phones. The crisis is a seventy year loss of real childhood, a genuine school pressure problem, and a family and money story underneath both. The phone is an accelerant on a fire that was already lit."
RIGHT: three soft green bricks STACK up, labelled "Lost childhood", "School pressure", "Family and money". A coral spark FADES in on top, labelled "The phone".

SCENE 3 FULL SCREEN B-ROLL
[NO AVATAR, hold 10 seconds.]
VO: "Which means neither a blanket ban nor a free for all fits what we know. A ban treats every child as the one in trouble. A free for all pretends the ones in trouble are not real."
Two cards SLIDE in and are gently crossed out in turn: "Blanket ban" and "Free for all". A soft green path DRAWS between them.

SCENE 4 A-ROLL
[Presenter centre, warm close.]
VO: "The thing that fits the evidence is quieter. Watch the pattern, not the clock. Support the whole family, not just the device. Build a pathway through this, one brick at a time, so that by sixteen a young person walks into the digital world ready. If you want a place to start, we made a free one. It is in the link."
Outro card: IN PROPORTION wordmark, then "guidedchildhood.com/starter-pack" in gold on cream.

CRITICAL ON SCREEN TEXT (display literally):

- "Verdict: mixed and inconclusive"
