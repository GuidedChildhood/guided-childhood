## The symmetry (for the "cuts both ways" move)

- Genetic fallacy / circumstantial ad hominem, informally the "shill gambit": rejecting a finding purely by its funder. Barnes et al. 2018, PNAS: COI allegations damage a claim's credibility as much as fraud allegations, though a COI says nothing about whether the data are right.
- Haidt: The Anxious Generation, #1 NYT, 2m+ copies, 100+ weeks on the list. Speaker fees roughly $20k to $200k depending on bureau and event. Let Grow, After Babel, the Anxious Generation Movement. FLAG: a personal royalty donation pledge could NOT be verified. Do not assert it.
- Yondr (pouches): US state school sales ~$174k (2021) to ~$2.13m (2023); ~$5m US government contracts through Q3 2024; ~2.5m students. FLAG: the ~$25 to $30 per student figure is widely reported but not primary confirmed; the contract totals are solid.
- What actually insulates the findings: preregistration, open data (Millennium Cohort Study, Monitoring the Future, Understanding Society, YRBS), specification curve analysis, independent replication (Twenge reanalyses the same public data and disagrees, which is the system working).
- Direction check: an edtech funder (Jacobs) is not served by "screen effects are small"; Przybylski publicly fought Facebook's data lockdown ("a disaster for academic researchers"). Neither fits capture.

## The reconciliation (the bridge to Denise, and the heart of the story)

- Orben and Przybylski, specification curve across ~355,000 adolescents: digital tech use explains at most about 0.4% of the variance in wellbeing, an association near eating potatoes and smaller than wearing glasses.
- Both true at once: the population average is small AND clinicians see severe harm daily. An average flattens a large fine majority and a smaller badly harmed minority. A referral caseload is the harmed tail by construction. Odgers: offline vulnerabilities mirror and amplify online risks. Orben: windows of developmental sensitivity (roughly girls 11 to 13, boys 14 to 15, both near 19).
- Dismissing the researchers as "bought" and dismissing the clinician as "just anecdote" are the same mistake in opposite directions: attacking the source instead of integrating the evidence.

## Standing note
This is a live scientific debate, not a settled result; the small effects position is contested (Haidt, Rausch, Twenge). The point of the reply and the story is not "the debate is over." It is "you cannot win it by funding source; you win it by method, and the front line clinician and the population researcher are describing the same reality from different ends."
