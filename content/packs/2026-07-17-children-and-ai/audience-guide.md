# How to use this pack — Children and AI (In Proportion series)

Built from the verified V2 briefing, 17 July 2026. Every claim traces to a confirmed source. Two harm buckets are kept deliberately separate and must never be blended: the severe, well counted harm (AI child abuse imagery) and the diffuse, weaker harms (brain rot, companion addiction). It is the activity, not the device. Connection over control. No developmental outcome claims for children anywhere in the pack.

## The one line
The AI abuse imagery harm is a real emergency and earns the alarm. Almost everything else is a set of diffuse worries that shrink under scrutiny, and the same tool helps or hurts depending on what the child is doing with it. Target the activity and the child, not the device.

## Sequencing note
The newsletter is the source of truth and lands the full thesis in week one. The seven LinkedIn posts each carry one brick of it, so spread them across the week rather than firing them together, and let the series run into a second week. Only one plain thesis landing goes out per week: the newsletter carries it in week one, Post 7 carries the LinkedIn version in week two. Keep that landing to roughly one in ten across the wider calendar so the thesis stays a slow reveal, not a headline. Post 3 (the IWF numbers) never sits next to a CTA or a light hearted post. The severe bucket travels alone.

## The one CTA rule
One direct ask only, to guidedchildhood.com/starter-pack, and it appears in exactly two places: the schools LinkedIn post (Post 6) and the newsletter close. Nowhere else. No CTA anywhere near the abuse imagery material.

## Guardrails baked in
- No dashes anywhere.
- Two harm buckets kept clean. Severe and unsoftened on AI child abuse imagery. Calm and sceptical on brain rot and companion addiction. Never blend the registers.
- It is the activity, not the device. The Harvard and ALEKS split is the proof: same tool, opposite outcomes.
- Connection over control. Children reject bans, so the pack models talking to them, not policing them.
- Never claim developmental outcomes for children. The learning studies describe retention and test performance, not development.
- Haidt appears only as a foil, never as supporting authority.
- Every stat is from the verified findings list. If challenged, cite the primary source by name.
- Handle with care items are quarantined. The MIT "Your Brain on ChatGPT" preprint (n=54 adults) is a caveat only. The Character.AI death is a single case, never a population effect. The National Academies 2024 verdict was mixed and inconclusive and is quoted as such. The loneliness study is adults and association only, never causal and never about children.
