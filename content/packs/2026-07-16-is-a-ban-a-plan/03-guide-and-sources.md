# Is a Ban a Plan? — evidence pack and guide

Every figure in the series and newsletter, with a named source, a date, and the exact
wording that keeps it honest. This doubles as the PATHWAY reply: when someone comments
PATHWAY, send them this. All claims fact checked 16 July 2026. Where a source warned us
off a number, the safe wording is what made it into the copy.

## The verified figures (safe to publish as written)

**4.7 million under 16 accounts.** eSafety Commissioner media release, "Platforms restrict
access to 4.7 million under 16 accounts across Australia," first half of December 2025,
after the law took effect 10 December 2025. Wording matters: eSafety says access was
*restricted or removed*, and calls the numbers *preliminary*. The series says "restricted
access," never "deactivated." Do not upgrade the verb.

**More than 85% still using.** Barnes et al., BMJ, 24 June 2026. Observational study of 408
Australian adolescents aged 12 to 16, before and three months after the ban. More than 85%
of under 16s still accessed at least one restricted platform. Solid, peer reviewed, safe.

**Three quarters found bypass easy.** University of Chicago, Becker Friedman Institute
Working Paper 2026-57, also NBER working paper w35162, "Why Bans Fail: Tipping Points and
Australia's Social Media Ban," April 2026 (authors include Cass Sunstein). About 75% of
surveyed teens said circumventing the ban was easy or very easy. Reported workarounds:
lying about age 57%, false birthdate 44%, parent or sibling account 42%, VPN 30%. TWO
CAUTIONS baked into the copy: (1) sample size is reported inconsistently across secondary
write ups (746 vs 835), so the series says "surveying hundreds of teenagers," never a
precise N. Confirm the exact N against the PDF before ever printing it. (2) It is a working
paper, not yet peer reviewed. The copy leans on the peer reviewed BMJ figure for the main
claim and uses this one for colour.

**438 experts, 32 countries.** Joint statement of scientists and researchers on age
assurance and verification, 438 signatories from 32 countries, dated February 2026, widely
reported March and April 2026. Safe as written.

**96% of fifteen year olds, over a third above three hours.** EU Joint Research Centre
report "Social media usage and adolescents' mental health in the EU" (JRC141047). In 2022,
on a typical weekday, 96% of 15 year olds engaged with social media, 37% for over three
hours a day. CAUTION baked in: the sample is four EU countries (Ireland, Hungary, Slovenia,
Spain), about 40,000 adolescents, not the whole EU. The copy says "a large four country
study across the EU." Never say "all of the EU."

**No fines yet.** Consistent with reporting as of mid 2026. CAUTION baked in: no source
states a hard "zero fines" figure, and in June 2026 the Australian government moved to
roughly double the maximum penalty, so enforcement is pending, not concluded. The series
does not print "zero fines." It stays on the safe ground of the deadline framing instead.

**eSafety on early effects.** eSafety's early check in reported no discernible reduction in
cyberbullying or image based abuse complaints from under 16s three months in. The series
does not lean on this, but it is available if asked.

## Numbers we deliberately did NOT use

**"300% VPN downloads" and "488% alternative app downloads."** From the source report,
OVERSTATED as stated. The real picture: VPN demand spiked to a multi year high, one provider
saw installs jump sharply in 24 hours, and Sensor Tower put demand around 103% above the
prior 28 day average. The 488% was specifically one app's *usage*, not downloads across the
board. So the copy says only "VPN demand spiked to a multi year high, and downloads of
smaller apps climbed steeply." True, sourced, and unshakeable. Do not reintroduce the
precise percentages.

**Kids Helpline "~100 contacts" and a rise in sextortion reports.** UNVERIFIED against any
primary yourtown or eSafety source. Cut entirely. Not in the series, not in the newsletter.
Do not add it back without a direct citation.

## The framing, in one line

A ban is a deadline, not a plan. The series never argues the line is wrong. It argues that
a line alone does not reach the child, so we build the thing that does. Hold the guardrail
(see 00-framing-guardrail.md) on every repost, comment reply and cutdown.

## How to run the series

- Post in order, Episode 1 through 7, one per day or every other day. Each teases the next,
  so gaps are fine but the order is not optional.
- Episode 1 is the one to boost and repost. It carries the whole hook.
- Episode 3 is the emotional centre and the one that states the hidden thread plainly. It
  is the "1 in 10" post. Expect it to travel furthest. Reply to comments there personally.
- PATHWAY comment mechanic on every episode. The reply is this document, trimmed to plain
  text, no dashes.
- Repost and follow ask on every episode. Link only on Episode 7, and only to
  guidedchildhood.com/starter-pack.
- The newsletter (02) is the reservoir. Point the series at it for anyone who wants it all
  at once. It can go out as one Substack issue or one LinkedIn newsletter edition.

## Cutdowns and waterfall (optional, if you want the full spread)

Each episode compresses to a short vertical for Reels, TikTok and Shorts: read the first
three lines to camera, hold on the teaser line, series number burned on screen. Facebook
takes the same film with a warmer caption ending on the episode's closing question. The
seven closing questions are already written as the last line of each post, so they double
as the engagement prompt on every platform. No dashes in any caption. Captions burned from
your real voiceover, never from AI text.

## The one thing to confirm before you print an exact N

If you ever want to quote the working paper's sample size in a caption, open the BFI or NBER
PDF and read the number off the paper itself. Until then, "hundreds of teenagers" is the
honest, unbreakable wording.
