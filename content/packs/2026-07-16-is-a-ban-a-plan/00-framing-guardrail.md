# Is a Ban a Plan? — framing guardrail (internal, read before posting)

This is the rule the whole series is built on. It is not for publishing. It keeps
Justin on the right side of the one line he must never cross.

## The one thing this series is NOT

It is not an argument against the ban. Justin does not relitigate the ban, for or
against, ever. The ban is settled background. The lines are drawn. Australia went
first on 10 December 2025. The EU and the UK are on the same road. Arguing about
whether the line should exist is a fight for other people. It is not ours, and
picking it up would make us the platform that is soft on safety, which we are not.

## The frame we DO own

A ban is a deadline, not a plan.

A line on a calendar tells you when. It does not tell you how, or what a child is
ready for when they cross it, or what happens to the child who climbs around it the
night before. The line is the easy part. The plan is everything the line cannot do
by itself, and that is the part we build.

Every episode lands one version of the same beat: the line alone does not reach the
child. So build the thing that does. Preparation. Capability. A pathway from age four,
not a wall at sixteen.

## How to hold it in every post

- Never say the ban is wrong, pointless, a failure, or should be repealed.
- Do say the ban is a deadline that only works if something is built behind it.
- When the evidence shows circumvention (85% still using, bypass found easy), that is
  not "the ban failed." It is "the line on its own does not reach the child, so what
  reaches the child."
- The villain is never the ban and never the parent. The villain is the belief that a
  date is a plan.
- The hidden thread still runs underneath. The real drivers of children's distress are
  poverty, adverse experiences and the mental health of the adults around them. The
  screen is real but smaller than the noise. Roughly one post in this series states
  that plainly (Episode 3). The rest each carry one brick.

## The proportionality standard (Sonia Livingstone, not us shouting)

Moral panic is the vehicle, never the destination. We never wave away a real harm.
We check the proportion, then point at what actually helps. Let the evidence do the
work. Never lean on the label.

## The tone

Justin's voice. Warm, plain, direct. No dashes anywhere. No AI-isms. He is not scoring
points against lawmakers. He is the calm builder in a loud room, saying the quiet true
thing: the date is coming either way, so let us make sure our children arrive ready.
