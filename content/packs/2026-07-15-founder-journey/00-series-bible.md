# THE LAST STRETCH
## A founder journey series, warts and all. Building Guided Childhood in the open.

Working title: **The Last Stretch.** Alternates: *Built in the Open* · *Ready by 16* · *The Wrong Villain (the build)*.

---

## What this is

A 10 episode story series, told to camera by Justin, in the weeks before Guided Childhood launches. Not a polished ad. A real founder, mid fight, letting people watch the last stretch. The struggles, the near disasters, the reason it exists, the family behind it. People do not follow products. They follow people they have watched try.

The promise to the viewer: **you are watching someone build the thing they wish had existed when their own child needed it, and you are watching it in real time, before anyone knows if it works.**

## Why it works now

You are on the last stretch before launch. That is the one window where the story has a clock on it. Every episode ends on a cliffhanger that pulls to the next, and the whole series pulls toward one date: launch, and the founder 50. By the time episode 10 lands, the people who watched from episode 1 are not an audience. They are the first fifty.

## The spine (never stated bluntly, always underneath)

The hidden thread is the emotional engine, carried the way you carry it everywhere else: mostly implied, stated plainly maybe twice across the ten.

> The real crisis in children's mental health is poverty, adverse childhood experiences and the mental health of the adults raising them. Social media gets the attention because it is visible and it is rich. The things doing the most damage do not make a headline.

The series is the human version of that argument. A dad who went looking for why his child changed, found the real answer was bigger and quieter than the screen, and built the calm, staged, evidence led response instead of joining the panic. Education, not eradication. Ready is built from age 4.

## Tone

Warm, plain, direct. Justin's voice. No dashes in any caption or on screen text, ever. No AI gloss, no hype words. Warts and all means the failures are on screen, not hidden: the night the whole site went down, the week nearly lost to a duplicate build, the hero rewritten five times in a day. Vulnerability is the product here. The calm evidence literacy is the differentiator, but the humanity is the hook.

## Format and production (keep it cheap and real)

- **Shot on your phone, to camera.** Kitchen, desk, on a walk, in the car parked outside school. Real beats fake. Do not over light it.
- **60 to 120 seconds each** for the vertical cut (Reels, Shorts, TikTok, LinkedIn video). A longer 3 to 5 minute cut can live on YouTube and Substack.
- **Open on the cliffhanger resolution or the hook in the first 2 seconds.** No slow intros. The first line is the thumbnail.
- **Burn captions from your actual voiceover, never from an AI text layer** (the algorithium lesson: AI misspells on screen text, so caption from what you really said).
- **One idea per episode.** One feeling. One cliffhanger.
- **End card, same every time:** the series name, the episode number, and "Founder 50" once the countdown starts. Consistent so people feel the series building.

## The cast (real)

- **Justin** — the founder. Warm, plain, a dad who did the homework, never a guru.
- **The family** — the reason. The daughter beat from your own roadmap ("the day I noticed my daughter had changed") is the origin. You tell it, I will not invent it.
- **DiGi** — the character you built, the calibrated pathway made friendly. The proof that your answer is guidance, not a wall.

- **The build itself** — the chaos. The parallel sessions, the migrations, the night it all went down. The struggle that makes it a story and not a brochure.

## How to use this pack

- `01-episodes.md` — the ten episodes in full: cold open line, the beats, the real material to use, the struggle, the emotional truth, the "your truth here" prompt where the personal story goes, and the cliffhanger into the next.
- `02-interview-sheet.md` — the questions only you can answer. Record yourself talking these through once, unedited, and half the series writes itself in your real words.

Rule for the whole series: **every documented beat is real and sourced from the build. Every personal beat is yours to tell. I have left the personal beats as prompts, never invented them.** That is what makes it warts and all instead of a story about someone else.
