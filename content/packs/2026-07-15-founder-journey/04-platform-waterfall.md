# The platform waterfall — one story, every channel

The video is the same across channels (your illustrated read aloud, or straight to camera). What changes is the caption, the framing and the ask. Same film, dressed for each room.

## The channels, in one line each
- **Instagram Reels / TikTok / YouTube Shorts:** the vertical illustrated film. Hook in 2 seconds, cliffhanger at the end, series numbered on screen. Retention is everything.
- **Facebook:** warmer, longer caption, written like you are talking to other parents. Ends on a question. Shares beat likes here.
- **LinkedIn:** the same film, caption framed as the founder build in public. Slightly more about the why and the evidence. Repost and follow ask.
- **YouTube (long):** stitch two or three episodes into a 5 to 8 minute chapter. This is where the binge lives.
- **Substack:** the written version of each episode, your two existing titles fit here (It Was Never The Hours, The Villain Was Already In The Room). The newsletter is the reservoir the shorts point back to.

## No dashes in any caption. One link, only in episode 10 and the bio: guidedchildhood.com/starter-pack.

### EP 2 — It was never the hours
**Instagram:** A paper in 2012 predicted social media would be the next thing we blame our kids' problems on. That was fourteen years before the ban. Two years of research later, here is the quiet part almost nobody says out loud. Comment KEEP for the series.
Hashtags: #ItWasNeverTheHours #ScreenTime #ChildrensMentalHealth #Evidence #DigitalParenting

## Production once, publish everywhere
1. Record the illustrated read aloud (script file 03), or shoot yourself to camera and drop the drawings over the top.
2. Export one vertical master per episode.
3. Reels, TikTok, Shorts: post the master, use the Instagram caption.
4. Facebook: same master, use the Facebook caption, pin the link in the first comment not the post.
5. LinkedIn: same master, founder in public framing, repost and follow ask.
6. Substack: the written version, one per episode, as the reservoir.
7. YouTube: stitch every two or three into a chapter for the binge.

Everything points, gently and only at the end, to guidedchildhood.com/starter-pack and the founder fifty.
