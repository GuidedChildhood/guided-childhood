## PLATFORM 2: FACEBOOK GROUPS (avoiding your friends)

This is your highest-volume route to new UK parents right now.

**Which groups to join:**
- UK Parenting Support (large national groups)
- Mumsnet Facebook group
- "Parents of Year 7/8/9" groups (the ban age group)
- Local town parent groups (search your city/county + parents)
- SEND parent groups
- Secondary school transition groups ("Starting secondary school September 2026")
- Digital parenting or screen time groups if they exist

**How to post (this is important):**
Do not post as Guided Childhood. Post as Justin, a parent of three who has been in the research.

Lead with the useful content. The sleep finding, the conversation guide, the alcohol argument. Give the value first. One sentence at the end maximum: "I've been building a resource on this if anyone wants it — guidedchildhood.com."

Join the conversations that are already happening about the ban. Find threads where parents are asking "what do I do now?" and give a genuinely useful answer. This drives more traffic than a standalone post.

**Post 3 times per week across different groups.** Do not post the same thing in multiple groups on the same day. Stagger by 2 to 3 days.

**What to post:**
- Week 1: Sleep finding (standalone, no CTA)
- Week 2: The conversation by age (soft CTA at end)
- Week 3: The alcohol argument (positions you as authoritative, drives profile visits)

## PLATFORM 3: INSTAGRAM

**Account type:** Post from a personal or creator account, not the company page. Same reasoning as Facebook — people engage with people.

**Format that works:**
Carousels outperform single images for saves and shares. Reels outperform carousels for reach to new accounts. Run both.

**Carousels — 3 per week during the series:**

Carousel 1: Sleep (already written — 6 slides)
Carousel 2: The conversation by age (already written)
Carousel 3: 20 issues (already written)
Carousel 4: The alcohol argument (new — 5 slides)
  Slide 1: Someone will say: "We don't remove alcohol laws just because teenagers drink."
  Slide 2: The problem: alcohol laws work at a supply chain with adult gatekeepers. Social media bans work at a login checkbox.
  Slide 3: 60% of Australian children were on Discord and Telegram within 8 weeks.
  Slide 4: When minors circumvent alcohol laws they get alcohol. When they circumvent the social media ban they get unmoderated internet with no safeguarding.
  Slide 5: The displacement makes the risk profile worse. That is not how the alcohol analogy works.

Carousel 5: What platforms need to change (5 slides, each feature with one line explanation)

**Reels — 1 per week:**
Short, 45 to 60 seconds, talking to camera. One idea only. No script reading — speak naturally to a single insight. Good starting topics:
- "The one thing the research says actually works" (sleep)
- "The question I wish someone had asked me as a parent"
- "What the Cambridge professors actually said about the ban"

**Hashtags:** #digitalparenting #ukparents #onlinesafety #screentime #digitalwellbeing #parenting #ukfamilies

**CTA:** "Link in bio" pointing to guidedchildhood.com. Change the bio link to the founding member page during the active series.

## PLATFORM 7: PR

The van der Linden and Orben quotes are genuinely newsworthy. Two Cambridge professors on camera saying the government ignored the evidence is a story.

**Who to pitch:**
- The Times (parenting section)
- The Guardian (education desk)
- The Telegraph (parenting)
- Mumsnet editorial (they have editorial staff who write about exactly this)
- The i newspaper
- Schools Week (for the schools angle)
- TES (Times Educational Supplement) — the TRUST framework for schools is a strong angle here

**The pitch (one paragraph):**
"Two Cambridge professors, both of whom advised the government, said on camera this week that the evidence base for the under-16 social media ban 'just isn't there.' I've spent two years in the peer-reviewed literature on this and have built a platform that translates what the research actually recommends into something parents can use tonight. Happy to talk to a journalist about what the science says and what parents can do right now."

**Keep it short. Offer a quote and a data point.** The 99.666% statistic and the r=0.36 vs r=0.05 comparison are the ones most likely to get picked up.
