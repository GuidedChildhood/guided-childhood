Sample Lesson · Module 9 · KS3
Year 7–9 · PSHE / Computing · 50 minutes

Social Media and the Brain

Learning objective: Understand why social media affects mood and self-image, and identify one personal strategy in response.

0–5 min

Starter

Quick poll, hands up or anonymous slips: "Have you ever felt worse after scrolling, without knowing why?" Do not collect answers aloud. The point is private recognition.

5–20 min

Main 1: The variable reward

Teach the slot machine mechanic: pull to refresh, unpredictable reward, dopamine loop. Pupils map one app they use against the mechanic. Key teaching point: the design is intentional and it is not a personal weakness to find it hard to stop.

20–35 min

Main 2: The comparison machine

Cambridge research (Dr Amy Orben) identifies ages 11 to 13 as the peak sensitivity window, especially for girls, with a later window for boys around 14 to 15. Activity: pupils analyse an anonymised feed and list what the curated version leaves out. They write one sentence: "What this feed does not show is ..."

35–45 min

Plenary

Each pupil chooses one strategy: bedroom charging, a follow audit, turning off one notification type. Exit ticket: "One thing I now know about how my apps are designed."

Home

Parent take-home note

A one-page plain-language note goes home: what was taught, the one question to ask at dinner, and the bedroom rule explained. Every lesson in the scheme connects school to home this way.

Safeguarding note for the DSL

This lesson can surface disclosures about mood and online experience. Teacher notes include the escalation pathway and the no-blame disclosure script.

Assembly Outline · 15 minutes · KS2 or KS3

"The App Is Not Your Friend. It Is Not Your Enemy Either."

1.Open with the one-hour experiment: search one thing, return an hour later, the recommendations changed. Ask: who changed them?
2.The reveal: the algorithm has one job, keeping you watching. That is different from showing you what is good for you.
3.The reframe: pupils who understand the machine are not passive users. They are the ones in control.
4.Close with one action for everyone, staff included: tonight, charge the phone outside the bedroom.
