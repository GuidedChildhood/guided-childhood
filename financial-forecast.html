Item Value Notes
Founder rate £7.99 / month Capped at 50 subscribers. Locked for life.
Standard monthly £12.99 / month Main retail price point.
Annual plan £99 / year = £8.25/month equivalent. Saves £57. Two months free.
School — Small £299 / year Up to 300 pupils. All teachers included.
School — Medium £499 / year 300–800 pupils.
School — Large / MAT £999+ / year 800+ pupils or multi-site. Custom pricing.
Blended parent ARPU ~£11 / month Weighted average across founder, standard, annual mix.
Monthly churn 4–5% Conservative vs. 3–7% industry average for parenting subscriptions.
School renewal rate 85% Annual contract. Renewal driven by parent and governor demand.
Founder salary £35,000 / year Deliberately below market (market: £60–90k). Founder's return is in equity.
COGS ~£0.10 / user / month Anthropic API at scale + Supabase + Vercel hosting.
Two scenarios presented throughout: Optimistic case (1,000 parents / 20 schools by end of Year 1) and Base case (300 parents / 10 schools). Both are presented side by side in the Summary Table on page 4.

07 Notes for Investors
Item Detail
Founder salary £35,000 per year. A senior SaaS founder typically earns £60,000–£90,000. This deliberate below-market rate ensures maximum capital is deployed toward growth. The founder's upside is in equity.
Not modelled — upside only NHS/LA partnerships · International revenue (Years 1–2) · White-label licensing · Viral growth events · MAT framework agreements
Policy catalyst The UK social media ban going live in Spring 2027 is modelled as a demand spike in Q3–Q4 Year 1. Even if delayed, the base case shows profitability in Year 2 without it.
Use of investment Marketing and paid acquisition (£90k) · Schools partnerships (£65k) · Product development (£55k) · Operations and founder salary (£40k). This is not a fundraise to cover losses — it is a fundraise to build the growth machine.
Exit potential At £1M+ ARR and established school relationships, the business is attractive to educational publishers, school platform providers, and parenting media groups. Series A or strategic acquisition in Years 4–5.
